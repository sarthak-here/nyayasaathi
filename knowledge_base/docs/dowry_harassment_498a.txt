DOWRY HARASSMENT AND CRUELTY — BNS SECTION 85, DOWRY PROHIBITION ACT
======================================================================

DOWRY PROHIBITION ACT 1961

WHAT IS DOWRY?
Dowry means any property or valuable security given or agreed to be given:
- By one party to a marriage to the other party.
- By parents of either party.
- By any other person to either party or their parents.
- In connection with the marriage.
Gifts not demanded and given voluntarily (stridhan) are NOT dowry.

GIVING OR TAKING DOWRY IS ILLEGAL:
Giving dowry: Imprisonment up to 5 years + Rs. 15,000 fine or value of dowry (whichever higher).
Taking dowry: Same punishment.
Demanding dowry: Up to 2 years + Rs. 10,000 fine.
Important exception: The person giving dowry under COMPULSION by the other side is not penalized if they report to police within 1 year.

Where to file: Police station for all dowry offences.

BNS SECTION 85 (Old IPC 498A) — CRUELTY BY HUSBAND OR RELATIVES

This is the primary criminal provision for dowry harassment and marital cruelty.
"Cruelty" includes:
1. Willful conduct likely to drive woman to suicide or cause grave injury/danger to life, limb, or health (physical or mental).
2. Harassment for dowry — coercing woman or her relatives to meet any unlawful demand for property or valuable security.

Punishment: Up to 3 years imprisonment + fine.
Cognizable, Non-bailable.
Who can be charged: Husband AND his relatives (mother-in-law, sister-in-law, father-in-law, etc.).

EVIDENCE IN 498A CASES
Key evidence to preserve:
- Records of dowry demands (WhatsApp messages, letters, emails).
- Medical records of injuries.
- Photographs of injuries (date-stamped).
- Witnesses (neighbours, relatives who saw or heard abuse).
- Records of money/valuables transferred as dowry.
- Diary entries or voice recordings of threats.

IMPORTANT NOTE ON 498A:
Supreme Court in Arnesh Kumar v. State of Bihar (2014) directed police to not make automatic arrests in 498A cases — magistrate approval required before arrest in most circumstances. This was to prevent misuse. However, if there is direct evidence of violence, arrest can still be made.

DOWRY DEATH — BNS SECTION 80 (Old IPC 304B)

If a woman dies within 7 years of marriage under suspicious circumstances, and there was dowry harassment before death:
Punishment: 7 years to life imprisonment.
Cognizable, Non-bailable. Tried by Sessions Court.
PRESUMPTION (Section 80 read with BSA): If it is shown that woman was subjected to dowry harassment soon before death, court shall presume dowry death. Burden shifts to accused.

Post-mortem is mandatory for all unnatural deaths within 7 years of marriage.
File complaint at police station — police must investigate.

ABETMENT TO SUICIDE — BNS SECTION 109

If dowry harassment drives a woman to suicide:
Anyone who abets/instigates the suicide is punishable under BNS Section 109.
Up to 10 years imprisonment + fine.
Cognizable, Non-bailable.

STRIDHAN — WOMAN'S ABSOLUTE PROPERTY

All gifts received by a woman before, during, and after marriage — from family, friends, husband, in-laws:
- Gold, jewellery, cash, property — this is her stridhan.
- Husband/in-laws cannot use or take it without her consent.
- If taken by force: Criminal breach of trust (BNS 316) + DV Act economic abuse.
- If not returned after separation/divorce: File FIR for breach of trust + civil suit.
The woman can sue for return of stridhan at any time — no limitation period issue in matrimonial property matters.

COMBINATION OF REMEDIES — FILE ALL SIMULTANEOUSLY:
1. BNS Section 85/80 complaint at police station (criminal).
2. Dowry Prohibition Act complaint at police station.
3. PWDVA (Domestic Violence Act) application before Magistrate for protection order, residence order, and monetary relief (civil).
4. Maintenance application under BNSS Section 144 before JMFC.
5. Divorce petition in Family Court (if marriage breakdown).

All these proceedings can run simultaneously in parallel.

==========================================================================
IF YOU ARE THE ACCUSED — DEFENSE RIGHTS IN DOWRY / BNS 85 CASES
==========================================================================

YOUR CONSTITUTIONAL RIGHTS:
- Right to remain silent. Do NOT give any statement to police without a lawyer.
- Supreme Court in Arnesh Kumar v. State of Bihar (2014): Police CANNOT automatically arrest in BNS 85 / 498A cases. They must first satisfy themselves that arrest is necessary and obtain magistrate approval. If arrested without this, apply for bail immediately citing Arnesh Kumar guidelines.
- Right to anticipatory bail: Apply proactively to Sessions Court if you suspect a false case will be filed.

WHAT PROSECUTION MUST PROVE FOR BNS SECTION 85:
- That cruelty occurred — physical or mental.
- That it was willful conduct likely to drive the woman to suicide or cause serious injury, OR that it was harassment for dowry.
- That YOU (specifically) participated — not just that cruelty occurred in the household.
Normal marital discord, arguments, and incompatibility is NOT cruelty under the law.

FOR DOWRY DEATH (BNS SECTION 80):
Prosecution benefits from a presumption: If the woman died within 7 years of marriage and dowry harassment is shown, court presumes dowry death. You must rebut this presumption with evidence.

COMMON DEFENSES:
No dowry demand: Produce evidence (messages, financial records) showing no demand was made. Show that gifts were given voluntarily by the bride's family.
Gifts were mutual: Many families give gifts freely — this is not "giving under compulsion."
False case for matrimonial advantage: If BNS 85 case is filed after the woman has already filed for divorce, show the timing and motive. Supreme Court has recognized misuse of 498A in matrimonial disputes.
Conduct of the complainant: If the complainant herself engaged in cruelty, harassment, or provocation, this is relevant to the defense.
Medical/forensic evidence contradicts allegations: In dowry death cases, challenge the post-mortem and forensic evidence if it supports natural/accidental death.

BAIL:
BNS Section 85: Non-bailable — apply to Magistrate immediately. Bail commonly granted for first-time accused with no prior record, especially after Arnesh Kumar guidelines.
BNS Section 80 (dowry death): Non-bailable, tried by Sessions Court — bail very difficult to obtain.
Anticipatory bail under BNSS Section 482: Apply to Sessions Court/HC before arrest.

PROTECTING YOURSELF:
Keep all communication records (WhatsApp, phone calls, emails) — they can prove or disprove what was said.
Never delete messages even if they seem embarrassing — selective deletion looks worse in court.
If harassment by wife's family: File counter-complaint for criminal intimidation (BNS 351) if threats were made.
