TELECOM CONSUMER RIGHTS IN INDIA
(TRAI Act 1997, TRAI Regulations, Telecom Commercial Communications Customer Preference Regulations)

============================
YOUR RIGHTS AS A TELECOM CONSUMER
============================

RIGHT TO QUALITY OF SERVICE (QoS):
TRAI (Telecom Regulatory Authority of India) sets mandatory Quality of Service benchmarks for all telecom operators:
- Call drop rate: Maximum 2% (operators exceeding this face penalty)
- Call setup success rate: Minimum 95%
- 4G/5G network performance benchmarks
- Broadband speed: Minimum guaranteed speed must be disclosed; actual average speed must be published monthly

CALL DROPS:
You have the right to quality service. If call drop rate is high: TRAI can penalise operator. You can complain to TRAI.

RIGHT TO INFORMATION:
Telecom operators must publish all tariff plans clearly on their website.
No hidden charges — all charges must be disclosed upfront.
Detailed bill showing all charges must be available (paper bill on request, digital bill by default).

SIM CARD RIGHTS:
SIM card is linked to your Aadhaar. Multiple SIMs can be issued on same Aadhaar (different operators).
If SIM is lost/stolen: Immediately call operator to block the SIM. File police complaint.
SIM swap fraud (someone gets duplicate SIM on your number to access your OTPs): Call operator's fraud helpline immediately, cybercrime.gov.in.

MOBILE NUMBER PORTABILITY (MNP):
You have the RIGHT to change your telecom operator while keeping the same mobile number. Porting is free. Process: SMS "PORT <Your 10-digit mobile number>" to 1900. You receive a Unique Porting Code (UPC). Submit at new operator's store. Porting completed within 7 days.
Operator CANNOT refuse porting except for outstanding dues or pending complaint.

RIGHT AGAINST UNSOLICITED COMMERCIAL COMMUNICATIONS (SPAM):
Register for DND (Do Not Disturb): Send SMS "START 0" to 1909 (fully blocked — no commercial calls/SMS) or select specific categories to block.
Report spam: SMS "COMPLAIN <operator code> <date and time of call> <telemarketer number>" to 1909.
After DND registration (within 30 days): Receiving unsolicited commercial communication is violation. Telemarketer can be blacklisted.

BROADBAND AND INTERNET RIGHTS:
Net Neutrality in India: TRAI has mandated net neutrality. ISPs cannot:
- Block any website or online service (without government order)
- Throttle (slow down) specific websites or services
- Charge differently for different websites (no "fast lanes")
Violation of net neutrality: Complaint to TRAI.

FAIR USAGE POLICY (FUP):
Internet plans often have FUP. After exhausting high-speed data, speed reduces (not blocked). This must be disclosed clearly in plan details.

============================
COMPLAINT REDRESSAL
============================

STEP 1 — TELECOM OPERATOR COMPLAINT:
Call customer care → Register complaint → Get complaint number.
Timelines for resolution (TRAI mandated):
- Fault repair: Within 3 working days (fixed line); 5 days (broadband)
- Billing disputes: Within 4 weeks
- Service disconnection without reason: Within 24 hours reconnection

STEP 2 — APPELLATE AUTHORITY:
If complaint not resolved within timelines OR if unsatisfied with resolution:
Approach Appellate Authority of the operator (each operator must have one) within 3 months of original complaint.

STEP 3 — TRAI/TDSAT:
TRAI: Complaint about tariff, quality of service, spam calls — directly to TRAI website (trai.gov.in) or Consumer Portal.
Telecom Disputes Settlement and Appellate Tribunal (TDSAT): For disputes between subscriber and operator exceeding Rs. 50 lakh or matters of national importance.

STEP 4 — Consumer Forum:
File complaint in District Consumer Forum for deficiency of service and compensation.

============================
LANDLINE AND BROADBAND RIGHTS
============================

BSNL/MTNL (Public Sector):
Complaints: 198 (fault repair), 1500 (general)
Online: bsnl.co.in → My Account → Complaint

Broadband fault repair timeline: 3 days. If not repaired: You are entitled to refund of proportionate subscription charges for downtime.

============================
OTT AND INTERNET PLATFORMS
============================

Over-The-Top (OTT) services (WhatsApp, Zoom, Netflix, etc.) are regulated separately under IT Act / MIB guidelines, not TRAI for voice/data. However, net neutrality protects them from being blocked/throttled by ISPs.

MESSAGING APP PRIVACY: WhatsApp, Telegram etc. are subject to Information Technology (Intermediary Guidelines and Digital Media Ethics Code) Rules 2021. They must:
- Appoint India-based Grievance Officer
- Respond to grievances within 15 days
- Significant Social Media Intermediaries (>5 million users) must also enable traceability of first originator of message if ordered by court/government

STREAMING CONTENT GRIEVANCES: OTT platforms must have a grievance redressal mechanism and content classification. Complaints: Contact platform's Grievance Officer → Level 2: Self-Regulatory Organisation → Level 3: Inter-Departmental Committee (MIB).
