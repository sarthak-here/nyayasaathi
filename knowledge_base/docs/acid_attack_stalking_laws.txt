ACID ATTACK, STALKING, VOYEURISM — BNS PROVISIONS AND VICTIM RIGHTS

============================
ACID ATTACK (BNS SECTIONS 124-125)
============================

WHAT IS AN ACID ATTACK:
Throwing or administering acid or any corrosive substance on any person, or attempting to do so.

PUNISHMENT (Section 124 BNS):
If the act causes permanent or partial damage or deformity or burns or maiming or disfigurement or disability to any part of the body:
- Minimum: 10 years rigorous imprisonment
- Maximum: Life imprisonment
- PLUS fine: Minimum Rs. 10 lakh (to be paid to victim for medical treatment)

ATTEMPT TO THROW ACID (Section 125 BNS):
Even throwing acid that does not cause injury:
- Imprisonment 5 to 7 years + fine

SALE OF ACID:
Under Madhu Kishwar v. State of Bihar (Supreme Court guidelines, 2013) and subsequent state regulations:
- Acid cannot be sold to persons below 18 years
- Buyer must produce valid ID proof
- Seller must maintain a register of purchasers
- Illegally selling acid: Criminal liability

IMMEDIATE STEPS FOR VICTIM:
1. CALL 112 immediately
2. Wash the acid off with large quantities of clean water for at least 15-20 minutes — do NOT use other substances
3. Go to nearest government hospital — government hospitals MUST provide FREE immediate treatment to acid attack victims (Supreme Court order)
4. Hospitals CANNOT deny treatment citing non-payment
5. File FIR — police must register FIR

VICTIM'S RIGHTS TO COMPENSATION:
Section 357A BNSS: Court shall award compensation to victim. In acid attack cases, minimum compensation of Rs. 3 lakh (some states: Rs. 5-10 lakh) from State Victim Compensation Fund.
DLSA (District Legal Services Authority) also provides immediate relief compensation.

GOVERNMENT SUPPORT:
- Free reconstructive surgeries at government hospitals
- Acid attack survivors entitled to disability certificate (disfigurement = disability under RPWD Act)
- Reservation benefits as disabled person
- Dedicated schemes in many states (Maharashtra, UP, etc.) for monthly pension, free education.

CHHANV Foundation and Sheroes Hangout: NGOs supporting acid attack survivors.

STALKING (BNS SECTION 78)
============================

WHAT IS STALKING:
Any man who:
1. Follows a woman and contacts her, or attempts to contact, to foster personal interaction despite clear indication of disinterest; OR
2. Monitors the use by a woman of the internet, email, or any other form of electronic communication

FIRST OFFENCE: Imprisonment up to 3 years + fine
SECOND OR SUBSEQUENT OFFENCE: Imprisonment up to 5 years + fine. Cognizable and non-bailable on second offence.

What counts as stalking: Following to office/home, repeated calls after being told to stop, monitoring social media/WhatsApp, sending repeated messages, sending unwanted gifts, showing up uninvited, having people follow the victim.

ONLINE STALKING: Monitoring a woman's online activity, tracking location through apps, hacking accounts, impersonating her online — all covered under BNS Section 78 and IT Act Section 66C/66D.

STEPS TO TAKE:
1. DOCUMENT everything — screenshots of messages, photos of stalker, call logs, witnesses
2. File complaint at nearest police station under Section 78 BNS
3. If police don't act: File complaint before Magistrate
4. Apply for protection order from Magistrate under Section 151 BNSS
5. File complaint with cyber cell if online stalking

PROTECTION ORDERS: Magistrate can order the accused to stay away from victim's residence, workplace, school.

VOYEURISM (BNS SECTION 77)
============================

WHAT IS VOYEURISM:
Any man who watches, or captures the image of, a woman engaging in a private act (including bathing, dressing, undressing, sexual act) in circumstances where she does not expect to be observed, OR who disseminates such image.

PRIVATE ACT: In a place where a person has a reasonable expectation of privacy; includes genitals whether covered or uncovered.

PUNISHMENT:
First offence: Imprisonment 1 to 3 years + fine
Subsequent offence: Imprisonment 3 to 7 years + fine

HIDDEN CAMERAS: Installing hidden cameras in trial rooms, bathrooms, hotel rooms, changing areas — voyeurism under BNS + offence under IT Act Section 66E (violation of privacy — imprisonment up to 3 years + fine Rs. 2 lakh).

WHAT TO DO IF YOU DISCOVER A HIDDEN CAMERA:
1. Do NOT touch it (preserve evidence)
2. Photograph its location with your own phone
3. Inform hotel management / shop management
4. Call police immediately (112)
5. File FIR under BNS Section 77 and IT Act Section 66E

MORPHED IMAGES / DEEPFAKES:
Creating and distributing morphed (digitally altered) intimate images of a woman:
- IT Act Section 66E + Section 67/67A (publishing obscene/sexually explicit material)
- BNS Section 77 if original images were captured
- National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal: cybercrime.gov.in / 1930

============================
SEXUAL HARASSMENT IN PUBLIC SPACES (STREET HARASSMENT)
============================

BNS Section 79 — Obscene Acts and Words in Public Place:
Any person who does any obscene act, utters obscene words/gestures to insult the modesty of a woman: Imprisonment up to 3 months or fine.

BNS Section 74 — Sexual Harassment (words, gesture, act):
Any man who makes sexually coloured remarks, shows pornography against a woman's will, demands sexual favours, makes physical contact of sexual nature: Imprisonment up to 3 years + fine.

Teasing/Eve-teasing on street, buses, public transport — FIR under Section 74 + 79 BNS.

WOMEN'S HELPLINE: 1091 (toll-free, 24 hours)
POLICE: 112
CYBER CRIME: 1930 / cybercrime.gov.in
