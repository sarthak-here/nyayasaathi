HINDU PERSONAL LAW — MARRIAGE, DIVORCE, INHERITANCE, ADOPTION, MAINTENANCE
(Hindu Marriage Act 1955, Hindu Succession Act 1956, Hindu Adoption and Maintenance Act 1956)

Applies to Hindus, Buddhists, Jains, and Sikhs. Does NOT apply to Muslims, Christians, Parsis (they have separate personal laws).

============================
HINDU MARRIAGE ACT 1955
============================

VALID MARRIAGE CONDITIONS (Section 5):
1. Neither party has a living spouse (monogamy — bigamy is a criminal offence under BNS)
2. Neither party is of unsound mind
3. Bridegroom is 21 years or above; Bride is 18 years or above (Prohibition of Child Marriage Act also applies)
4. Parties are not within prohibited degrees of relationship (sapindas) unless custom permits

REGISTRATION OF MARRIAGE:
Not mandatory under HMA 1955 at the central level, but many states make it mandatory. Register your marriage with Sub-Registrar for proof. Supreme Court has directed all marriages to be compulsorily registered.

GROUNDS FOR DIVORCE (Section 13):
Either spouse can file for divorce on grounds of:
1. Adultery (sexual intercourse with a person other than spouse)
2. Cruelty (physical or mental — including persistent demands for dowry, denying conjugal rights, false accusations)
3. Desertion for 2 continuous years without reasonable cause and without consent
4. Conversion to another religion
5. Mental disorder of incurable nature
6. Virulent and incurable form of leprosy (removed by 2019 amendment — no longer a ground)
7. Venereal disease in communicable form
8. Renunciation of the world (sannyas)
9. Not been heard of for 7 years (presumed dead)
10. No resumption of cohabitation for 1 year after judicial separation decree

ADDITIONAL GROUNDS FOR WIFE ONLY:
- Husband guilty of rape, sodomy, or bestiality
- No resumption of cohabitation after maintenance order
- Repudiation of marriage (marriage solemnized before 15 years when wife was below 15 years)

DIVORCE BY MUTUAL CONSENT (Section 13B):
Both parties file jointly after separation for at least 1 year. Second motion after 6 months (cooling off period — may be waived by court). Marriage is dissolved on court passing decree.

MAINTENANCE/ALIMONY (Section 24, 25 HMA):
Interim maintenance during court proceedings: Either spouse (including husband if wife earns more) can claim.
Permanent alimony: Court considers income, property, conduct of parties. No fixed formula — discretionary.

CHILD CUSTODY:
Court's paramount consideration is the welfare of the child (not the rights of parents).
Generally: children below 5 years stay with mother (tender age doctrine).
Visitation/access rights given to non-custodial parent.

============================
HINDU SUCCESSION ACT 1956 (Amended 2005)
============================

MAJOR 2005 AMENDMENT — DAUGHTERS' EQUAL RIGHTS:
Daughters are now COPARCENERS (co-owners) in ancestral (joint Hindu family/HUF) property — EQUAL to sons. This applies regardless of whether the father was alive or not on 9 September 2005.
Supreme Court confirmed in Vineeta Sharma v. Rakesh Sharma (2020): daughter's right is by birth.

INTESTATE SUCCESSION (dying without a will) — CLASS I HEIRS:
All Class I heirs take equal shares simultaneously:
- Son, Daughter
- Widow (wife)
- Mother
- Son of a pre-deceased son (grandson)
- Daughter of a pre-deceased son (granddaughter)
- Widow of pre-deceased son (daughter-in-law)
- Son of pre-deceased daughter
- Daughter of pre-deceased daughter
- Widow of pre-deceased son's pre-deceased son

NOTE: Class II heirs (father, siblings, etc.) only inherit if there are NO Class I heirs.

RIGHTS OF WIDOW (Wife):
Wife has equal share with sons and daughters in husband's separate property.
She also has rights in HUF property.
A widow can remarry and still retain her share she inherited.

STRIDHAN (Woman's own property):
Property received by woman as gifts (at marriage, from relatives) is her absolute property. She alone can dispose of it. Husband and in-laws have NO rights over stridhan.

WILL (TESTAMENT):
Any Hindu of sound mind above 18 years can make a will.
Will must be in writing, signed by testator, and attested by 2 witnesses.
A Hindu can by will exclude any heir, including daughters and sons.
Will must be registered for greater legal certainty (not mandatory).

============================
HINDU ADOPTION AND MAINTENANCE ACT 1956
============================

ADOPTION:
A Hindu male/female of sound mind and not a minor can adopt.
Consent of wife (or wives) required for husband to adopt.
A Hindu woman can adopt if: unmarried, divorced, or widowed.
Conditions for adopted child: must be Hindu, below 15 years (unless custom permits older), not already adopted.
After valid adoption: adoptee gets same rights as natural born child; severs all ties with natural family.

MAINTENANCE (Section 18–23):
A Hindu wife is entitled to be maintained by her husband during marriage including separation.
She loses right to maintenance if: she is unchaste, ceases to be Hindu by conversion, or remarries.
Children (legitimate and illegitimate): entitled to maintenance till majority (18 years); daughters till marriage.
Aged/infirm parents: entitled to maintenance from adult children with sufficient means.
Amount of maintenance: decided by court based on income of person liable and needs of claimant.

JOINT HINDU FAMILY (HUF):
HUF is a body of persons related by blood from a common ancestor.
HUF property is managed by Karta (eldest male member, or now female after 2005).
Partition of HUF: Any coparcener (son, daughter) can demand partition.
After partition: each coparcener gets equal share.
