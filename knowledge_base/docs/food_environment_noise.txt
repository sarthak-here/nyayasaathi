FOOD SAFETY, NOISE POLLUTION, ENVIRONMENT AND NEIGHBOURHOOD DISPUTES
=====================================================================

FOOD SAFETY AND ADULTERATION — FSSAI / FOOD SAFETY AND STANDARDS ACT 2006

YOUR RIGHTS AS A CONSUMER OF FOOD:
Right to safe food free from adulteration, contamination, or misbranding.
Right to accurate labeling (ingredients, expiry date, allergen info, nutritional info).
Right to file complaint against any food business.

FOOD ADULTERATION:
Section 59 FSS Act — Selling adulterated food:
Injury possible: Up to 1 year imprisonment + Rs. 3 lakh fine.
Grievous hurt caused: Up to 6 years + Rs. 5 lakh fine.
Death caused: Up to 7 years (life in some cases) + Rs. 10 lakh fine.

Section 63 FSS Act — Substandard food (below prescribed quality):
Fine up to Rs. 5 lakh.

Section 52 FSS Act — Misbranded food (false label, wrong info):
Fine up to Rs. 3 lakh.

HOW TO COMPLAIN ABOUT BAD FOOD:
1. FSSAI Consumer App: Anytime App or fssai.gov.in.
2. Consumer Helpline: 1800-11-4000 (food complaints).
3. Designated Food Safety Officers in every district.
4. Consumer Forum: Restaurant serving bad food, food delivery sending wrong/contaminated order = deficiency in service.
5. State Food Safety Commissioner.

RESTAURANT/HOTEL COMPLAINTS:
Overcharging (adding service charge without disclosure): Consumer Forum.
Serving expired food: FSSAI complaint + Consumer Forum.
Rodents/cockroaches in food: FSSAI complaint + municipal health department.
Note: Service charge is now VOLUNTARY per government guidelines — you can refuse to pay.

NOISE POLLUTION — ENVIRONMENT PROTECTION ACT / NOISE RULES 2000

PERMISSIBLE NOISE LIMITS (Ambient Noise Standards):
Residential areas: 55 dB (day, 6 AM to 10 PM), 45 dB (night, 10 PM to 6 AM).
Commercial areas: 65 dB (day), 55 dB (night).
Industrial areas: 75 dB (day), 70 dB (night).
Silence zones (hospitals, schools, courts): 50 dB (day), 40 dB (night).

LOUDSPEAKERS AND PUBLIC ADDRESS SYSTEMS:
Cannot be used between 10 PM and 6 AM without permission.
Maximum sound limit with permission: 10 dB above ambient level.
Police can seize loudspeaker equipment used in violation.

COMPLAINTS FOR NOISE POLLUTION:
Police station (Sections 268/290 IPC/BNS for public nuisance).
District Magistrate (can issue prohibitory order).
State Pollution Control Board (for industrial noise).
Municipality/Panchayat for neighbourhood noise.
NGT (National Green Tribunal) for serious environmental violations.

NEIGHBOURHOOD NOISE DISPUTES:
Loud music from neighbour: Police complaint for public nuisance.
Construction noise at night: Complaint to municipality + police.
Generator noise: Pollution Control Board complaint.

AIR POLLUTION AND INDUSTRIAL POLLUTION:
Environment Protection Act 1986 + Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act 1981.
State Pollution Control Boards (SPCBs) regulate.
Complaint to SPCB if factory/industry is violating emission norms.
National Green Tribunal (NGT): ngt.gov.in — for any environmental matters. Can be filed by any person.
NGT has suo motu power — can act on news reports. Public interest is sufficient to file.

WATER POLLUTION:
Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act 1974.
Complaint to State Pollution Control Board if river, lake, borewell, or water body is being polluted.
NGT for serious river/water body pollution.

SOLID WASTE / GARBAGE DUMPING:
Solid Waste Management Rules 2016.
Complaint to municipal corporation / panchayat.
If industrial: SPCB complaint.
NGT for large-scale illegal dumping affecting multiple people.

TREE CUTTING WITHOUT PERMISSION:
Tree felling without municipal permission is illegal in most states.
Complaint to Tree Officer of Municipal Corporation.
Forest Department for trees in forest land.
NGT for large-scale illegal felling.

CONSTRUCTION WITHOUT PERMISSION:
Complaint to local municipal corporation (building department).
They can issue stop-work notice and demolish unauthorized construction.
Complaint to District Collector if authorities are unresponsive.

PUBLIC NUISANCE — BNS SECTIONS 268-291
General public nuisance (affecting community health, safety, comfort):
Complaint to Magistrate under Section 133 BNSS: Magistrate can issue conditional order to remove nuisance within specified time.
Also: Civil suit for injunction (if affecting your private property specifically).
Also: Police complaint under BNS Section 289/290 (negligent acts causing public danger).
