RIGHT OF CHILDREN TO FREE AND COMPULSORY EDUCATION ACT 2009 (RTE ACT)

The RTE Act makes free and compulsory education a Fundamental Right for all children aged 6 to 14 years (Article 21A of the Constitution).

KEY RIGHTS OF CHILDREN UNDER RTE:

FREE EDUCATION:
Every child between 6 and 14 years has the RIGHT to free elementary education (Classes 1 to 8) in a neighbourhood school. No school can charge any fee, capitation fee, or screening procedure for admission in Classes 1 to 8. Any fee charged is illegal and must be refunded.

COMPULSORY ATTENDANCE:
State and local government is obligated to ensure every child attends school. The term "compulsory" places the duty on the government, not the child or parent.

NEIGHBOURHOOD SCHOOL: Government must establish a school within:
- 1 km for Classes 1–5 (primary)
- 3 km for Classes 6–8 (upper primary)
If no school exists: government must make alternate arrangements (free transport, residential facility).

25% RESERVATION IN PRIVATE SCHOOLS (Section 12(1)(c)):
Every private unaided school MUST reserve 25% of seats in Class 1 (entry-level class) for children from Economically Weaker Section (EWS) and Disadvantaged Groups.
- EWS: Family income below Rs. 1 lakh per year (may vary by state)
- Disadvantaged: SC, ST, OBC, children with disabilities, HIV-affected, children of bonded labour, orphans
- Government reimburses the school the reimbursement amount per child
- SCHOOL CANNOT CHARGE THESE CHILDREN ANY FEE

How to apply for 25% EWS seat:
- Apply online or offline to the District Education Officer in March each year
- Lottery system for selection
- Required documents: Income certificate, residence proof, birth certificate, caste certificate

NO DETENTION POLICY:
Until Class 8, no child can be held back (failed) or expelled from school. Continuous and Comprehensive Evaluation (CCE) used instead of annual examinations (though this has been partially modified by 2019 amendment allowing board exams in Class 5 and 8 at state discretion).

NO CORPORAL PUNISHMENT:
Physical punishment and mental harassment of children is PROHIBITED under RTE. Any teacher found inflicting corporal punishment can be disciplined under service rules. Complaint to District Education Officer.

NORMS AND STANDARDS FOR SCHOOLS (Schedule):
Schools must comply with:
- Pupil-Teacher Ratio: 30:1 for primary, 35:1 for upper primary
- Classrooms: At least 1 classroom per teacher and one for headmaster/headmistress
- Separate toilets for boys and girls
- Drinking water facility
- Playground
- Kitchen for midday meal
- Books, uniforms, stationery (free for government school students)
- Library

SCHOOL MANAGEMENT COMMITTEES (SMC):
Every government school must have an SMC with 75% parents/guardians (including SC, ST, OBC, weaker section representation). SMC monitors utilization of grants and school functioning. Parents have a right to participate in school governance.

TEACHER QUALIFICATIONS:
All teachers in elementary schools must have minimum qualifications: Class 12 + 2-year Diploma in Elementary Education (D.El.Ed.) or B.Ed. All existing teachers without qualifications had to acquire them by 2019.

ADMISSION:
No child can be refused admission. School cannot ask for birth certificate as sole proof of age — other documents like affidavit are acceptable. No entrance test for Classes 1–8.

TRANSFER CERTIFICATE:
Every child has the right to receive a Transfer Certificate promptly. School CANNOT withhold TC for non-payment of fees. TC must be issued within 10 days of request.

MID-DAY MEAL SCHEME (PM-POSHAN):
All government and government-aided schools must provide cooked nutritious meal to students — free of cost. Under National Food Security Act.

SPECIAL TRAINING FOR OUT-OF-SCHOOL CHILDREN:
Children above 6 years who could not attend school must be admitted in age-appropriate class and provided special training to reach grade level.

WHERE TO COMPLAIN:
1. Complaint to the Headmaster / Principal of school
2. Complaint to Block Education Officer (BEO)
3. Complaint to District Education Officer (DEO)
4. State Commission for Protection of Child Rights (SCPCR)
5. National Commission for Protection of Child Rights (NCPCR): ncpcr.gov.in / helpline 1800-180-1001
6. File writ petition in High Court for violation of Article 21A

PENALTIES:
School not following RTE norms: Fine up to Rs. 1 lakh; Rs. 10,000 per day for continuing violation.
School charging capitation fee: Fine up to 10 times the fee charged.
