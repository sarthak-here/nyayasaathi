MOTOR VEHICLES ACT 1988 — ACCIDENTS, HIT AND RUN, INSURANCE CLAIMS
====================================================================

OVERVIEW
The Motor Vehicles Act 1988 governs road accidents, compensation claims, traffic offences, and vehicle insurance. Accident compensation is claimed before Motor Accident Claims Tribunals (MACT), not civil courts.

THIS DOCUMENT COVERS TWO DIFFERENT SITUATIONS — READ CAREFULLY:
SITUATION A: You were INJURED or your vehicle was DAMAGED by another driver who fled → you are the VICTIM → see "Hit and Run Compensation" section below.
SITUATION B: YOU drove away after hitting someone's vehicle or a person → you are the DRIVER WHO FLED → see "Driver Who Fled the Scene" section below.

DRIVER WHO FLED THE SCENE — WHAT TO DO IF YOU HIT SOMETHING AND DROVE AWAY

Section 134 MV Act — Duty of driver after accident:
Every driver involved in an accident MUST:
1. Stop the vehicle immediately.
2. Provide medical assistance to any injured person.
3. Report the accident to the nearest police station within 24 hours.
Failure to do any of these is a separate offence under Section 187.

Section 187 MV Act — Punishment for failure to report accident:
Fine up to Rs. 500 or imprisonment up to 3 months (first offence).
Fine up to Rs. 1,000 or imprisonment up to 6 months (repeat offence).
This is relatively minor — BUT if a person was injured and you fled, BNS Section 106 (causing hurt by negligence) may also apply.

IF YOU HIT ONLY A PARKED VEHICLE (no person injured):
Your legal exposure is: Section 187 MV Act (failure to report) + civil liability for vehicle damage. No criminal negligence charge (no person was hurt).

IF YOU HIT A PERSON WHO WAS INJURED:
Your legal exposure is: Section 187 MV Act + BNS Section 106 (causing grievous hurt by negligence, up to 5 years) + civil compensation liability.

HOW TO FIX IT — VOLUNTARY REPORTING (STRONGLY ADVISED):

Courts and police treat voluntary reporting far more leniently than being traced after the fact.

Step 1 — IMMEDIATELY: Go to the nearest police station within 24 hours and report the accident voluntarily under Section 134 MV Act. Tell them: date, time, location, what happened, and that you panicked and left.

Step 2: If you know whose vehicle/property you damaged, contact the owner directly, apologize, and offer to pay for repairs. Get a written settlement if possible.

Step 3: Contact your motor insurance company and inform them of the accident. Voluntary disclosure protects your insurance position.

Step 4: Consult a lawyer before going to police if a person was injured — a lawyer can accompany you and help negotiate the reporting process. Contact DLSA for a free lawyer.

Step 5: If a person was injured, try to locate the victim and offer to pay medical expenses. This is treated as a strong mitigating factor by courts.

WHY VOLUNTARY REPORTING HELPS:
- Converts an aggravated offence into a minor one.
- Courts routinely reduce or waive sentences for genuinely remorseful first-time offenders who come forward voluntarily.
- Being traced later after CCTV or witness identification leads to arrest, FIR, and much harsher treatment.
- Insurance claims by the victim can be processed — which reduces civil liability on you.

PROPERTY DAMAGE ONLY — CIVIL LIABILITY:
If only property was damaged (parked car, boundary wall, etc.):
- You owe the owner compensation for actual repair cost.
- If you pay voluntarily, no court case is likely.
- If owner files civil suit, court will order payment plus interest.
- Motor insurance (own-damage cover) may cover the other vehicle's damage too.

HIT AND RUN — IMMEDIATE COMPENSATION

Section 161 — Solatium Scheme (Hit and Run where driver is unknown/untraced):
Death: Rs. 2 lakh fixed compensation.
Grievous hurt: Rs. 50,000 fixed compensation.
Paid from the Motor Vehicle Accident Fund (Central Government).
Available even if vehicle owner/driver is untraceable.
No need to prove fault — just prove accident occurred.
Apply to: Claims Inquiry Officer or nearest MACT.

Section 164 — No-Fault Liability (for all accidents):
Even if fault cannot be proven, compensation is payable:
Death: Rs. 5 lakh.
Grievous hurt: Rs. 2.5 lakh.
This is in addition to regular compensation if fault is later proven.

REGULAR ACCIDENT COMPENSATION — MACT CLAIM

Section 166 — Application for compensation to MACT:
File within: No hard statutory deadline but file early; courts rarely refuse on delay alone.
Where to file: MACT in district where accident occurred OR where owner/driver resides OR where claimant resides.
Who can claim: Injured person, or legal heirs/dependants in case of death.

COMPENSATION IS CALCULATED ON:
1. Medical expenses (past and future).
2. Loss of income/earning capacity.
3. Loss of dependency (for family members of deceased).
4. Pain, suffering, and mental agony.
5. Funeral expenses (death cases).
6. Loss of consortium (spouse's claim).
MACT uses multiplier method: (annual income) x (multiplier based on age) = compensation.

INSURANCE — MANDATORY THIRD-PARTY INSURANCE

Section 146 — Mandatory insurance:
Every vehicle must have at minimum third-party insurance covering injury/death to others.
Driving without insurance: Fine up to Rs. 2,000 (first offence), Rs. 4,000 (repeat).

Section 149 — Pay-and-recover principle:
Even if owner/driver violates policy conditions, insurance company must first pay compensation to victim and then recover from owner/driver. Victim cannot be denied compensation due to policy violations.

HOW TO FILE MACT CLAIM — STEP BY STEP

Step 1: File FIR at nearest police station immediately after accident.
Step 2: Get spot inspection done — police will note tyre marks, vehicle position, injuries.
Step 3: Collect documents: FIR copy, medical bills, post-mortem report (death cases), salary/income proof, insurance details of vehicle (check VAHAN portal — vahan.parivahan.gov.in), RC and driving licence of vehicle.
Step 4: File claim petition at MACT in prescribed form.
Step 5: Insurance company will file reply. Hearing and evidence. Award within reasonable time (target: 6 months under new rules).

INTERIM COMPENSATION:
MACT can award interim compensation at any stage to meet urgent needs. Apply specifically for this if you need immediate money for medical treatment.

HIT AND RUN — DRIVER FLED THE SCENE

If vehicle hit and fled:
1. Note vehicle number, colour, make, direction it fled.
2. Note time and location precisely.
3. File FIR with all available details — even partial number plate is useful.
4. Request police to check CCTV from area.
5. Apply for Rs. 2 lakh (death) or Rs. 50,000 (grievous hurt) solatium without waiting for driver to be found.

DRUNK DRIVING BY ACCUSED
Section 185 — Driving under influence of alcohol: Blood Alcohol Content above 30mg/100ml.
Punishment: Fine of Rs. 10,000 or imprisonment up to 6 months, or both.
Evidence of drunk driving significantly increases compensation in MACT claims.

COMPENSATION IN CASE OF DEATH — FAMILY MEMBERS CAN CLAIM
Legal heirs can claim even without formal legal heir certificate initially.
Dependants include: spouse, children, parents.
Courts have awarded compensation to live-in partners and financial dependants as well.

ACCIDENT BY GOVERNMENT VEHICLE
If accident is caused by government vehicle (bus, truck, police vehicle):
File MACT claim same as private vehicle.
Government is vicariously liable for negligence of its employees in course of duty.
Also: File RTI for vehicle maintenance records to establish negligence.

HELPFUL RESOURCES
- VAHAN portal (vehicle and insurance check): vahan.parivahan.gov.in
- Road Accident Fund claim form: Available at MACT offices.
- National Highways Authority of India crash helpline: 1033.
- Emergency: 112.

IF YOU ARE THE ACCUSED DRIVER — DEFENSE RIGHTS IN ACCIDENT / MV ACT CASES
==========================================================================

YOUR RIGHTS AS AN ACCUSED DRIVER:
- Right to remain silent — do NOT admit fault to anyone at the scene beyond calling for medical help.
- Right to a lawyer before giving any statement to police.
- Right to challenge the FIR if it contains false facts.

WHAT PROSECUTION MUST PROVE:
For BNS Section 106 (causing death by negligence): Prosecution must prove (1) you were driving negligently — not that an accident merely occurred, (2) the negligence CAUSED the death, (3) you were operating the vehicle. Accidents can happen even with careful driving — mere occurrence of accident is not proof of negligence.
For Section 187 MV Act (failure to report): Prosecution must prove you were the driver, the accident occurred, and you failed to report within 24 hours. Voluntary reporting before detection is a strong mitigating factor.

COMMON DEFENSES:
No negligence: The accident was caused by the other party's sudden negligent action (jaywalking, abrupt lane change), a road defect, or mechanical failure beyond your control.
Victim's contributory negligence: If the victim was also negligent (speeding, drunk driving, not wearing helmet), compensation can be reduced proportionately in civil claims.
Mechanical failure: If a tyre burst, brake failure, or steering failure caused the accident and you had properly maintained the vehicle, this reduces or negates criminal negligence.
Challenging speed/alcohol evidence: Speed readings and breath analyser tests must follow prescribed procedures — challenge if procedures were not followed.

BAIL:
BNS Section 106 (causing death by negligence, non-rash driving): Bailable — absolute right to bail.
BNS Section 106 (medical practitioner): Bailable.
Causing death by rash and negligent driving: Generally bailable at police station.
If racing or joyriding involved: Possibly non-bailable depending on facts.

INSURANCE PROTECTS YOU CIVILLY:
Third-party insurance covers civil compensation to the victim — your insurer pays the MACT award. This does not affect the criminal case but limits your financial exposure in civil proceedings.
