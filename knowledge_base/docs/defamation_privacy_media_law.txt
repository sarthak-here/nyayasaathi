DEFAMATION, PRIVACY, AND MEDIA LAW IN INDIA
(BNS Sections 356-357, IT Act, Press Council Act 1978)

============================
DEFAMATION (BNS SECTION 356)
============================

DEFINITION: Making or publishing any imputation concerning any person intending to harm, or knowing that it will harm, the reputation of that person.

FORMS OF DEFAMATION:
Libel: Written/printed/broadcast defamatory statement.
Slander: Spoken defamatory statement.
(India treats both as criminal offences under BNS, unlike UK where slander is generally not criminal.)

CRIMINAL DEFAMATION (BNS Section 356):
Punishment: Imprisonment up to 2 years OR fine OR both.
Non-cognizable, non-bailable, compoundable. Must be filed by victim; police cannot take suo motu action.
File complaint before Magistrate.

CIVIL DEFAMATION:
File civil suit in District Court for damages (compensation). No imprisonment — only monetary damages awarded.
Civil and criminal proceedings can run simultaneously.

WHAT IS NOT DEFAMATION (Exceptions under BNS Section 356):
1. True statement published for public good
2. Fair and accurate report of court proceedings
3. Fair comment on public acts of public servants
4. Fair criticism of published works (books, films, etc.)
5. Merited censure by a person with authority over another
6. Accusation made in good faith to authorised person
7. Opinion expressed by a person on another's conduct in a case in which he has interest
8. Warning given in good faith

KEY: Truth is an absolute defence in criminal defamation (provided it was for public good).
Key case: Subramanian Swamy v. Union of India (2016) — Supreme Court upheld validity of criminal defamation.

ONLINE DEFAMATION:
Defamatory posts, tweets, WhatsApp messages — criminal under BNS Section 356 + IT Act Section 66A (struck down) but civil liability remains under IT Act Section 66E (privacy violation) and tort law.
Platform (Twitter, Facebook) can be required to take down defamatory content on court order.
Defamatory content removal: File complaint with platform's Grievance Officer → court order for takedown → cybercrime cell if anonymous.

============================
CONTEMPT OF COURT
============================

CONTEMPT OF COURTS ACT 1971:

Civil Contempt: Willful disobedience of any judgment, decree, direction, order, writ of a court. Punishment: Imprisonment up to 6 months + fine up to Rs. 2,000.

Criminal Contempt: Publication of any matter that: scandalizes or tends to scandalize any court; prejudices any judicial proceeding; or interferes with or obstructs administration of justice.
Punishment: Imprisonment up to 6 months + fine up to Rs. 2,000.

Defenses: Truth (in public interest) is a valid defence for criminal contempt.
"Fair comment" on judicial proceedings after conclusion is permissible.

Scandalizing the Court: Social media posts attacking judges personally, making false allegations about judicial conduct — can attract criminal contempt.

============================
PRESS FREEDOM AND MEDIA RIGHTS
============================

PRESS COUNCIL OF INDIA ACT 1978:
Press Council of India (PCI) is a statutory body that:
- Adjudicates complaints against the press (newspapers, magazines) for inaccurate reporting, sensationalism, paid news, etc.
- Can warn or censure a publication
- Cannot impose fines or imprisonment
- Complaints filed by: Aggrieved persons OR the Council suo motu

FOR ELECTRONIC MEDIA (TV channels, news portals):
No equivalent of Press Council. Governed by:
- News Broadcasters and Digital Association (NBDA) — self-regulatory body
- Ministry of Information and Broadcasting (MIB)
- Press Information Bureau (PIB): Accreditation and fact-checking
- IT Rules 2021: News publishers must follow Code of Ethics; must have Grievance Officer; Level II: Self-Regulatory Body; Level III: Government body

RIGHT OF REPLY: You have the right to demand publication of a correction or reply from a newspaper/broadcaster that published something inaccurate/defamatory about you. File complaint with PCI/NBDA if refused.

PAID NEWS: Publishing news reports as paid advertisements without disclosure is violation of electoral law and press ethics. Complaint to Election Commission (during elections) or PCI.

JOURNALIST PROTECTION:
No specific national journalist protection law, but:
- Assaulting journalists: BNS provisions apply
- Attacking journalists for their work: Additional offence under some state laws
- Press Freedom: Protected under Article 19(1)(a) — Freedom of Expression
- Journalists cannot be compelled to reveal sources (shield law is evolving in India)

============================
RIGHT TO PRIVACY IN MEDIA
============================

PRIVACY PROTECTION:
Publication of private photographs/videos of a person without consent: Violation of privacy (Article 21), IT Act Section 66E, and potential defamation.
Publishing personal details (phone number, address) of private persons that leads to harassment: IT Act + civil liability.
REVENGE PORN (non-consensual intimate image sharing): BNS Section 77 (voyeurism), IT Act Section 67 (obscene content), and Section 67A (sexually explicit content). Complaint to cyber crime cell.
