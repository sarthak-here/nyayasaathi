HIGHER EDUCATION RIGHTS IN INDIA — UGC, AICTE, FEES, ADMISSIONS, RAGGING

============================
UNIVERSITY GRANTS COMMISSION (UGC)
============================

UGC regulates and maintains standards of higher education (universities, colleges).

DEGREE RECOGNITION:
Always verify that your college/university is UGC-recognised and the degree you will receive is valid.
Verify at: ugc.gov.in → Universities List
"Deemed Universities" and "Fake Universities": UGC publishes a list of fake universities — check before admission.

FEE REFUND POLICY (UGC Regulations 2023):
If a student withdraws admission:
- Withdrawal BEFORE the last date of admission: 90% refund (deducting Rs. 1,000 processing fee)
- Withdrawal within 15 days after last admission date: 80% refund
- Withdrawal 16-30 days after last admission date: 50% refund
- Withdrawal more than 30 days but before start of academic session: 25% refund
- After start of academic session: No refund (except security deposits — fully refundable)

ORIGINAL DOCUMENTS: Institutions CANNOT retain original documents (marksheets, certificates) as a condition of admission or to prevent transfer. UGC prohibits this practice. If documents are withheld: Complaint to UGC and approach High Court.

MIGRATING BETWEEN UNIVERSITIES: Students have the right to migrate from one university to another (within state or across India) per their university/UGC regulations.

ONLINE EDUCATION: UGC recognises online degrees from NAAC A/A+ accredited institutions. Check approved institutions at ugc.gov.in.

============================
ANTI-RAGGING LAW
============================

RAGGING IS A CRIMINAL OFFENCE. UGC Anti-Ragging Regulations 2009 and Supreme Court directives (Aman Kachroo case):

WHAT IS RAGGING:
Any disorderly conduct by a senior student toward a junior that involves teasing, treating, or handling with rudeness; indulging in rowdy/undisciplined activities; violation of decency, causing/likely to cause annoyance, hardship, psychological harm, or raising apprehension.

Includes: Forced consumption of alcohol, physical violence, sexual harassment, making juniors do tasks humiliating in nature, isolation/ostracism.

PUNISHMENT:
UGC can expel the student from institution.
Under IPC/BNS: Assault, wrongful confinement, sexual harassment, grievous hurt — all applicable.
Supreme Court: Expelled students cannot be re-admitted anywhere for 2 years.

EVERY INSTITUTION MUST HAVE:
- Anti-Ragging Committee
- Anti-Ragging Squad (for spot checks)
- Helpline number displayed prominently
- Zero tolerance policy with annual undertaking from students/parents

HOW TO COMPLAIN:
- Anti-Ragging Helpline (UGC): 1800-180-5522 (24x7, toll-free)
- Online: antiragging.in
- Complaint to institution's Anti-Ragging Committee
- FIR at police station
- Complaint to UGC (ugc.gov.in)
- High Court writ petition for inaction by institution

============================
AICTE (TECHNICAL EDUCATION)
============================

AICTE (All India Council for Technical Education) regulates technical institutions (engineering, management, pharmacy, architecture).

FEE REGULATION: AICTE regulates fees for technical institutions. State governments have Fee Regulation Committees. If institution charges more than approved fee: Complaint to AICTE / State Fee Regulatory Authority.

PLACEMENT CELL: Every AICTE-approved institution must have a placement cell. Non-provision of placement assistance: Complaint to AICTE.

GRIEVANCE CELL: Every technical institution must have a Student Grievance Redressal Committee. If grievance not resolved: Complaint to AICTE Grievance Portal (grievance.aicte-india.org).

============================
MEDICAL EDUCATION
============================

NMC (National Medical Commission) regulates medical education (MBBS, MD, MS).
NBE (National Board of Examinations) for DNB (post-graduate).
Admission: Through NEET (mandatory for all medical admissions).
Fee: State Counselling Committees regulate fees for state medical colleges.
Central Counselling: MCC (Medical Counselling Committee) for AIQ, Deemed Universities.

Capitation fee (donation demand) in medical colleges: Illegal. Complaint to NMC and Anti-Corruption Bureau.

EDUCATIONAL QUALIFICATION FRAUD:
Fake degrees, fake marksheets — criminal offence under BNS (forgery) + UP/state laws on education fraud.
Verify degrees at NDR (National Academic Depository): nad.gov.in or Digilocker.

============================
SCHOLARSHIP AND EDUCATIONAL LOANS
============================

PRE-MATRIC AND POST-MATRIC SCHOLARSHIPS:
SC/ST/OBC/Minority students are entitled to government scholarships. Apply at scholarship portals:
- NSP (National Scholarship Portal): scholarships.gov.in
- State-specific scholarship portals

If scholarship is not disbursed: Complaint to respective Ministry (Ministry of Social Justice for SC/OBC; Ministry of Tribal Affairs for ST; Ministry of Minority Affairs for minorities).

EDUCATION LOAN:
Under Vidya Lakshmi Portal (vidyalakshmi.co.in): Apply for education loan from multiple banks.
Banks CANNOT deny education loan solely on grounds of caste or social status (it constitutes discrimination under Article 15).
Interest subsidy: Central Scheme of Interest Subsidy (CSIS) — interest on education loans paid by government during moratorium period for economically weaker students.

GRIEVANCE ABOUT EDUCATION LOAN: Banking Ombudsman (RBI) handles complaints if bank refuses loan without valid reason or delays disbursement.
