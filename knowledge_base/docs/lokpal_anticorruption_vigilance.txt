LOKPAL AND LOKAYUKTAS ACT 2013, PREVENTION OF CORRUPTION ACT 1988 — FIGHTING CORRUPTION

============================
PREVENTION OF CORRUPTION ACT 1988 (AMENDED 2018)
============================

BRIBERY OF PUBLIC SERVANT:
Section 7 PC Act: Any public servant who accepts or obtains a bribe (gratification other than legal remuneration) for doing or forbearing to do an official act is guilty. Punishment: 3 to 7 years imprisonment + fine.

Section 7A: Taking bribe on behalf of public servant by a third party — same punishment.

BRIBERY BY PRIVATE PERSONS:
Section 8 PC Act (2018 Amendment): Any person who gives or promises or offers bribe to a public servant is ALSO guilty. Punishment: Up to 7 years + fine.
EXCEPTION: If the person was compelled to give bribe AND reports the matter to law enforcement within 7 days — they are PROTECTED from prosecution. This is the key provision to encourage reporting.

COMMERCIAL BRIBERY:
Giving bribe to obtain an undue advantage in a business transaction — also covered (2018 amendment aligns India with UN Convention Against Corruption).

CRIMINAL MISCONDUCT:
A public servant who possesses assets disproportionate to known sources of income is presumed to have committed criminal misconduct. Reversal of burden of proof — accused must explain how they acquired the assets.

INVESTIGATING AGENCIES:
Central Vigilance Commission (CVC): Supervises vigilance of central government.
Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI): Investigates serious corruption cases (central government employees, public sector enterprises). CBI needs state government permission to investigate state government employees.
State ACB (Anti-Corruption Bureau): Investigates state government corruption. Each state has its own ACB.
Enforcement Directorate (ED): Investigates money laundering angle of corruption.

TRAP CASES:
ACB/CVC conducts trap operations — marked currency given to complainant to bribe the official. After the bribe is given, CBI/ACB officers apprehend the official with the bribe money. Phenolphthalein powder on the money turns hands/clothes blue/purple on reaction.
Process: Complaint to ACB/CVC → Verification → Trap operation → Arrest → Prosecution.

============================
LOKPAL AND LOKAYUKTAS ACT 2013
============================

LOKPAL: A national anti-corruption ombudsman. Lokpal of India is headed by a Chairperson (retired Chief Justice or Judge of Supreme Court or eminent person) and up to 8 Members.

JURISDICTION OF LOKPAL:
- Prime Minister (with restrictions and safeguards)
- Union Ministers
- Members of Parliament
- Officers of central government (Groups A, B, C, D)
- Chairpersons, Members, Officers of Central Public Authorities
- Any person involved in alleged offences of public servants under PC Act

WHO CAN COMPLAIN TO LOKPAL:
Any person can file a complaint against the above. File complaint online at lokpal.nic.in.
Complaint must be made within 7 years of commission of the offence.
Frivolous/false complaints: Complainant can face prosecution.

LOKAYUKTA (STATE LEVEL):
Each state has a Lokayukta — equivalent of Lokpal at state level — to investigate corruption of state government employees and ministers. Jurisdiction and powers vary by state.

WHISTLE BLOWERS PROTECTION ACT 2014:
Any person can make a "public interest disclosure" (whistleblowing complaint) about corruption or wilful misuse of power/discretion by a public servant.
FILE WITH: Central Vigilance Commission (central employees), state vigilance commission (state employees).
PROTECTION: Identity of whistleblower is kept confidential. Victimisation of whistleblower (dismissal, transfer, harassment) — punishable with imprisonment up to 3 years and fine up to Rs. 50,000. Court/CVC can direct immediate protection.
Complaint to Lokpal also has whistleblower protection.

============================
WHERE TO REPORT CORRUPTION
============================

Central Government Corruption:
- CVC (Central Vigilance Commission): cvc.gov.in — File online complaint / call 1800-11-0180
- CBI: cbi.gov.in — serious corruption, online complaint form
- Lokpal: lokpal.nic.in
- PM Grievance Portal (CPGRAMS): pgportal.gov.in

State Government Corruption:
- State Anti-Corruption Bureau (ACB) — every state
- State Lokayukta
- Chief Minister's Grievance Cell

Police Corruption:
- State Human Rights Commission
- National Human Rights Commission (nhrc.nic.in)
- NHRC Helpline: 14433
- Internal Complaints Committee of Police

Judicial Corruption:
- Cannot directly be reported to CBI without High Court permission
- Complaint to Chief Justice of respective High Court
- Supreme Court: Can entertain complaints about High Court judges

RIGHT TO INFORMATION (RTI) AS ANTI-CORRUPTION TOOL:
RTI is the most powerful tool for an ordinary citizen to expose corruption. Ask for:
- File notings on decisions
- Inspection of documents
- List of beneficiaries
- Expenditure details
- Meeting records
This forces transparency and accountability. See also: rti_act_2005.txt

============================
PUNISHMENTS SUMMARISED
============================

Taking bribe (public servant): 3-7 years + fine
Giving bribe (private person): Up to 7 years + fine (but protected if reported within 7 days of compulsion)
Assets disproportionate to income: 4-10 years + fine
Habitual offender (PC Act): Minimum 5 years, up to 10 years
Criminal misconduct (Section 13): 4-10 years + fine
False complaint to Lokpal: Up to 1 year + fine (discourages frivolous complaints)
