PASSPORTS ACT 1967, FOREIGNERS ACT 1946 — PASSPORT AND VISA RIGHTS IN INDIA

============================
PASSPORT RIGHTS AND REMEDIES
============================

RIGHT TO PASSPORT (Section 6 Passports Act):
Every Indian citizen has the right to a passport UNLESS one of the specific grounds for refusal applies:
- Applicant is not a citizen of India
- Application made in wrong jurisdiction (not the correct Passport Seva Kendra)
- Applicant's name appears on specified list of citizens whose passports should be impounded
- Departure from India is restricted by court order
- Applicant has a warrant issued against them
- Proceedings for criminal offence punishable with 2+ years imprisonment are pending
- Repatriation of applicant from foreign country
- The applicant is assessed to be not of sound mind
- Passport is likely to be used for illegal purpose

Passports CANNOT be denied for ordinary political or ideological reasons.

TYPES OF PASSPORTS:
- Ordinary (Blue) Passport: For regular citizens. Valid 10 years (adults) / 5 years (children under 18).
- Official (White) Passport: For government officials on official duty.
- Diplomatic (Maroon) Passport: For Indian diplomatic personnel.

HOW TO APPLY:
1. Apply online at passportindia.gov.in
2. Book appointment at nearest Passport Seva Kendra (PSK) or Post Office Passport Seva Kendra (POPSK)
3. Bring documents: Aadhaar, birth certificate, address proof, old passport (if any)
4. Police verification: Done after passport is issued (for fresh passports) or before (for re-issue in some cases)

TATKAAL (URGENT) PASSPORT:
Higher fee; issued within 1–3 working days. Police verification done post-issuance. Verification adverse report can lead to revocation.

IMPOUNDING OF PASSPORT (Section 10):
Passport can be impounded by Passport Authority if:
- Passport obtained by suppression of material information
- Holder is in wrongful possession
- Passport is used for travelling to unauthorized countries
- Holder has been repatriated at government expense
- Holder is convicted of offence involving moral turpitude

If your passport is impounded:
1. Receive reasons in writing (mandatory)
2. File appeal before Appellate Authority (Ministry of External Affairs)
3. File writ petition in High Court if appeal fails

LOST PASSPORT:
1. File police report (FIR) immediately
2. Submit FIR + Form to Passport Seva Kendra for re-issue
3. Inform nearest Indian Embassy if lost abroad

CONSULAR SERVICES ABROAD:
Indian Embassy/Consulate provides:
- Emergency Certificate for travel back to India (if passport is lost)
- Attestation of documents
- Power of Attorney
- Emergency support for Indians in distress

OVERSEAS CITIZENSHIP OF INDIA (OCI):
OCI is NOT dual citizenship. OCI cardholders (of Indian origin) can:
- Enter India without visa for life
- Work and live in India without restrictions
- Cannot vote, hold constitutional office, government employment
OCI cards are now lifelong; no need to re-register at 20 and 50 years.

============================
FOREIGNERS IN INDIA — RIGHTS AND OBLIGATIONS
============================

VISA CATEGORIES:
Tourist, Business, Student, Employment, Research, Medical, Journalist, Missionary, Conference, Entry, Transit.

VISA EXTENSION:
Applications at Foreigners Regional Registration Office (FRRO/FRO) or Foreigners Division, Ministry of Home Affairs.
Overstaying visa: Punishable under Foreigners Act — detention and deportation. Fine and blacklisting from future entry.

REGISTRATION:
Foreigners from most countries on long-term visa (180+ days) must register with FRRO within 14 days of arrival.
Pakistani, Chinese, Afghan, and few other nationalities must register within 24 hours.
Register online at indianfrro.gov.in

RIGHTS OF FOREIGNERS IN INDIA:
- Right to life and personal liberty (Article 21 is available to all "persons" — not just citizens)
- Right against arbitrary arrest and detention
- Right to consular assistance (inform their embassy on arrest — Vienna Convention)
- Right to legal representation
- Right to medical aid
- Right not to be tortured

DEPORTATION:
Government can deport any foreigner who violates visa conditions or is deemed undesirable. Deportation order can be challenged before High Court.

REFUGEES AND ASYLUM:
India is NOT a signatory to the 1951 UN Refugee Convention. There is no formal refugee law.
UNHCR (UN High Commissioner for Refugees) provides refugee status in India to some extent.
Rohingya, Sri Lankan Tamil refugees, Tibetan refugees — complex status.
Asylum seekers are technically foreigners and subject to Foreigners Act.

FOREIGNERS TRIBUNAL (Assam):
Special tribunal in Assam to determine if a person is a foreigner or Indian citizen (related to NRC process). Burden of proof may fall on accused — challenged before courts.

OCI and PIO CARD holders:
PIO card discontinued in 2015; converted to OCI. If you still have PIO card, get it converted to OCI.
