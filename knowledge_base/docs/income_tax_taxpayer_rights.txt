INCOME TAX ACT 1961 — TAXPAYER RIGHTS AND REMEDIES

THE TAXPAYER'S CHARTER (Adopted 2020):
The Income Tax Department has adopted a Taxpayer's Charter that gives every taxpayer the following rights:
1. Right to be treated with fairness, courtesy, and respect
2. Right to expect competency of tax officials
3. Right to receive timely decisions
4. Right to privacy and confidentiality
5. Right to appeal against decisions
6. Right to certainty
7. Right to complete and accurate information
8. Right to a fair and just tax system

TAX SLABS (Assessment Year 2025-26, New Tax Regime — default):
Income up to Rs. 3,00,000: Nil
Rs. 3,00,001 to Rs. 7,00,000: 5%
Rs. 7,00,001 to Rs. 10,00,000: 10%
Rs. 10,00,001 to Rs. 12,00,000: 15%
Rs. 12,00,001 to Rs. 15,00,000: 20%
Above Rs. 15,00,000: 30%
Rebate under Section 87A: No tax if total income up to Rs. 7 lakh (new regime).

OLD TAX REGIME (Optional):
Up to Rs. 2.5 lakh: Nil; Up to Rs. 5 lakh: 5%; Up to Rs. 10 lakh: 20%; Above Rs. 10 lakh: 30%.
Old regime allows deductions: Section 80C (up to Rs. 1.5 lakh), 80D (health insurance), HRA, LTA, home loan interest.

FILING INCOME TAX RETURN (ITR):
Deadline: July 31 of the assessment year (for non-audit cases).
Belated return: Can be filed till December 31 with late fee Rs. 5,000 (Rs. 1,000 if income below Rs. 5 lakh).
Forms: ITR-1 (Sahaj) for salaried with income up to Rs. 50 lakh; ITR-2 for capital gains; ITR-3 for business income; ITR-4 (Sugam) for presumptive business income.
File online: incometax.gov.in

TDS — TAX DEDUCTED AT SOURCE:
Your employer, bank, or any payer must deduct TDS from payments above threshold and deposit it with government.
View your TDS on Form 26AS (available on income tax portal) and Annual Information Statement (AIS).
If excess TDS deducted: Claim refund in ITR.

NOTICE FROM INCOME TAX DEPARTMENT — YOUR RIGHTS:
Section 143(1): Intimation for income/deduction mismatch. Can be corrected online.
Section 143(2): Scrutiny notice — department wants to examine your return in detail. Respond within time specified.
Section 148: Notice for income escaping assessment (income you didn't disclose). Respond within 30 days.
Section 156: Demand notice — tax due. You can pay or appeal.

ALWAYS RESPOND TO NOTICES within due date. Ignoring notices can lead to ex-parte assessment (tax assessed without your input) and penalties.

RIGHT TO APPEAL:
If you disagree with assessment:
1. Commissioner of Income Tax (Appeals) [CIT(A)]: File within 30 days of service of order. File online on e-filing portal.
2. Income Tax Appellate Tribunal (ITAT): Second appeal within 60 days of CIT(A) order. No court fee.
3. High Court: On substantial question of law only.
4. Supreme Court: Final appeal.

DISPUTE RESOLUTION SCHEME:
- Vivad se Vishwas Scheme (2024): Settle pending tax disputes by paying disputed tax (no penalty/interest in many cases). Check current scheme status.

PENALTIES:
Section 234F: Late filing of ITR — Rs. 1,000 (income below Rs. 5 lakh) or Rs. 5,000.
Section 271(1)(c): Concealment of income — 100% to 300% of tax evaded.
Section 276C: Wilful attempt to evade tax — imprisonment 3 months to 7 years.
Section 270A: Under-reporting income — 50% penalty; mis-reporting — 200% penalty.

TAN (Tax Deduction Account Number):
Every entity deducting TDS must have TAN. File complaint on incometaxindiaefiling.gov.in if employer not deducting/depositing TDS.

PAN (Permanent Account Number):
Mandatory for income above Rs. 2.5 lakh, bank accounts, property transactions, vehicles, foreign travel. Apply online at NSDL/UTIITSL. Lost PAN: Apply for duplicate.

REFUND:
If ITR shows refund, it is credited to bank account linked to PAN within 20-45 days of ITR processing. Check status on incometax.gov.in. If delayed beyond 3 months: Claim interest at 6% per annum under Section 244A.

INCOME TAX OMBUDSMAN:
Grievances against tax department officials: Complain to Principal Commissioner of Income Tax or on e-filing portal. Ombudsman scheme available in major cities.

FORM 16 / FORM 16A:
Employer MUST provide Form 16 (TDS certificate for salary) by June 15 each year. If employer doesn't provide, file complaint with jurisdictional tax officer. Form 16A: TDS certificate for non-salary payments.

SECTION 80C DEDUCTIONS (Old Regime):
Up to Rs. 1.5 lakh deduction for: PPF, EPF, Life insurance premium, ELSS (mutual funds), NSC, 5-year bank FD, home loan principal repayment, SSY (Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana), tuition fees.
Section 80D: Health insurance premium up to Rs. 25,000 (Rs. 50,000 for senior citizens).
Section 24(b): Home loan interest up to Rs. 2 lakh deduction (self-occupied property).
Section 80G: Donations to approved charitable institutions — 50% or 100% deduction.
