RESERVATION RIGHTS IN INDIA — SC, ST, OBC, EWS CATEGORIES

============================
CONSTITUTIONAL BASIS
============================

Article 15(4): State can make special provisions for socially and educationally backward classes, SC, and ST (education).
Article 16(4): State can make provisions for reservation in appointments for any backward class not adequately represented.
Article 46: State shall promote educational and economic interests of weaker sections, especially SC and ST.
Article 335: Claims of SC and ST shall be taken into consideration consistently with maintenance of efficiency of administration.

============================
SCHEDULED CASTES (SC) — RESERVATION RIGHTS
============================

RESERVATION PERCENTAGES (Central Government):
- Education (Central Institutions): 15%
- Government Jobs (Direct Recruitment): 15%
- Promotions: 15% (Supreme Court upheld in M. Nagaraj case)

IDENTIFICATION:
SC status is identified by caste and state — not all castes are SC in all states. Verified by Caste Certificate issued by Tahsildar/SDM.

CREAMY LAYER: NO creamy layer for SC/ST — ALL SC/ST persons are entitled to reservation regardless of income or parent's job status. (This is different from OBC).

SC CERTIFICATE FRAUD:
Obtaining SC/ST certificate fraudulently: Criminal offence. Genuine SC/ST persons deprived: Complaint to SC/ST Commission or High Court.

NATIONAL COMMISSION FOR SCHEDULED CASTES (NCSC):
Constitutional body. Investigates complaints related to rights of SCs. File complaint at ncsc.nic.in.

============================
SCHEDULED TRIBES (ST) — RESERVATION RIGHTS
============================

RESERVATION: 7.5% in Central Government education and employment.
Forest Rights: See Forest Rights Act 2006 (separate document).
Fifth and Sixth Schedule protection for tribal areas.

TRIBAL LAND RIGHTS (PESA — Panchayats (Extension to Scheduled Areas) Act 1996):
In Fifth Schedule areas (tribal areas across 10 states), Gram Sabha has special powers:
- Approval needed for land acquisition in Scheduled Areas
- Gram Sabha controls minor forest produce
- Gram Sabha can regulate money lending to tribals
- No land acquisition without Gram Sabha consent

ALIENATION OF TRIBAL LAND: In most tribal states, tribals CANNOT sell their land to non-tribals without government permission. Illegal transfers can be cancelled.

NATIONAL COMMISSION FOR SCHEDULED TRIBES (NCST): Constitutional body. File complaints at ncst.gov.in.

============================
OTHER BACKWARD CLASSES (OBC) — RESERVATION RIGHTS
============================

RESERVATION: 27% in Central Government education and employment.
Creamy Layer EXCLUDED from OBC reservation: OBC persons whose parents' income exceeds Rs. 8 lakh per year (revised periodically) or who belong to specified constitutional/higher government positions are in "creamy layer" and NOT entitled to OBC reservation.

CENTRAL OBC LIST: National Commission for Backward Classes (NCBC) recommends inclusion/exclusion of communities in Central OBC list.
STATE OBC LIST: Separate from Central OBC list. A community may be OBC in one state but not another.

VERIFY OBC STATUS: Central List of OBCs available at ncbc.nic.in.
OBC Certificate: Issued by competent authority (Tahsildar/SDM) in prescribed format for Central OBC.

NATIONAL COMMISSION FOR BACKWARD CLASSES (NCBC): Constitutional body (2018 amendment). Investigates complaints about rights of backward classes. ncbc.nic.in.

EDUCATIONAL RESERVATIONS (Post-Graduate):
- All centrally-funded institutions must maintain SC/ST/OBC quotas
- If seats go unfilled under reserved category: Carry forward for 3 years, then dereservation

============================
EWS RESERVATION (ECONOMICALLY WEAKER SECTIONS)
============================

103RD CONSTITUTIONAL AMENDMENT (2019): Added 10% reservation for EWS (Economically Weaker Sections — those not covered by existing reservations) in education and government employment.
Supreme Court upheld its validity in Janhit Abhiyan v. Union of India (2022) (3:2 majority).

ELIGIBILITY FOR EWS:
- Family income below Rs. 8 lakh per year from all sources
- Agricultural land below 5 acres
- Residential flat below 1,000 sq ft
- Residential plot below 100 sq yards (municipal) or 200 sq yards (non-municipal)
- Does NOT apply to SC/ST/OBC (they have their own reservations)

EWS CERTIFICATE: Issued by Tahsildar/Revenue Officer/SDM.

============================
DISABILITY RESERVATION
============================

RPWD Act 2016: 4% reservation in government jobs and educational institutions for persons with benchmarked disabilities:
- 1% each for blindness and low vision
- 1% for deaf and hard of hearing
- 1% for locomotor disability including cerebral palsy, leprosy cured, dwarfism, acid attack victims, muscular dystrophy

============================
PRIVATE SECTOR RESERVATION
============================

Currently NO mandatory reservation in private sector employment. But:
- Some states have enacted laws requiring minimum percentage of local residents in private sector jobs (Gujarat, Haryana, AP, etc.)
- Constitutional validity of such state laws is contested in courts.

============================
HOW TO GET CERTIFICATES
============================

Caste/OBC/EWS Certificate:
Apply at Tahsildar's office or online through state's revenue portal.
Documents: Aadhaar card, ration card, school leaving certificate (for date/place of birth), old caste certificate of parent if available.
Processing time: 7-30 days (varies by state).

If certificate is wrongly denied: Appeal to District Collector/SDM. Then High Court.
Fake certificate: Criminal offence (forgery under BNS + relevant SC/ST Act provisions).
