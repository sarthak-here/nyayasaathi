EMPLOYEES' PROVIDENT FUND ACT 1952 (EPF), EMPLOYEES' STATE INSURANCE ACT 1948 (ESI), AND PAYMENT OF GRATUITY ACT 1972

These three laws are core labour welfare laws protecting employees in India.

============================
EMPLOYEES' PROVIDENT FUND (EPF)
============================

APPLICABILITY: Applies to establishments with 20 or more employees.

CONTRIBUTION RATES:
- Employee contributes: 12% of basic salary + dearness allowance
- Employer contributes: 12% of basic salary (3.67% goes to EPF, 8.33% goes to EPS — Employee Pension Scheme)
- Maximum salary cap for mandatory EPF: No ceiling (since 2014 amendment); employees above Rs. 15,000/month basic salary can opt out.

YOUR EPF RIGHTS:
1. Employer MUST deduct and deposit EPF every month before the 15th of next month
2. Employee can check their EPF balance on EPFO portal (epfindia.gov.in) or UMANG app using UAN (Universal Account Number)
3. EPF account is portable — UAN remains same even if you change jobs
4. Employer must activate your UAN and link it to your Aadhaar

WITHDRAWAL RULES:
- Full withdrawal: After 2 months of unemployment (reduced from earlier 1 year)
- Partial withdrawal (advance): Allowed for marriage (50%), education, medical, home purchase/construction/renovation, disability
- After retirement (58 years): Full withdrawal with pension
- Death: Nominee gets full EPF + EPS + EDLI insurance (up to Rs. 7 lakh)

EPF PENSION (EPS):
- After 10 years of service, entitled to monthly pension from EPFO
- Minimum pension: Rs. 1,000/month
- Pension starts at age 58 (early pension at 50 with reduced amount)

IF EMPLOYER DOES NOT DEPOSIT EPF:
This is a criminal offence under Section 14 EPF Act — imprisonment up to 3 years and fine.
1. File complaint online at epfindia.gov.in or call helpline 1800-118-005
2. File complaint with Regional Provident Fund Commissioner (RPFC)
3. RPFC can attach employer's property and auction to recover dues
4. Employee continues to earn interest even if employer doesn't deposit

FORM 31 (Advance), FORM 19 (Final Settlement), FORM 10C (Pension), FORM 10D (Monthly Pension) — claim online via UAN portal.

============================
EMPLOYEES' STATE INSURANCE (ESI)
============================

APPLICABILITY: Establishments with 10 or more employees (some states: 20) where employees earn up to Rs. 21,000/month (Rs. 25,000 for disabled persons).

CONTRIBUTION:
- Employee: 0.75% of wages
- Employer: 3.25% of wages

ESI BENEFITS — YOUR RIGHTS:
1. Medical Benefit: Free medical treatment for employee AND family (spouse, children, parents) at ESI hospitals and dispensaries — no limit
2. Sickness Benefit: 70% of daily wages for up to 91 days per year during certified illness
3. Extended Sickness Benefit: For 34 specified long-term diseases (TB, cancer, etc.) — up to 2 years at 80% of wages
4. Maternity Benefit: Full wages for 26 weeks maternity leave (employees registered under ESI)
5. Disablement Benefit: Permanent partial/total disablement due to employment injury — monthly pension for life
6. Dependants' Benefit: Monthly pension to dependants (widow, children) of deceased employee
7. Funeral Expenses: Rs. 15,000 to family on death of insured person
8. Unemployment Allowance (RGSKKY): Under Rajiv Gandhi Shramik Kalyan Yojana, up to 24 months cash relief if retrenched/laid off

IF EMPLOYER DOESN'T REGISTER OR CONTRIBUTE:
File complaint with Regional Director, ESIC (Employee State Insurance Corporation).
ESIC can recover as arrears of land revenue. Employer faces prosecution.
Helpline: 1800-11-2526

============================
PAYMENT OF GRATUITY ACT 1972
============================

APPLICABILITY: Establishments with 10 or more employees. Once applicable, remains applicable even if below 10 employees later.

ELIGIBILITY: Every employee who has worked for MINIMUM 5 CONTINUOUS YEARS (except in case of death or disablement, where gratuity is paid regardless of years served).

CALCULATION:
Gratuity = (Last drawn basic salary + DA) × 15/26 × Number of years of service
- 15 days salary per completed year of service
- 26 is the number of working days per month
- Fraction of year more than 6 months is rounded up to full year
- Maximum gratuity: Rs. 20 lakh (for private sector; no cap for government employees)

WHEN IS GRATUITY PAID?
- On superannuation (retirement)
- On resignation after 5 years
- On death (paid to nominee/legal heir — no 5-year requirement)
- On disablement (no 5-year requirement)
- On termination after 5 years

PAYMENT TIMELINE: Gratuity must be paid within 30 days of it becoming due.
If delayed: Simple interest at rate notified by government from the due date.

DISPUTE RESOLUTION:
1. File application to Controlling Authority (Labour Commissioner) under Section 7(4) within 60 days
2. Controlling Authority summons employer and passes award
3. Appeal to Appellate Authority (District Judge) within 60 days
4. Further appeal to High Court

TAX TREATMENT:
Gratuity received from government: Fully exempt from tax
Private sector: Exempt up to Rs. 20 lakh

FORFEITURE OF GRATUITY: Employer can forfeit (wholly or partially) gratuity only if employee is terminated for:
- Moral turpitude or wilful omission causing damage/loss (up to extent of damage)
- Violence, disorderly conduct

NOMINATION: Employee must submit Form F (nomination) to employer to nominate beneficiary for gratuity in case of death.
