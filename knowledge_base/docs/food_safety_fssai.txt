FOOD SAFETY AND STANDARDS ACT 2006 (FSSAI) — CONSUMER RIGHTS ON FOOD SAFETY

The Food Safety and Standards Act 2006 governs the manufacture, storage, distribution, sale and import of food in India. FSSAI (Food Safety and Standards Authority of India) is the regulatory body.

============================
YOUR RIGHTS AS A FOOD CONSUMER
============================

RIGHT TO SAFE FOOD:
Every consumer has the right to food that is safe, unadulterated, and fit for consumption. Sale of adulterated, contaminated, or unsafe food is a criminal offence.

RIGHT TO INFORMATION:
Every packaged food must display:
- Name and address of manufacturer/packer
- Net quantity (weight/volume)
- Date of manufacture and "Best Before" / "Use By" date
- List of ingredients in descending order of their proportion
- Nutritional information (per 100g/ml)
- FSSAI licence number
- Country of origin (for imported food)
- "Veg" (green circle) or "Non-veg" (brown/red triangle) symbol
- Allergen information (if applicable)
- Any artificial colour, flavour, or preservative

SELLING EXPIRED FOOD: Criminal offence under FSSA. Complaint can be filed.

ADULTERATION: Adding any substance that renders food injurious to health.

KEY OFFENCES AND PENALTIES:

Sale of Unsafe Food:
- First offence (no injury): Fine up to Rs. 2 lakh
- Grievous injury caused: Imprisonment up to 1 year + fine up to Rs. 3 lakh
- Death caused: Imprisonment up to 6 years + fine up to Rs. 5 lakh

Sub-standard Food (quality below prescribed standard):
Fine up to Rs. 5 lakh

Misbranded Food (incorrect/misleading labelling):
Fine up to Rs. 3 lakh

Misleading Advertisement:
Fine up to Rs. 10 lakh. Repeat offence: Prosecution.

Extraneous matter (insect, foreign material, hair):
Fine up to Rs. 1 lakh

Manufacturing/Selling without FSSAI Licence:
Fine up to Rs. 5 lakh (for operators who need licence); Rs. 25,000 (small/petty operators needing registration)

RESTAURANT AND FOOD SERVICE RIGHTS:
- You have the right to know ingredients of food being served
- You cannot be forced to pay for contaminated or substandard food
- Service charges at restaurants: NOT mandatory by law (CCPA Guidelines 2022). Consumer has the right NOT to pay service charge. Complaint to Consumer Helpline 1915 if forced to pay.
- Restaurants must display menu and prices clearly
- No charging more than MRP printed on packaged items

BOTTLED WATER (IS 14543 Standard):
Packaged drinking water must meet BIS (Bureau of Indian Standards) IS 14543. Deviation: File complaint with BIS.

MILK ADULTERATION:
Addition of water, detergent, starch, or synthetic milk is an offence. Most states have specific campaigns. Report to Food Safety Officer (FSO).

RESTAURANT HYGIENE:
Restaurants must maintain hygiene, have proper food storage, and valid FSSAI licence. Unhygienic restaurant: Complaint to FSO leads to inspection and possible closure.

============================
HOW TO COMPLAIN
============================

1. Local Food Safety Officer (FSO):
Every district has Food Safety Officers who can inspect, sample, and prosecute. File written complaint.

2. FSSAI Complaint (Online):
Portal: foscos.fssai.gov.in
Food Safety Connect App (FSSAI): Report food safety concerns directly.
FSSAI Helpline: 1800-11-2100 (toll-free)
Email: foodsafetyhelpline@fssai.gov.in

3. State Food Safety Authority:
Each state has a Designated Officer (DO) and Commissioner of Food Safety.

4. Consumer Helpline:
National Consumer Helpline: 1800-11-4000 / 14404 (toll-free)
INGRAM portal: consumerhelpline.gov.in

5. Consumer Forum:
File complaint in District Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission for compensation. Deficiency of service by food operator, harm caused by adulterated food — claim compensation.

6. Police Complaint:
For deliberate food adulteration causing injury or death — file FIR under BNS.

============================
SPECIAL PROVISIONS
============================

ORGANIC FOOD: Must be certified by approved certification bodies. Claiming "organic" without certification is misleading labelling.

FORTIFIED FOOD: Food fortified with vitamins, minerals (like rice fortified with iron) must display "F++" logo.

FSSAI STAR RATING (HYGIENE RATING): Restaurants and food businesses can display FSSAI hygiene rating (1–5 stars) based on government inspection. Higher stars = higher hygiene standards.

FOOD RECALL: If unsafe food is identified, FSSAI can order recall. Consumer can return recalled product and get refund.

ALCOHOL REGULATION: State governments regulate alcohol (not FSSAI). Spurious liquor (hooch) tragedies — responsibility of state excise department. File complaint with State Excise Commissioner.

GENETICALLY MODIFIED (GM) FOOD: Currently, only Bt Cotton is approved for commercial cultivation in India. GM food products for consumption require GEAC (Genetic Engineering Appraisal Committee) approval. Unapproved GM food products: File complaint with FSSAI/GEAC.
