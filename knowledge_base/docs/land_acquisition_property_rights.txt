RIGHT TO FAIR COMPENSATION AND TRANSPARENCY IN LAND ACQUISITION, REHABILITATION AND RESETTLEMENT ACT 2013 (LARR ACT)

The LARR Act 2013 replaced the colonial Land Acquisition Act 1894 and gives significantly stronger rights to landowners and displaced persons.

WHEN CAN GOVERNMENT ACQUIRE LAND?
Only for "public purpose" including:
- Strategic purposes (defence, national security)
- Infrastructure (roads, railways, airports, power)
- Industrial corridors and manufacturing zones
- Planned development, housing for poor
- Government projects

For private companies: Government can acquire only if 80% of project-affected families consent (Social Impact Assessment + 80% consent).
For PPP (Public-Private Partnership): 70% consent required.

SOCIAL IMPACT ASSESSMENT (SIA):
Before acquisition, an SIA must be conducted by government to assess the nature and intensity of the impact. SIA must be completed within 6 months.
Affected persons can participate in SIA process and raise objections.

COMPENSATION — YOUR RIGHTS:

Market Value: Determined as the highest of:
- Value of land as per sale deed registered in the area in the last 3 years
- Minimum land value fixed by government for registration purposes (circle rate)
- Consented amount in case of private acquisition

MULTIPLIER: Market value is multiplied by:
- 2x in rural areas
- 1x in urban areas (no multiplier)

SOLATIUM: 100% of the total compensation (so effectively doubles the already-multiplied amount in rural areas).

EXAMPLE: Rural land with market value Rs. 10 lakh:
Compensation = Rs. 10 lakh × 2 (multiplier) = Rs. 20 lakh
+ 100% solatium on Rs. 20 lakh = Rs. 20 lakh
Total = Rs. 40 lakh

REHABILITATION AND RESETTLEMENT (R&R) — in addition to compensation:
Each displaced family (losing house, livelihood) is entitled to R&R benefits including:
- One-time payment (First Schedule: Rs. 50,000 to 5 lakh depending on category)
- Subsistence allowance: Rs. 3,000/month for 12 months
- Transportation allowance for moving belongings
- One-time resettlement allowance: Rs. 50,000
- Agricultural land (minimum 1 acre) from irrigated land bank if available
- One employment per affected family OR annuity of Rs. 2,000/month for 20 years OR 500 equity shares of the project company

HOMESTEAD LAND: Every displaced family that owned a house must be provided a plot in resettlement area OR Rs. 1.5 lakh for house construction (urban) or Rs. 1 lakh (rural) + housing benefits.

URGENCY CLAUSE (Section 40):
Government can invoke urgency clause for national defence or any sudden change of circumstances. Only 2 notices required instead of full process. Compensation still must be paid before taking possession.

ACQUISITION PROCEDURE:
1. Social Impact Assessment (6 months)
2. Preliminary Notification (Section 11) — published in official gazette and local newspapers
3. Hearing of objections (within 60 days of preliminary notification)
4. Environmental Appraisal
5. Declaration under Section 19 (finalizing acquisition)
6. Notice to affected persons
7. Payment of compensation before taking possession (mandatory)

OBJECTION TO ACQUISITION:
Within 60 days of preliminary notification, file written objection to Collector (District Magistrate) stating reasons why the land should not be acquired or why compensation is inadequate.
Further appeal: Land Acquisition Rehabilitation and Resettlement Authority (LARRA) — like a court for land acquisition disputes.

IF COMPENSATION IS INADEQUATE:
- File reference to Collector under Section 64 within 6 weeks of award — Collector must refer to LARRA
- LARRA can increase compensation
- Appeal from LARRA to High Court

RETURN OF ACQUIRED LAND:
If acquired land is not used for the stated public purpose within 5 years, it must be returned to original owners or their legal heirs, or transferred to Government Land Bank. This is a major new right under 2013 Act.

TRIBAL LAND — SPECIAL PROTECTION:
Acquisition of land in Scheduled Areas (Fifth and Sixth Schedule areas with tribal populations) requires:
- Free, Prior, and Informed Consent of Gram Sabha
- Prior consent of tribal communities under Forest Rights Act
- Resettlement BEFORE displacement

PATTA LAND (Assigned Land):
Many poor families were given government land (patta) for cultivation. If this land is acquired, they are entitled to full compensation like any other landowner.

TENANTS AND SHARECROPPERS:
Persons who have been cultivating government/private land under lease or sharing arrangements for 3 years are entitled to:
- Rehabilitation and Resettlement benefits (not compensation, unless they have legal entitlement)
- One-time payment

SQUATTERS:
Persons living on government land (non-forest) without legal title for a long time are entitled to:
- Rehabilitation and Resettlement benefits (house plot in resettlement colony)
