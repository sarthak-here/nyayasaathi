INDUSTRIAL DISPUTES ACT 1947 — RETRENCHMENT, LAYOFF, CLOSURE, TERMINATION RIGHTS

This law governs resolution of industrial disputes between employers and workers, and protects workers against unfair dismissal.

DEFINITIONS:
- Workman: Any person employed in skilled, unskilled, manual, operational, technical, clerical, or supervisory work earning up to Rs. 25,000/month (for certain provisions). Managers and supervisors above this limit are excluded.
- Industry: Any business, trade, undertaking, or calling of employers including services.

RETRENCHMENT (Section 25F) — UNLAWFUL UNLESS:
Retrenchment means termination of service of a workman for any reason (other than disciplinary action, retirement, or end of fixed-term contract).

Before retrenching a workman who has completed 1 year of continuous service, employer MUST:
1. Give 1 month's written notice OR pay 1 month wages in lieu of notice
2. Pay retrenchment compensation: 15 days average pay for every completed year of service
3. Seek prior permission from appropriate government if 100+ workers (Section 25N)
4. Follow "last in, first out" principle — junior employees retrenched first

If retrenchment is without following procedure: It is void and illegal. Worker entitled to reinstatement with full back wages.

LAYOFF (Section 25C):
Temporary inability of employer to give employment due to shortage of material, power, or breakdown of machinery.

Layoff Compensation:
- 50% of basic wages + dearness allowance per day for all days of layoff (excluding weekly holidays)
- First 45 days in a year: compensation mandatory
- After 45 days: employer may either pay layoff compensation or retrench

Worker is NOT entitled to layoff compensation if they:
- Refuse alternative employment in same establishment
- Do not present themselves at the workplace during the normal working hours every day
- Work in an establishment with less than 50 workers

CLOSURE (Section 25O):
Employer must give 3 months' notice to appropriate government and workers before closure of establishment with 100+ workers, and seek permission.
If closure is illegal (no permission): workers entitled to 45 days' average wages per year of service.

STANDING ORDERS (Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act 1946):
Establishments with 100+ workers must have certified Standing Orders defining conditions of employment. These must specify:
- Classification of workers
- Hours of work, holidays, pay days
- Shift working
- Attendance, leave rules
- Termination, suspension, dismissal procedures

UNFAIR LABOUR PRACTICES (Fifth Schedule IDA):
Employer cannot:
- Restrain workers from joining trade unions
- Threaten workers with dismissal for union activity
- Discharge workers for legitimate union activity
- Establish employer-sponsored unions to interfere with workers' right to organize
- Grant preferential treatment to one union
- Recruit workers during a legal strike

DISCIPLINARY PROCEEDINGS — DOMESTIC ENQUIRY:
Before dismissal for misconduct, employer must:
1. Issue charge sheet specifying the charge
2. Give worker reasonable time (at least 48 hours) to reply
3. Conduct a domestic enquiry with enquiry officer
4. Give worker opportunity to be heard and cross-examine witnesses
5. Receive enquiry report
6. Pass speaking order (reasoned order) of dismissal

If enquiry is not conducted fairly: Dismissal is illegal. Worker can claim reinstatement + back wages.

MISCONDUCT WARRANTING DISMISSAL:
- Theft, fraud, dishonesty
- Willful damage to employer's property
- Assault on fellow worker or supervisor
- Habitual absence or habitual late coming after warning
- Habitual neglect of duty
- Strikes in violation of notice period

DISPUTE RESOLUTION MACHINERY:
1. Conciliation: Labour Conciliation Officer (free) tries to reach settlement. Most disputes settled here.
2. Labour Court: For individual rights disputes (wrongful termination, wages)
3. Industrial Tribunal: For collective interest disputes (wages, service conditions, hours)
4. National Industrial Tribunal: National importance disputes
5. Arbitration: Both parties can refer dispute to mutually agreed arbitrator

RELIEF AVAILABLE:
- Reinstatement with full back wages
- Compensation in lieu of reinstatement
- Payment of dues (retrenchment compensation, notice pay, gratuity)

TIME LIMIT: Claim must be raised within 3 years of the cause of dispute.

HOW TO FILE COMPLAINT:
1. File complaint with Regional Labour Commissioner / Deputy Labour Commissioner
2. Government refers dispute to Labour Court if conciliation fails
3. Worker can directly approach Labour Court for certain disputes (wrongful dismissal)

TRADE UNIONS ACT 1926:
Workers have the right to form and join trade unions. Minimum 7 workers can register a trade union with Registrar of Trade Unions. Registered unions have legal status, can own property, enter into collective bargaining agreements.

RIGHT TO STRIKE:
Strike is a collective stoppage of work. Legal conditions:
- Give 14 days' notice before strike (42 days in public utility services like transport, power, health)
- Strike during pendency of conciliation/tribunal proceedings is illegal
- Illegal strike: Workers forfeit wages and can be dismissed

PAYMENT OF BONUS ACT 1965:
Every employer must pay ANNUAL BONUS to employees earning up to Rs. 21,000/month (basic + DA).
- Minimum bonus: 8.33% of annual wages (irrespective of profit or loss)
- Maximum bonus: 20% of annual wages
- Payable within 8 months of closing of financial year
- Eligible after completing 30 working days in the year
