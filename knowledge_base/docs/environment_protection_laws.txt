ENVIRONMENT PROTECTION LAWS IN INDIA
(Environment Protection Act 1986, Air Act 1981, Water Act 1974, Wildlife Protection Act 1972)

============================
ENVIRONMENT PROTECTION ACT 1986 (EPA)
============================

The EPA is the umbrella legislation for environmental protection. The Central Government has sweeping powers to take measures to protect and improve the environment.

KEY PROVISIONS:

Section 7 — No person shall discharge any environmental pollutant in excess of prescribed standards.

Section 8 — Hazardous substances must be handled according to prescribed procedures.

Section 15 — PENALTY: Any violation of EPA provisions: Imprisonment up to 5 years and/or fine up to Rs. 1 lakh. For continuing violation: additional fine of Rs. 5,000 per day. If violation continues for 1 year after conviction: imprisonment up to 7 years.

Section 19 — Citizen can file complaint in court for violation of EPA after giving 60 days notice to the Central Government/authorities.

ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACT ASSESSMENT (EIA):
Under EPA, certain industries, projects, and activities REQUIRE PRIOR ENVIRONMENTAL CLEARANCE (EC) from Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change (MoEFCC) or State Environment Impact Assessment Authority (SEIAA).
Projects requiring EC include: Mining, thermal power plants, cement plants, large construction projects, airports, highways, coastal development.
Construction without EC: Illegal, subject to demolition and penalty.
ANYONE can challenge an EC before National Green Tribunal (NGT).

NATIONAL GREEN TRIBUNAL (NGT ACT 2010):
NGT is a specialized court for environmental cases. ANY person can file an application before NGT for:
- Substantial questions of environment
- Enforcement of legal rights related to environment
- Relief and compensation for damage to persons and property due to environmental damage
Filing fee: Very nominal (Rs. 30 for individuals)
NGT can award compensation, restore environment, impose penalties on polluters.
NGT has 5 benches: New Delhi (Principal), Bhopal, Pune, Kolkata, Chennai.

============================
AIR (PREVENTION AND CONTROL OF POLLUTION) ACT 1981
============================

State Pollution Control Boards (SPCB) regulate air pollution.

Consent to Establish and Consent to Operate: Industries must obtain consent from SPCB before establishing and operating. Industries without consent are illegal.

Standards: SPCB sets standards for emission of air pollutants from industrial plants, vehicles, and other sources.

Common Air Pollution Complaints:
- Industrial emissions causing respiratory problems
- Burning of waste/crops (stubble burning)
- Vehicular pollution
- Dust from construction

WHERE TO COMPLAIN:
1. State Pollution Control Board (SPCB) in your state
2. Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB): cpcb.nic.in
3. National Green Tribunal (NGT)
4. Green App: Some states have mobile apps for pollution complaints

VEHICLE EMISSIONS:
Vehicles must have valid Pollution Under Control (PUC) certificate. Driving without valid PUC: Fine up to Rs. 10,000.

============================
WATER (PREVENTION AND CONTROL OF POLLUTION) ACT 1974
============================

Industries cannot discharge effluents into water bodies without consent of SPCB. Standards for discharge are fixed.

PENALTY: Imprisonment 1.5 to 6 years and fine for causing water pollution.

NOISE POLLUTION (Regulation and Control) Rules 2000:
Day time (6 AM–10 PM): Industrial: 75 dB; Commercial: 65 dB; Residential: 55 dB; Silence zone: 50 dB
Night time (10 PM–6 AM): Residential: 45 dB; Silence zone: 40 dB
Loudspeakers at night require permission from authority. Complaint to police, local authority, or PCB.

============================
WILDLIFE PROTECTION ACT 1972 (Amended 2022)
============================

PROTECTED SPECIES: Hunting, capturing, poisoning, trapping of any wild animal listed in Schedules I, II, III, IV of the Act is prohibited.
Schedule I: Highest protection — tiger, lion, elephant, rhino, snow leopard, wolf
Penalty for hunting Schedule I animal: Imprisonment 3–7 years and fine Rs. 10,000–25,000.
Repeat offence: Imprisonment 3–7 years (mandatory) and fine Rs. 25,000.

PROTECTED AREAS:
- National Parks: No human activity permitted. No grazing, no settlement rights.
- Wildlife Sanctuaries: Limited human activity permitted.
- Tiger Reserves: Under Project Tiger — highest level of protection.

IVORY, SKINS, HORNS: Possession, sale, transport of any animal body part of protected species is illegal. Possession of ivory: Imprisonment up to 7 years.

TRADE IN WILDLIFE: Any trade in any wild animal or part thereof is prohibited. Report illegal wildlife trade to Wildlife Crime Control Bureau (WCCB): wildlifecrime.gov.in or call 9013010606.

FOREST CONSERVATION ACT 1980:
No forest land can be diverted for non-forest purposes (mining, industry, highway) without Central Government approval. State government cannot divert forest land without this approval.
Illegal diversion: NGT can order restoration.

COMPENSATORY AFFORESTATION: For every hectare of forest diverted, compensatory afforestation must be done on equivalent land.

FOREST RIGHTS ACT 2006:
Scheduled Tribes and Other Traditional Forest Dwellers who have been living in forests for 75 years have legal rights to:
- Cultivate forest land (up to 4 hectares) they have been cultivating
- Minor forest produce (tendu leaves, fruits, herbs)
- Grazing and fishing rights
- Community forest management rights
File claims before Gram Sabha → Sub-Divisional Level Committee → District Level Committee.
