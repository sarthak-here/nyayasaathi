ELECTRICITY ACT 2003 — CONSUMER RIGHTS AND REMEDIES

The Electricity Act 2003 governs generation, transmission, distribution, and trading of electricity in India.

============================
YOUR RIGHTS AS AN ELECTRICITY CONSUMER
============================

RIGHT TO NEW CONNECTION:
Every consumer has the right to a new electricity connection within:
- 7 days in urban areas (LT connection — low tension, domestic/commercial)
- 30 days in rural areas
Distributor CANNOT refuse connection without valid reason. If refused or delayed: file complaint with State Electricity Regulatory Commission (SERC).

RIGHT TO METERED SUPPLY:
Every consumer has the right to metered supply. Charging flat rates (unmetered) without agreement is irregular.
Smart meters are being deployed — you cannot be compelled to bear cost of smart meter (it is DISCOm's asset).

BILLING RIGHTS:
1. Bill must be based on actual meter reading, not estimated reading (except in cases of inaccessibility)
2. If estimated bill is raised: it must be reconciled in the next bill on actual reading
3. Bill must be delivered at least 7 days before payment due date
4. Consumer can inspect meter reading and request re-testing of meter
5. Disputed bill: You can pay the undisputed portion while the dispute is pending — DISCOm CANNOT disconnect supply for the disputed amount

METER TESTING:
If you suspect the meter is faulty (running fast):
1. Request meter testing from DISCOM in writing
2. DISCOM must test within 15 days
3. If found defective: Bill recalculated and excess charged amount refunded
4. You can also apply for testing before Electrical Inspector (government officer)

SUPPLY QUALITY:
You are entitled to supply at the declared voltage (230V for single phase, 415V for three phase) within +/- 6% variation.
Frequent low-voltage, high-voltage, or power cuts: Complaint to DISCOM + SERC.
Compensation for damage to appliances due to voltage fluctuation: File complaint with DISCOM and Consumer Forum.

DISCONNECTION — RULES:
DISCOM can disconnect supply only:
- For non-payment of electricity bills after due notice of 15 days
- For theft of electricity
- For dangerous conditions
- For breach of supply conditions

CANNOT disconnect:
- During night hours (5 PM to 7 AM) — Rule under many State Codes
- For disputed amount while dispute is pending
- Without giving proper notice
- For outstanding dues of previous occupant (new occupant is not liable)

RECONNECTION: After disconnection for non-payment, DISCOM must reconnect within 24 hours of payment of dues + reconnection charges.

THEFT OF ELECTRICITY (Section 135, 138 Electricity Act):
If electricity theft is detected:
Assessment of electrical units consumed illegally + penalty (typically 2x the tariff for assessed units).
Imprisonment up to 3 years and fine up to Rs. 10 lakh.
If you are falsely accused of theft: Contest the assessment before the Special Court (Electricity).

LOAD SHEDDING / OUTAGES:
Planned outages must be notified in advance (at least 24 hours in most states).
Unplanned outages compensation: Some states have compensation regulations — check your state SERC tariff order.

============================
TARIFF AND BILLING DISPUTES
============================

EXCESS BILLING:
If you receive a hugely inflated bill (say 10x normal):
1. First check if meter is tampered or has been read correctly
2. Request physical re-inspection of meter
3. Pay only the average of last 6 months' bills as undisputed amount
4. File formal grievance with DISCOM grievance cell
5. Approach Assistant Engineer / Executive Engineer of DISCOM
6. File complaint with State Electricity Ombudsman (most states have this)
7. File complaint with Consumer Forum

SOLAR NET METERING:
If you install rooftop solar panels, you are entitled to net metering (feed excess power to grid and get credit/payment). DISCOM CANNOT unreasonably refuse to install net meter or connect solar system.

============================
WHERE TO COMPLAIN
============================

DISCOM Grievance:
1. Sub-Division office → Division office → Circle office
2. Online portal of your state DISCOM (BESCOM, MSEDCL, TPDDL, etc.)
3. Call centre of DISCOM

State Electricity Ombudsman:
If DISCOM does not resolve within 30 days, approach State Electricity Ombudsman. The Ombudsman is quasi-judicial and orders are binding. Free process.

State Electricity Regulatory Commission (SERC):
File complaint for regulatory violations. Each state has its SERC (e.g., MERC for Maharashtra, DERC for Delhi, KERC for Karnataka).

Consumer Forum:
For compensation for deficient service or unfair trade practice by electricity company. File in appropriate Consumer Forum based on compensation claimed.

ELECTRICITY SUPPLY CODE:
Each state has an Electricity Supply Code / Distribution Code that specifies detailed rights of consumers. Available on SERC website. Know your state's code.

NATIONAL ELECTRICITY OMBUDSMAN: For inter-state disputes.

CENTRAL ELECTRICITY REGULATORY COMMISSION (CERC): For inter-state transmission and other regulatory matters. cerc.gov.in.
