DIGITAL PERSONAL DATA PROTECTION ACT 2023 (DPDP ACT)

India's first comprehensive data protection law. Governs the processing of digital personal data of Indian citizens (data principals) by data fiduciaries (companies, organisations).

============================
YOUR RIGHTS AS A DATA PRINCIPAL
============================

DATA PRINCIPAL: Any individual whose personal data is being processed.
DATA FIDUCIARY: The entity (company, organisation) that determines the purpose and means of processing your data.

RIGHT TO ACCESS INFORMATION (Section 11):
You have the right to know:
- What personal data the fiduciary holds about you
- A summary of processing activities related to your data
- Identity of all other fiduciaries and processors to whom your data has been disclosed

RIGHT TO CORRECTION AND ERASURE (Section 12):
You have the right to:
- Correct inaccurate or misleading personal data
- Complete incomplete personal data
- Update your personal data
- Erase personal data that is no longer necessary for the original purpose

CONSENT (Section 6):
Your personal data can only be processed with your FREE, SPECIFIC, INFORMED, UNCONDITIONAL, and UNAMBIGUOUS consent (expressed through a clear affirmative action).
Consent must be obtained separately for each processing purpose — bundling consent is not allowed.
You have the RIGHT TO WITHDRAW consent at any time. Withdrawal must be as easy as giving consent.
The fiduciary must stop processing within a reasonable time after consent is withdrawn.

LEGITIMATE USES (without consent):
- State functions (government services, public interest)
- Medical emergencies and public health
- Legal proceedings
- Employer processing employee data for employment purposes
- Mergers, acquisitions, safety purposes

RIGHT TO GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL (Section 13):
Every data fiduciary must provide a grievance redressal mechanism. Grievance must be addressed within a reasonable period (to be prescribed by rules).

RIGHT TO NOMINATE (Section 14):
You can nominate any individual to exercise your data rights on your death or incapacity.

============================
OBLIGATIONS OF DATA FIDUCIARIES
============================

DATA MINIMISATION: Collect only the personal data necessary for the specified purpose.

PURPOSE LIMITATION: Use personal data only for the specified purpose for which consent was obtained.

STORAGE LIMITATION: Do not retain personal data beyond the period necessary for the stated purpose.

SECURITY SAFEGUARDS: Implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to prevent personal data breach.

BREACH NOTIFICATION: In case of personal data breach, notify:
1. Data Protection Board of India (DPBI)
2. Each affected data principal
within a specified period (to be prescribed in rules).

SIGNIFICANT DATA FIDUCIARIES:
Large platforms handling massive data volumes, children's data, national security data — designated as Significant Data Fiduciaries with additional obligations (Data Protection Officer, Data Protection Impact Assessment, Data Audits).

============================
CHILDREN'S DATA
============================

VERIFIABLE PARENTAL CONSENT required before processing personal data of children (below 18 years).
Data fiduciaries must ensure they do not process children's data in a manner that harms children's well-being.
PROHIBITION on:
- Behavioural monitoring of children
- Targeted advertising directed at children
- Tracking of children's location

============================
DATA PROTECTION BOARD OF INDIA (DPBI)
============================

The DPBI adjudicates complaints about violations of the DPDP Act.
Any data principal can file a complaint with DPBI if a data fiduciary:
- Fails to respond to their request within a reasonable time
- Processes data without consent or legitimate use
- Fails to implement security measures (resulting in breach)
- Fails to notify of breach
- Processes children's data without parental consent

PENALTIES:
Personal data breach due to inadequate safeguards: Up to Rs. 250 crore
Breach of children's data obligations: Up to Rs. 200 crore
Failure to notify breach: Up to Rs. 200 crore
Other violations: Up to Rs. 50 crore per instance; up to Rs. 500 crore for repeated violations

HOW TO COMPLAIN (once DPBI is constituted):
File complaint online with DPBI. Process similar to Consumer Forum.

============================
EXISTING PROTECTIONS (before DPDP rules notified)
============================

Right to Privacy (Article 21): Supreme Court in K.S. Puttaswamy v. Union of India (2017) declared right to privacy as a fundamental right. Any violation of privacy by state actors can be challenged in High Court/Supreme Court.

IT Act 2000 (Section 43A): Companies handling sensitive personal data must implement reasonable security practices. Wrongful disclosure of sensitive personal data: Compensation to affected person.
Sensitive personal data: Passwords, financial data (bank account/credit card), health data, sexual orientation, biometric data.

Information Technology (Reasonable Security Practices and Procedures and Sensitive Personal Data or Information) Rules 2011: Currently applicable to "body corporates" (companies). Stricter obligations for sensitive personal data.
