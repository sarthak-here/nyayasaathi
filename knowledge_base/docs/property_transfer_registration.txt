TRANSFER OF PROPERTY ACT 1882 AND REGISTRATION ACT 1908 — PROPERTY TRANSACTIONS

============================
TRANSFER OF PROPERTY ACT 1882
============================

SALE OF PROPERTY:
A "Sale" is the transfer of ownership in exchange for a price. For immovable property worth Rs. 100 or more: Sale must be by registered sale deed (Section 54 TPA). Unregistered sale deed of immovable property is not valid in law.

AGREEMENT TO SELL:
A contract to sell property in future on certain terms. Buyer can sue for specific performance if seller backs out (Specific Relief Act 1963 — Section 10). Compulsory registration of Agreement to Sell above certain amounts — varies by state.
Stamp duty must be paid on Agreement to Sell (varies by state, typically 1-5% of market value or actual consideration whichever is higher).

GIFT OF PROPERTY:
A gift of immovable property must be in writing (Gift Deed), registered, and accepted by the donee. CANNOT be revoked (except in cases of fraud, undue influence, or specific conditions).
Gift to a close relative (blood relation, spouse): Reduced stamp duty in many states.

MORTGAGE:
Security interest created on property for a loan.
Types: Simple mortgage (no possession transfer), mortgage by deposit of title deeds (equitable mortgage — for bank loans by depositing original title documents), English mortgage, usufructuary mortgage.
Mortgagor's right: Redeem the mortgage (pay back loan and get property back — no condition in deed can extinguish this right).
Mortgagee's (lender) right: Foreclose or sell property if loan not repaid.

LEASE:
Transfer of right to enjoy property for a fixed time or in perpetuity, for consideration (rent).
Lease above 11 months must be registered (otherwise treated as month-to-month tenancy).
Most residential leases are done as 11-month Leave & Licence agreements (not leases) to avoid rent control protection for tenant.

EASEMENTS:
Right to use another's property (right of way, right to light and air). Easements by prescription (long use — 20 years) are recognized. Owner cannot block an easement right once acquired.

============================
REGISTRATION ACT 1908 — WHAT MUST BE REGISTERED
============================

COMPULSORY REGISTRATION (Section 17):
These documents are VOID if not registered:
- Sale deed for immovable property worth Rs. 100 or more
- Gift deed for immovable property
- Lease deed for more than 1 year
- Documents creating rights, title or interest in immovable property

OPTIONAL REGISTRATION (Section 18):
These documents may be registered:
- Wills (highly recommended for safety)
- Agreement to sell
- Lease below 1 year
- Powers of Attorney

PROCESS FOR REGISTRATION:
1. Prepare the deed (sale deed, gift deed, etc.) on stamp paper (value as per stamp duty)
2. Pay stamp duty (state government — varies 4-10% of market value/consideration)
3. Appear before Sub-Registrar's office with both parties and 2 witnesses
4. Present original document + ID proof of all parties
5. Biometric verification (thumb impression, photo)
6. Document registered and returned with seal

STAMP DUTY:
State subject — varies significantly. E.g., Maharashtra: 6% for men, 5% for women buyers. Karnataka: 5-6.5%. Delhi: 6% for men, 4% for women. Gujarat: 4.9%.
Pay correct stamp duty — underpaid stamp duty can lead to document being impounded.
Online stamp duty payment: Available in most states (e-GRAS, SHCIL, iGR, etc.)

ENCUMBRANCE CERTIFICATE (EC):
Certificate from Sub-Registrar showing all registered transactions on a property (mortgages, liens, transfers). Apply for EC for past 30 years before buying property. This shows if property has any existing charges.

TITLE VERIFICATION:
Before buying property:
1. Check original title documents (chain of title going back 30 years)
2. Obtain Encumbrance Certificate
3. Check no court cases/litigation on the property (court records, High Court website)
4. Check land revenue records (Pahani/Jamabandi/Record of Rights — RoR) for agricultural land
5. Verify approved plan from local authority for buildings
6. Verify RERA registration for under-construction projects

============================
PROPERTY DISPUTE REMEDIES
============================

FRAUDULENT SALE (Selling same property to multiple buyers):
Criminal complaint under BNS (cheating) + civil suit for declaration of title.
Section 420 BNS (cheating): Imprisonment up to 7 years.

FORGED DOCUMENTS:
If someone has sold your property using forged documents:
1. File FIR immediately
2. File caveat in Sub-Registrar's office
3. File suit for declaration of title and cancellation of fraudulent deed in civil court

CAVEAT:
Anyone expecting property dispute can file a caveat in court so that no order is passed without hearing them. File at the relevant civil court.

LIMITATION PERIOD:
Suit for recovery of immovable property: 12 years from date of dispossession (Limitation Act 1963, Article 65).
Suit for specific performance of sale agreement: 3 years from date fixed for performance or breach.

PARTITION SUIT:
Co-owners (heirs) can file a partition suit in civil court to divide jointly-held property. Court passes preliminary decree determining shares, then final decree directing division.
