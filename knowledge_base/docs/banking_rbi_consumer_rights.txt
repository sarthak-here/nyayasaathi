BANKING CONSUMER RIGHTS IN INDIA
(Banking Regulation Act 1949, RBI Guidelines, Banking Ombudsman Scheme 2021)

============================
YOUR RIGHTS AS A BANK CUSTOMER
============================

BASIC SAVINGS BANK ACCOUNT (BSBA) / JAN DHAN:
Every individual is entitled to a Basic Savings Bank Deposit (BSBD) account at any bank branch.
BSBD Account features:
- No minimum balance requirement
- 4 withdrawals per month (ATM + branch combined) — free
- No charges for not maintaining minimum balance
- Debit card (RuPay) provided free
Banks CANNOT refuse to open a BSBD account for an eligible person.

MINIMUM BALANCE CHARGES:
If your account requires a minimum balance (non-BSBD account):
- Charges must be notified in advance (at least 30 days notice before levying new/revised charges)
- Charges must be proportionate — if balance is 25% below minimum, charge cannot be levied on full minimum
- Banks cannot arbitrarily levy charges for SMS, cheque book, ATM card — must be disclosed upfront

FREE SERVICES (RBI Mandated):
- Cash deposits at any branch: No charge up to certain limit
- NEFT and RTGS transactions: No charge (since January 2020 — bank cannot charge)
- Savings account maintenance: Up to 5 free transactions per month at own bank ATM; 3 free at other bank ATMs in metro, 5 in non-metro (beyond which Rs. 21 per transaction is the cap)

LOAN RIGHTS:
- Right to know reason for rejection of loan application
- Right to receive a written sanction letter with terms and conditions before accepting loan
- Right to pre-payment of loan without penalty (floating rate loans under RBI guidelines — no prepayment penalty for individuals)
- Right to a copy of all loan documents (loan agreement, hypothecation deed, mortgage deed)
- Right to receive NOC and release of all original property documents within 30 days of full loan repayment
- Limitation to recover loan: 3 years from date of default (Limitation Act) — but bank can still invoke SARFAESI

SARFAESI ACT (SECURITIZATION AND RECONSTRUCTION OF FINANCIAL ASSETS AND ENFORCEMENT OF SECURITY INTEREST ACT 2002):
Banks can seize and auction property without going to court for defaults.
Your rights under SARFAESI:
- Must receive 60-day demand notice before action
- Right to make representation within 30 days of demand notice
- Right to apply to Debts Recovery Tribunal (DRT) within 30 days of possession notice
- Right to demand accounts statement from bank
- Right to surplus sale proceeds after bank's dues

CREDIT SCORE:
You have the right to get 1 free credit report per year from each Credit Information Bureau (CIBIL, Experian, CRIF High Mark, Equifax).
Dispute wrong entries on CIBIL: Contact CIBIL and file dispute online. Bank must respond within 30 days.

DIGITAL BANKING FRAUD:
If unauthorized transaction occurs in your account:
- Report to bank IMMEDIATELY (same day ideally, within 3 days at most)
- File complaint at cybercrime.gov.in and call 1930 (cyber crime helpline)
Limited liability — RBI circular (Customer Protection):
- Zero liability if: You report BEFORE the transaction is discovered or same day, AND it is the bank's fault (system breach, third-party breach without your negligence)
- Maximum liability Rs. 5,000–Rs. 25,000 if: You report within 4–7 working days, depending on account type
- Full liability on customer only if: Customer's negligence caused the fraud AND reported late (after 7 working days)
BANKS CANNOT REFUSE LIABILITY if you have followed these guidelines. Bank must credit (shadow reversal) within 10 working days of reporting.

BANK LOCKER:
RBI Revised Guidelines (2022):
- Banks must give locker agreement with terms; can't impose arbitrary charges
- Bank's liability for loss of locker contents due to bank negligence/fire/theft: 100 times annual locker rent
- Bank cannot link locker allotment to any product purchase (like FD) — it is illegal
- Waiting list must be maintained transparently

NOMINATION:
Every bank account holder should nominate a nominee. Nominee receives the amount in the account on death of account holder. Banks must pay to nominee within 15 days of receiving death certificate + nomination documents.
Without nomination: Legal heir certificate/succession certificate required (delays matter).

============================
BANKING OMBUDSMAN SCHEME 2021 (Now RBI Integrated Ombudsman Scheme 2021)
============================

Free complaint resolution for bank customers.

COMPLAINTS ACCEPTED:
- Non-payment or delay in payment of cheque, draft, bill
- Non-acceptance of small coins without sufficient cause
- Non-payment or delay in payment of remittance
- Delay in credit of proceeds of cheque, bill
- Internet banking / mobile banking issues
- Debit / credit card complaints
- Pre-payment penalty charged illegally
- Failure to close account within 5 working days
- Harassment or intimidation by recovery agents
- Non-compliance with RBI fair practice code
- Wrongful charges on accounts

PROCESS:
1. First: File complaint with bank's grievance cell (written).
2. Wait 30 days. If not resolved or unsatisfied: File complaint with RBI Ombudsman.
3. File online at cms.rbi.org.in or call 14448.
4. Complaints are resolved typically within 30–45 days.
5. Ombudsman award up to Rs. 20 lakh is binding on the bank.
6. Compensation for harassment up to Rs. 1 lakh additionally.

RECOVERY AGENT HARASSMENT:
Banks/NBFCs use recovery agents. These agents CANNOT:
- Harass you or your family members
- Make false or misleading representations
- Call before 8 AM or after 7 PM
- Contact your employer or relatives without your consent (except for reference/verification)
- Use intimidating or threatening language
- Physically intimidate or harm
If any of these occur: Complain to RBI Ombudsman; file police complaint under BNS for criminal intimidation.

RBI REGULATIONS ON WILFUL DEFAULTERS:
Large borrowers who deliberately don't repay despite ability are declared "wilful defaulters" and cannot get new bank loans from any bank in India. Their names are published.
