INSURANCE LAWS IN INDIA — CONSUMER RIGHTS AND CLAIM REMEDIES
(Insurance Act 1938, IRDA Act 1999, Motor Vehicles Act 1988)

============================
YOUR RIGHTS AS AN INSURANCE POLICYHOLDER
============================

RIGHT TO INFORMATION (Principle of Utmost Good Faith — Uberrimae Fidei):
Both insurer and insured must disclose all material facts. Insurer must:
- Explain policy terms clearly
- Provide free look period
- Issue policy document within 30 days
- Give complete Key Feature Document (KFD) in simple language

FREE LOOK PERIOD:
For life insurance policies: 15 days from receipt of policy document to review and cancel if not satisfied (30 days for electronic policies and policies sold through distance marketing).
For unit-linked policies: 30 days free look period.
If cancelled during free look: Premium refunded (after deducting medical exam costs, stamp duty, proportionate risk premium).
For health policies: 15 days free look period.

NOMINATION:
Every policyholder has the right to nominate a beneficiary. Nominee receives claim amount on death of insured. INSURER CANNOT refuse to pay claim to nominee without valid legal reason.

Assignment of Policy:
Policy can be assigned (transferred) to another person or financial institution (for home loan).

============================
CLAIM SETTLEMENT — YOUR RIGHTS
============================

LIFE INSURANCE CLAIMS:
Claim must be decided within 30 days of receiving all required documents.
If claim requires investigation: Must be completed and claim paid/repudiated within 90 days of receipt of claim.
If delayed beyond 30 days: Insurer must pay interest at 2% above bank rate from due date of settlement to actual payment date.

HEALTH INSURANCE CLAIMS:
Cashless hospitalization: Insurance company must respond to cashless request from hospital within 1 hour.
Reimbursement claim: Must be settled within 30 days of receiving all documents.
Pre-authorization cannot be denied without valid reason in writing.

MOTOR INSURANCE CLAIMS:
Survey must be completed within 48 hours of intimation (total loss survey within 72 hours).
Settlement within 30 days of completion of survey and receipt of all documents.

REPUDIATION (Rejection) OF CLAIM:
If insurer rejects your claim:
- They MUST give reasons in writing
- You must receive the repudiation letter
- You have the right to challenge the repudiation

GROUNDS INSURER CAN VALIDLY REJECT CLAIM:
- Non-disclosure of material facts at time of taking policy (health conditions, habits)
- Policy lapsed (premium not paid)
- Exclusion clause clearly mentioned in policy applies
- Fraudulent claim

GROUNDS ON WHICH INSURER CANNOT REJECT:
- Technicalities not mentioned in policy
- Procedure not followed by hospital but not policyholder's fault
- Pre-existing disease exclusion not clearly explained at time of policy sale

============================
GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL
============================

STEP 1 — Internal Grievance of Insurance Company:
Every insurer must have a Grievance Redressal Officer (GRO). File complaint:
- Online on insurer's portal
- Email to compliance officer
- In writing at branch office
Insurer must acknowledge within 3 working days and resolve within 14 working days.
Register complaint on Bima Bharosa portal (IRDAI): bimabharosa.irdai.gov.in

STEP 2 — Insurance Ombudsman:
If grievance not resolved within 30 days OR if not satisfied with insurer's response:
File complaint with Insurance Ombudsman. FREE, no advocate needed.
Jurisdictions: 17 Ombudsman offices across India.
Ombudsman handles: Claims, rejection of claims, premium disputes, policy servicing issues.
No complaint to Ombudsman if amount in dispute exceeds Rs. 50 lakh.
Awards by Ombudsman are binding on insurer (if accepted by complainant within 30 days).
Contact: ioinsuranceombudsman.gov.in / 1800-42-5565

STEP 3 — IRDAI (Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India):
For systemic issues, mis-selling, unfair practices.
IRDAI Bima Bharosa: 1800-4254-732 (toll free)
Complaint portal: bimabharosa.irdai.gov.in or igms.irda.gov.in

STEP 4 — Consumer Forum:
File complaint in District Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission. Can claim deficiency of service + compensation + costs.

============================
MOTOR THIRD PARTY INSURANCE
============================

Third party insurance is MANDATORY for all vehicles under Motor Vehicles Act.
If a third party (pedestrian, other vehicle owner) is injured by your vehicle:
- Third party can claim compensation from Motor Accident Claims Tribunal (MACT)
- Your insurance company pays the claim (no cap for bodily injury/death; Rs. 7.5 lakh cap for property damage)
- You CANNOT be held personally liable for third-party death/injury beyond insurance

If your vehicle is uninsured and causes accident: You are personally liable for all compensation.

MACT CLAIM (if you are the victim of an accident):
File claim at MACT (Motor Accident Claims Tribunal — constituted under MVA at District Court level).
No time limit technically, but file as soon as possible.
Compensation is calculated using Sarla Verma formula (income × multiplier) for loss of income.
Structured Formula Compensation also available for simplified quick awards.

PM Suraksha Bima Yojana: Rs. 12/year premium — Rs. 2 lakh accidental death/disability cover for bank account holders.
PM Jeevan Jyoti Bima Yojana: Rs. 436/year — Rs. 2 lakh life cover for bank account holders.

============================
LIFE INSURANCE — KEY TYPES
============================

Term Insurance: Pure risk cover. No maturity benefit. Cheapest premium for maximum cover.
Endowment: Risk cover + savings. Maturity benefit even if no claim.
ULIP (Unit Linked Insurance Plan): Risk cover + market-linked investment. Subject to market risk.
Annuity/Pension Plans: Regular income after retirement.

IMPORTANT: NEVER stop paying premium without understanding surrender value implications. Lapsing a life insurance policy before completing 2–3 years loses most premium paid.

AGENT COMMISSION DISCLOSURE: Agent must disclose commission payable on products sold. Mis-selling of insurance products is a violation — report to IRDAI.
