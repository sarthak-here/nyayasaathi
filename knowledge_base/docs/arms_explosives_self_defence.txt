ARMS ACT 1959 AND EXPLOSIVES ACT 1884 — WEAPONS AND LICENCES

============================
ARMS ACT 1959 (Amended 2019)
============================

WHAT REQUIRES A LICENCE:
Any firearm (pistol, revolver, rifle, shotgun) requires an Arms Licence under the Arms Act.
Air guns (below .177 bore), certain antique firearms, and specified equipment are exempt.
Permitted weapons without licence: Traditional hunting weapons in some northeastern states under tribal custom.

WHO CAN GET A LICENCE:
- Indian citizen above 21 years
- No criminal record
- No mental disorder
- Application must show need (self-defence, crop protection, etc.)
- Character verification by police
- Physical fitness
- Licence granted by District Magistrate (DM)

TYPES OF LICENCES:
- Clause (a): Possession and carrying (self-defence)
- Clause (b): Possession at home only
- Clause (c): Manufacture / sale / dealing

MAXIMUM ARMS ALLOWED:
Post-2019 Amendment — major restriction:
- Civilians (non-licensed dealers): Maximum 2 licensed firearms (reduced from 3).
- One gun per licence.
- No person can hold more than 2 arms licences.
Exception: Farmers in notified states may hold additional shotgun/rifle for crop protection.

LICENCE RENEWAL: Every 3 years. Failure to renew is an offence.

STORAGE: Licensed firearms must be stored safely when not in use. A locked cabinet is recommended. Negligent storage causing accident: Offence.

CARRYING FIREARMS:
You can carry a licensed firearm in public only for legitimate purposes (self-defence travel, going to shooting range, etc.). Cannot brandish or display in public to create fear.

TRANSPORTATION:
- Firearms must be unloaded in a closed bag while transporting in vehicle or public place.
- Inform airline (checked baggage — special rules apply), railways (permitted with licence).

UNLICENSED FIREARMS:
Possession of unlicensed firearm: Imprisonment 7 to 14 years (post-2019 Amendment — significantly enhanced).
Manufacturing/selling unlicensed: 10 years to life.
Using firearm to commit offence: Mandatory minimum 7 years.

CANCELLATION OF LICENCE:
DM can cancel licence if:
- Person becomes convicted criminal
- Unsound mind
- National security/public safety concern
- Licence obtained by fraud
- Failure to renew
- Violation of conditions

Appeal against cancellation: Board of Revenue / State Government.

PROHIBITED ARMS:
Fully automatic weapons, sub-machine guns, burst-fire weapons — strictly prohibited for civilians. Any possession: 7 years to life imprisonment.

============================
RIGHT OF PRIVATE DEFENCE WITH WEAPONS
============================

BNS Sections 34–44 allow use of even deadly force (firearm) in exercise of right of private defence if:
- There is reasonable apprehension of death or grievous hurt to you or another
- The apprehension is based on the conduct of the aggressor
- Force used is no more than necessary
LIMITATION: Cannot use deadly force for protection of property alone. Death caused only to protect life.

If you use a licensed firearm in genuine self-defence:
1. Immediately call police (100 or 112)
2. Do not flee the scene
3. Cooperate with police investigation
4. Exercise right to silence regarding what happened until lawyer arrives
5. The fact that you had a licensed firearm and acted in self-defence is relevant to your defence

============================
EXPLOSIVES ACT 1884 AND EXPLOSIVE SUBSTANCES ACT 1908
============================

Manufacture, possession, use, sale of explosive substances requires licence from Chief Inspector of Explosives (Petroleum and Explosives Safety Organisation — PESO).

Legal uses: Mining, construction, demolition, fireworks manufacturing.

Illegal explosives: Possession without licence — imprisonment up to 10 years. Use to cause explosion endangering life — imprisonment up to life or death.

FIREWORKS:
Only licensed manufacturers can produce fireworks. Retailers need trade licence.
Display of fireworks requires permission from District Magistrate.
Green crackers only (reduced emission) allowed in many cities.
No firecracker display: Between 10 PM to 6 AM (Supreme Court order).
Complaint against illegal fireworks: Police, District Magistrate, Pollution Control Board.

============================
IMITATION FIREARMS AND TOY GUNS
============================

Realistic-looking toy guns and imitation firearms are regulated. Selling toy guns that closely resemble real firearms without clear marking: Can be seized. Using a toy gun to commit robbery/threat: Treated as using a real firearm under BNS.
