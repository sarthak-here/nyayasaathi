BHARATIYA NYAYA SANHITA (BNS) 2023 — GENERAL CRIMINAL LAW
(Replaces Indian Penal Code 1860 from July 1, 2024)

The BNS is the primary criminal law of India covering offences against the body, property, public order, and the state.

OFFENCES AGAINST THE HUMAN BODY

Section 100 BNS — Culpable Homicide (not amounting to murder)
Causing death with intention to cause death or grievous hurt, but without premeditation. Punishment: Imprisonment up to life, or up to 10 years, and fine.

Section 101 BNS — Murder
Culpable homicide is murder if done with intention to cause death, or with knowledge that the act will likely cause death. Punishment: Death or life imprisonment, and fine.
Exception: Grave and sudden provocation reduces murder to culpable homicide (not murder).

Section 115 BNS — Grievous Hurt
Causing fracture or dislocation of bone, permanent damage to sight/hearing, permanent disfigurement, or endangering life. Punishment: Up to 7 years imprisonment and fine.

Section 118 BNS — Voluntarily Causing Hurt
Causing bodily pain, disease, or infirmity. Punishment: Up to 1 year or fine up to Rs. 10,000 or both.

Section 126 BNS — Wrongful Restraint
Preventing a person from proceeding in any direction they have a right to go. Punishment: Up to 1 month, or fine up to Rs. 5,000.

Section 127 BNS — Wrongful Confinement
Restraining a person to a bounded area. Punishment: Up to 1 year, or fine up to Rs. 5,000. If for 3 or more days: up to 2 years.

Section 131 BNS — Assault
Making any gesture or preparation causing apprehension of criminal force being used. Punishment: Up to 3 months or fine up to Rs. 5,000.

Section 140 BNS — Criminal Force and Assault to Outrage Modesty
Assault or criminal force to any person, intending to outrage or knowing it likely to outrage the modesty of that person. Punishment: Up to 2 years, or fine, or both.

OFFENCES AGAINST PROPERTY

Section 303 BNS — Theft
Dishonestly taking any movable property out of the possession of any person without consent. Punishment: Up to 3 years, or fine, or both.
Theft of goods worth over Rs. 5 lakh: up to 7 years.

Section 304 BNS — Robbery (Theft with force or threat)
When committing theft, the offender causes or attempts death, hurt, or wrongful restraint. Punishment: Up to 10 years and fine. If armed: up to life.

Section 308 BNS — Dacoity (Robbery by 5 or more persons)
Punishment: Up to life or death in cases of murder during dacoity.

Section 316 BNS — Cheating
Dishonestly inducing a person to deliver property or to do something they would not otherwise do. Punishment: Up to 3 years, or fine, or both.
Cheating and dishonestly inducing delivery of property: up to 7 years and fine.

Section 318 BNS — Cheating by Impersonation
Pretending to be someone else to deceive. Punishment: Up to 3 years and fine.

Section 324 BNS — Mischief
Damaging or destroying property. Punishment: Up to 3 months or fine.
If value exceeds Rs. 20 lakh: up to 5 years.

Section 329 BNS — Criminal Trespass
Entering property to commit an offence or to intimidate, insult, or annoy. Punishment: Up to 3 months or fine up to Rs. 5,000 or both.

Section 331 BNS — House Trespass
Criminal trespass in a building, tent, or vessel used as human dwelling. Punishment: Up to 1 year or fine up to Rs. 10,000.

FRAUD AND DOCUMENT OFFENCES

Section 336 BNS — Forgery
Making a false document with intent to cause damage or injury. Punishment: Up to 2 years and fine.
Forgery of court records/public registers: up to 7 years.

Section 340 BNS — Using Forged Document
Using as genuine a document known to be forged. Same punishment as forgery.

ORGANISED CRIME AND TERRORISM (New in BNS)

Section 111 BNS — Organised Crime
Continuing unlawful activity by a syndicate — kidnapping, extortion, land grabbing, contract killing. Punishment: 5 years to life; death if causes death.

Section 113 BNS — Terrorism
Acts causing death, danger to property, threat to national security. Punishment: Death or life imprisonment.

SEDITION REPLACED

Section 152 BNS replaces Section 124A IPC (Sedition). It criminalises acts that endanger sovereignty, unity, and integrity of India. Pure criticism of government is NOT sedition.

ABETMENT AND ATTEMPT

Section 45 BNS — Abetment
Instigating, conspiring, or aiding in commission of an offence. Punishment: Same as the offence abetted.

Section 62 BNS — Attempt
Attempting to commit an offence and failing. Punishment: Half the maximum punishment for the completed offence.

RIGHT OF PRIVATE DEFENCE (Sections 34–44 BNS)
Every person has the right to defend their body and property against any offence. The right extends to causing death only when facing:
- Assault reasonably causing death or grievous hurt
- Rape or kidnapping
- Wrongful confinement
- Acid attack
The right does not extend beyond what is necessary to repel the attack.

HOW TO FILE A COMPLAINT UNDER BNS
1. Go to the nearest police station and file an FIR (First Information Report)
2. If police refuse to register FIR: send complaint by post to SP (Superintendent of Police)
3. If still no action: file complaint under Section 175(3) BNSS before a Magistrate
4. Magistrate can direct police to register FIR
5. Zero FIR: Can be registered at ANY police station regardless of jurisdiction

COGNIZABLE vs NON-COGNIZABLE OFFENCES
- Cognizable (police can arrest without warrant): murder, robbery, rape, theft above certain value
- Non-cognizable (police need Magistrate's permission to arrest): assault, defamation, cheating below threshold
