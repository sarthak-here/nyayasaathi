SENIOR CITIZEN RIGHTS — MAINTENANCE AND WELFARE ACT 2007
=========================================================

OVERVIEW
The Maintenance and Welfare of Parents and Senior Citizens Act 2007 protects the rights of parents and senior citizens (60 years and above) to be maintained by their children, to be protected from abuse, and to reclaim property transferred to negligent children.

WHO IS A SENIOR CITIZEN?
Any person who is 60 years of age or above and is a citizen of India.

RIGHT TO MAINTENANCE FROM CHILDREN — SECTION 4

Parents or grandparents who cannot maintain themselves from their own earnings or property can claim monthly maintenance from:
- Adult children (sons and daughters equally).
- Grandchildren if children are dead or incapable.
- Childless senior citizens can claim from any relative who will inherit their property.

Key points:
- Biological children AND adopted children are equally obligated.
- Married daughters are equally responsible as sons.
- Children cannot say "I don't earn enough" if they have any income.

HOW TO FILE — MAINTENANCE TRIBUNAL (SECTION 5)

Step 1: File application to the Maintenance Tribunal. The Maintenance Tribunal is typically the Sub-Divisional Magistrate (SDM) or Executive Magistrate designated by state government.
Step 2: Application can be made personally OR through Maintenance Officer or NGO.
Step 3: Case must be disposed of within 90 days (extendable by 30 days).
Step 4: Tribunal can pass interim maintenance order during proceedings.

AMOUNT: Maximum Rs. 10,000 per month per parent (from each child, as court determines fair share).
Note: If child's income is very high, courts in some states have gone beyond this cap under other legal provisions.

ENFORCEMENT: If child refuses to pay, Maintenance Officer can recover as arrears of land revenue. Alternatively, warrant can be issued.

PROTECTION OFFICER: State government appoints Maintenance Officers in each district. Senior citizens can approach them directly for free help in filing application.

PROPERTY TRANSFER RECLAIM — SECTION 23

If a senior citizen transferred property (house, land, etc.) to children or relatives on the condition that they will be taken care of — and the children fail to provide that care — the senior citizen can get the transfer DECLARED VOID by the Maintenance Tribunal.

The transfer will be cancelled and property returned to the senior citizen.
No need to prove fraud — just prove the transfer was made with expectation of care and care was not provided.

ABANDONMENT OF SENIOR CITIZEN — SECTION 24

Whoever abandons a senior citizen (who is incapable of self-protection):
Up to 3 months imprisonment OR Rs. 5,000 fine, OR both.
File complaint at police station or before the Tribunal.

PROTECTION FROM ABUSE — SECTION 22

State governments must establish:
- Old Age Homes (at least one per district with 150-bed capacity).
- Adequate protection for senior citizens through police.
- Senior Citizen Protection Cells in police stations.

If a senior citizen is being physically/mentally abused by family or others:
1. File complaint at police station (BNS Section 115/351 for hurt/threats).
2. Also file with Maintenance Tribunal (abandonment under Section 24).
3. Contact Protection Officer.
4. Elder Line: 14567 (Toll free, 8 AM to 8 PM, national helpline for senior citizens).

SPECIAL COURTS AND FACILITIES

Cases under this Act must be tried as speedily as possible (target: 90 days).
Senior citizens get priority hearing in many courts.
Senior citizens above 75 years or with serious illness: Exemption from personal court appearance (can file affidavit instead).

ELDER ABUSE — WHAT IT INCLUDES
Physical abuse: Hitting, slapping, restraining.
Emotional/psychological abuse: Threats, humiliation, ignoring, isolating from friends/family.
Financial abuse: Stealing money/property, forcing to sign documents, not giving pension/savings.
Neglect: Not providing food, medicine, clothing, medical care.
All forms of elder abuse can be reported to police (FIR) + Tribunal (for maintenance/property relief) + Elder Line 14567.

GOVERNMENT SCHEMES FOR SENIOR CITIZENS
Indira Gandhi National Old Age Pension Scheme (IGNOAPS): Rs. 200-500/month for BPL senior citizens. Apply at block/panchayat office.
Pradhan Mantri Vaya Vandana Yojana: Pension scheme through LIC.
Senior Citizen Savings Scheme (SCSS): Higher interest rate at post offices/banks.
Ayushman Bharat: Health insurance up to Rs. 5 lakh per year — senior citizens are covered.
Free legal aid: DLSA provides free lawyers to all senior citizens regardless of income.

HELPLINES
- Elder Line (National Helpline for Senior Citizens): 14567
- Women Helpline (for elderly women being abused): 181
- Police Emergency: 112
- National Legal Services Authority: 15100

==========================================================================
IF YOU ARE THE CHILD / RELATIVE FACING A MAINTENANCE CLAIM — DEFENSE RIGHTS
==========================================================================

YOUR RIGHTS AS RESPONDENT:
- Right to file a written reply before the Maintenance Tribunal.
- Right to produce evidence of your income and financial capacity.
- Right to show that the senior citizen has sufficient means of their own.
- Right to appeal any order to the Sessions Court within 60 days.

WHAT THE SENIOR CITIZEN MUST PROVE:
- That they are unable to maintain themselves from their own property or income.
- That you have sufficient means to pay maintenance.
If the parent has substantial property, pension, or savings, they may not qualify for maintenance under this Act.

COMMON DEFENSES:
Parent has sufficient independent income: If the parent has pension, rental income, savings, or property, maintenance claim may fail or be reduced.
You genuinely cannot afford to pay: Show your income, dependants, and expenses to demonstrate inability.
Property was transferred voluntarily, not as condition for care: For Section 23 (reclaiming transferred property) cases, show the transfer was an unconditional gift — not conditioned on providing care.
Multiple children: If there are multiple children, maintenance burden should be shared — ask the Tribunal to apportion.

IMPORTANT NOTE ON MAXIMUM AMOUNT:
The Act caps maintenance at Rs. 10,000 per month per parent. However, this is a cap on the Tribunal's jurisdiction — the total amount cannot exceed this unless parent seeks remedy under another law (e.g., Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act for higher amounts).
