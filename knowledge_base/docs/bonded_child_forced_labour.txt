BONDED LABOUR SYSTEM (ABOLITION) ACT 1976 AND HUMAN TRAFFICKING LAWS

============================
BONDED LABOUR SYSTEM (ABOLITION) ACT 1976
============================

BONDED LABOUR DEFINED:
A system under which a debtor (or ancestor/family member) enters into an agreement with creditor to render service to the creditor (or nominee) for a specified or unspecified period, for no wages or nominal wages, to repay a debt/advance.

ABOLITION:
Section 4: Every obligation to render bonded labour is extinguished.
Section 6: Every bonded labourer is freed from his obligation to repay the bonded debt.
Section 7: Property of bonded labourer that was forfeited/given as security stands restored.
All bonded debts are VOID — no court will enforce them.

IN SIMPLE WORDS: If you have been given an advance and forced to work to repay it, THE DEBT IS VOID. You cannot be forced to work for it. You are free.

PUNISHMENT:
Person who maintains bonded labour system: Imprisonment up to 3 years + fine.
Government official who fails to act on complaint: Disciplinary action.

VIGILANCE COMMITTEES:
Every district must have a District Vigilance Committee to identify and release bonded labourers.

HOW TO GET RELEASED:
1. File complaint with District Magistrate (DM) / Sub-Divisional Magistrate (SDM)
2. Contact District Legal Services Authority (DLSA) — free legal help
3. Contact NGOs working on bonded labour — they can approach court on your behalf
4. DM must release you IMMEDIATELY and initiate rehabilitation

REHABILITATION:
Released bonded labourers are entitled to:
- Immediate temporary accommodation
- Subsistence allowance
- Economic rehabilitation assistance (Central Government scheme: Rs. 1-3 lakh per released bonded labourer under Central Sector Scheme for Rehabilitation of Bonded Labourers)
- Training for livelihood

NATIONAL HELPLINE FOR BONDED/CHILD LABOUR: 14434 (Ministry of Labour)

============================
HUMAN TRAFFICKING (BNS SECTIONS 143-146)
============================

TRAFFICKING IN PERSONS:
Recruiting, transporting, harbouring, transferring, or receiving persons by force/fraud/coercion/deception for purposes of exploitation (sexual exploitation, forced labour, organ harvesting, begging, any other form).

PUNISHMENT:
Basic trafficking: 7 to 10 years + fine
Trafficking of minor (child): 10 to 14 years + fine
Trafficking of more than one person / multiple occasions: 14 years to life + fine
Trafficking for sexual exploitation: 10 to 14 years + fine
Trafficking of a child below 12 years: 14 years to life / death + fine
Trafficking by public servant: 14 years to life + fine
If the person trafficked dies or is subject to grievous hurt: Up to life imprisonment

EXPLOITATION:
Any person who maintains a trafficked person in exploitative conditions (forced labour, commercial sexual exploitation) is ALSO guilty — same punishment as trafficker.

NATIONAL CRIME RECORDS BUREAU (NCRB): Tracks trafficking cases in India.

ANTI-TRAFFICKING CELL:
Every state must have an Anti-Human Trafficking Unit (AHTU) within police — specialized training for investigation.
National Human Rights Commission (NHRC) monitors anti-trafficking measures.

============================
IMMORAL TRAFFIC (PREVENTION) ACT 1956 (ITPA)
============================

While sex work itself is not criminalised (Supreme Court in Budhadev Karmaskar v. State of West Bengal, 2022 recognised sex workers' rights), the following are illegal:

- Running or owning a brothel
- Pimping (living on earnings of a sex worker)
- Soliciting in or near public places
- Trafficked/underage persons being used in sex work

Sex workers have the right to:
- Not be arrested for consensual adult sex work
- Be rescued from trafficked/coerced situations
- Not be harassed by police
- Access medical services, social security

Child sex tourism and POSCO Act offences are dealt with strictly — full POCSO provisions apply.

============================
ORGAN TRAFFICKING
============================

TRANSPLANTATION OF HUMAN ORGANS AND TISSUES ACT 1994 (THOTA):
Commercial dealing in human organs is strictly prohibited.
Selling or buying a human organ: Imprisonment 3 to 10 years + fine up to Rs. 20 lakh.
Removal of organ without consent: Imprisonment up to 10 years + fine.
Hospital conducting transplant without authorisation: Cancellation of registration + imprisonment of hospital management.

Legal organ donation: Free and voluntary. Register as donor on organindia.org or National Organ and Tissue Transplant Organization (NOTTO): 1800-11-4770.
