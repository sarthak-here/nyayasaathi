CHILD LABOUR (PROHIBITION AND REGULATION) ACT 1986 (Amended 2016) AND JUVENILE JUSTICE (CARE AND PROTECTION OF CHILDREN) ACT 2015

============================
CHILD LABOUR (PROHIBITION AND REGULATION) ACT 1986, AMENDED 2016
============================

COMPLETE BAN ON CHILD LABOUR:
Children below 14 years CANNOT be employed in ANY occupation or process whatsoever (absolute ban since 2016 amendment). This includes domestic work, dhabas/restaurants, brick kilns, garages, fireworks factories, and any other establishment.

EXCEPTION — FAMILY BUSINESS: A child may work in family business/enterprise (not in hazardous occupations) but ONLY outside school hours and during holidays — and MUST NOT affect education.

ADOLESCENTS (14–18 years) — PARTIAL RESTRICTIONS:
Adolescents (14 to 18 years) CANNOT be employed in hazardous occupations and processes including:
- Mining
- Explosive and inflammable substances
- Hazardous processes under Factories Act
- Construction/demolition
- Handling of toxic substances
They CAN work in non-hazardous occupations but not during school hours.

PENALTY FOR EMPLOYING A CHILD:
First offence: Imprisonment 6 months to 2 years AND/OR fine Rs. 20,000 to Rs. 50,000
Repeat offence: Imprisonment 1 to 3 years (mandatory)
Parents/guardians who permit: Fine up to Rs. 10,000

REHABILITATION:
District Magistrate must ensure rescued child is rehabilitated. Child Welfare Fund must be established for rehabilitation. Employer must pay a rehabilitation fund for each child employed.

WHERE TO COMPLAIN:
1. File FIR at the nearest police station — police must act
2. Call Childline: 1098 (toll free, 24 hours)
3. Complain to District Collector / District Magistrate
4. Complain to Child Welfare Committee (CWC) in your district
5. State Labour Department
6. National Commission for Protection of Child Rights (NCPCR): ncpcr.gov.in
7. File complaint on PENCIL portal: pencil.gov.in (Ministry of Labour's platform for child labour complaints)

============================
JUVENILE JUSTICE (CARE AND PROTECTION OF CHILDREN) ACT 2015
============================

PURPOSE: Protection of children in need of care (abandoned, orphaned, beggars, missing, victims of trafficking/abuse) and children in conflict with law.

CHILD: Any person below 18 years of age.

CHILDREN IN NEED OF CARE AND PROTECTION (Section 2(14)):
- Abandoned, lost, or runaway child
- Child victim of abuse, exploitation, or trafficking
- Child of a mentally ill or addicted parent
- Child in moral danger
- Child of a parent undergoing imprisonment
- Child working contrary to labour laws
- Homeless child
These children are produced before the Child Welfare Committee (CWC).

CHILD WELFARE COMMITTEE (CWC):
Every district must have a CWC. The CWC is responsible for decisions about care and protection of children. It can place children in foster care, adoption, or child care institutions.

ADOPTION:
The JJ Act regulates adoption in India through CARA (Central Adoption Resource Authority).
- Abandoned and surrendered children can be declared legally free for adoption
- Indian families have priority for 60 days; after which NRI and foreign families
- All adoptions must be processed through CARA
- Intercountry adoption: Stringent process with CARA approval mandatory

FOSTER CARE:
Children placed with foster families by CWC/court order. Foster families receive monthly support. Biological parents retain legal rights during foster care period.

CHILDREN IN CONFLICT WITH LAW (CCL):
A child who has committed or is alleged to have committed an offence.

JUVENILE JUSTICE BOARD (JJB):
Every district must have a JJB. All offences by children below 18 are dealt with by JJB (not regular criminal court) EXCEPT:

SERIOUS OFFENCES BY CHILD (16–18 years):
For heinous offences (punishable with 7+ years), the JJB can conduct a preliminary assessment and may TRANSFER the case to Children's Court (Sessions Court designated as Children's Court) to be tried as an adult. Only for children aged 16–18 years.

PRINCIPLES OF JJB:
- Presumption of innocence
- Best interest of the child
- Restoration and reintegration, not punishment
- Privacy of proceedings — no media identification
- Legal aid provided by State

REHABILITATION AND SOCIAL REINTEGRATION:
JJB focuses on reformation. Punishment: Reformatory homes, community service, counselling, fine. NEVER life imprisonment or death penalty for any child.

After completing 18 years during trial: Remains in JJB jurisdiction. Not transferred to adult court simply because age crossed 18 during trial.

RIGHTS OF A CHILD IN CONFLICT WITH LAW:
1. Right to be heard and participate in proceedings
2. Right to free legal aid
3. Right not to be handcuffed or kept with adult offenders
4. Right to have parents/guardians informed
5. Right to bail as a rule (not exception) — bail is the norm, detention is the exception
6. Right to have proceedings in child-friendly manner

SPECIAL COURTS (POCSO + JJ Act):
Where POCSO Act (sexual offences against children) is the charge, Special Courts constituted under POCSO Act handle the case (with child-friendly procedures).

TRAFFICKING OF CHILDREN:
Under BNS Sections 143–146 (trafficking) and POCSO Act, trafficking of children is punishable with rigorous imprisonment up to 10 years and up to life for aggravated trafficking (child below 12 years, repeated offences).
Anti-trafficking helpline: 1800-419-8588 (Shakti Vahini)
