RIGHTS OF PERSONS WITH DISABILITIES — RPWD ACT 2016
====================================================
(Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act 2016 — replaced PWD Act 1995)

OVERVIEW
The RPWD Act 2016 is India's comprehensive law for persons with disabilities. It significantly expanded the definition of disability, created new rights, enhanced reservations, and provides for criminal penalties for atrocities against disabled persons.

WHO IS COVERED?
The Act covers 21 specified disabilities (up from 7 in old law):
Physical: Locomotor disability, leprosy cured, dwarfism, acid attack victims, muscular dystrophy.
Sensory: Blindness, low vision, deaf, hard of hearing, speech and language disability.
Intellectual/Developmental: Intellectual disability, specific learning disabilities, autism spectrum disorder.
Mental: Mental illness, multiple sclerosis, Parkinson's disease.
Blood disorders: Hemophilia, thalassemia, sickle cell disease.
Plus: Multiple disabilities and other conditions as notified.

A person must have a "Benchmark Disability" certificate (40%+ disability) for most benefits.
How to get: Certifying authority is CMO/Medical Superintendent of Government hospital.

RIGHTS UNDER RPWD ACT

Non-discrimination (Section 3):
No person can be discriminated against on the ground of disability in:
- Education (admission, examinations).
- Employment (recruitment, promotion, transfer).
- Access to public services, buildings, transport.
- Any government scheme or program.
Denial of equal opportunity without reasonable justification = discrimination.

Equality (Section 7):
State must ensure disabled persons enjoy right to equality, life with dignity, respect for integrity on equal basis with others.

Inclusive Education (Section 16):
All educational institutions must provide inclusive education with:
- Reasonable accommodation and support.
- Transport assistance.
- Scribes for exams.
- Extra time in examinations (usually 20 minutes per hour).
- Modified question papers where appropriate.
- Free education up to Class 18 for children with benchmark disabilities.

Higher Education:
5% reservation in government and government-aided universities for benchmark disability persons.
Free education in central universities for students with disability.

Employment Rights (Section 20):
4% reservation in government jobs:
- 1% for blindness and low vision.
- 1% for deaf and hard of hearing.
- 1% for locomotor disability and cerebral palsy.
- 1% for other disabilities.
Government employers CANNOT discriminate in hiring, promotion, or transfer.
Private sector employers are encouraged (not mandated) to provide 5% reservation.

Accessibility (Section 40-46):
All government buildings, transport, public spaces must be made accessible within 5 years of Act.
Every service provider must ensure websites are accessible.
All public documents must be available in accessible formats (Braille, large print, audio).

ATROCITIES AGAINST PERSONS WITH DISABILITIES — SECTION 92

Criminal offence to:
- Intentionally insult or intimidate a disabled person.
- Assault or use force against a disabled person.
- Deny a disabled person food, water, or care.
- Exploit property of a disabled person.
- Abuse or neglect a disabled person in care institution.
- Force a person with disability into begging.
- Abandon a disabled person.
- Voluntarily injure a person with disability.
Punishment: 6 months to 5 years imprisonment + fine.

Where to file: Police station + complaint to Chief Commissioner for Persons with Disabilities.

HOW TO FILE COMPLAINTS

Chief Commissioner for Persons with Disabilities (National):
Website: disabilityaffairs.gov.in
Address: New Delhi. Hears cases against central government authorities.
Complaint disposed within 60 days.

State Commissioner for Persons with Disabilities:
For all state government, private entities, educational institutions.
Available in every state. Find contact on state's Social Welfare Department website.

NCPEDP (National Centre for Promotion of Employment for Disabled People):
For workplace discrimination complaints.

BENEFITS AND SCHEMES

Disability Certificate: Issued by government hospital. Required for all benefits.
Assistive Devices: ADIP scheme — government provides free assistive devices (wheelchairs, hearing aids, crutches) through district administration.
Scholarship: National Scholarship Portal — special scholarships for disabled students.
Pension: Indira Gandhi National Disability Pension Scheme (IGNDPS) — Rs. 300/month for BPL persons with 80%+ disability.
Railway concession: 25-75% concession depending on disability and travel class.
Income Tax: Section 80U deduction of Rs. 75,000 to Rs. 1.25 lakh per year for persons with disability.

HELP AND LEGAL AID
- Disability Rights helpline: 011-23386054 (CCPD office).
- NALSA (free legal aid): 15100.
- DLSA (District Legal Services Authority): Free lawyers for all disabled persons.
- Blind Relief Association, AYJNIHH, and other specialized NGOs provide support.

IF YOU ARE ACCUSED OF DISCRIMINATION — DEFENSE RIGHTS UNDER RPWD ACT
==========================================================================

YOUR RIGHTS AS AN ACCUSED EMPLOYER / INSTITUTION:
- Right to be heard before the Commissioner passes any order.
- Right to produce evidence of reasonable accommodation already provided.
- Right to appeal any Commissioner order to the High Court.

WHAT COMPLAINANT MUST PROVE:
- That the person is a person with disability (valid disability certificate).
- That discrimination occurred specifically on the ground of disability.
- That the discrimination was without justification.

COMMON DEFENSES:
Reasonable accommodation provided: You made genuine efforts to accommodate the disability — document all steps taken (ramps installed, modified workstation, extra time in exams).
Proportionate justification: Some distinctions based on disability are justified if they serve a legitimate purpose proportionately (e.g., a visual disability may legitimately disqualify from driving-related work — not general discrimination).
No disability nexus: The adverse action was based on legitimate performance grounds, not disability.
