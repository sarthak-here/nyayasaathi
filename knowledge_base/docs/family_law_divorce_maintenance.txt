FAMILY LAW — DIVORCE, MAINTENANCE, CHILD CUSTODY
==================================================

MAINTENANCE UNDER BNSS SECTION 144 (Replaced CrPC Section 125)

WHO CAN CLAIM MAINTENANCE:
1. Wife unable to maintain herself from husband.
2. Legitimate or illegitimate children (minor, or adult if disabled) from father.
3. Illegitimate children from father.
4. Parents (father or mother) unable to maintain themselves from adult child.

WHERE TO FILE:
Judicial Magistrate First Class (JMFC) having jurisdiction.
Jurisdiction: Where respondent resides, OR where they last resided together, OR where claimant currently resides.

PROCEDURE:
Application → Notice to respondent → Reply within 30 days → Evidence → Order.
Interim maintenance must be decided within 60 days of notice (new BNSS provision).
Final order must be reasonably prompt.

ENFORCEMENT:
If maintenance is not paid, the Magistrate can:
- Issue warrant for recovery of money.
- In default, sentence respondent to imprisonment up to 1 month per month of arrears.
- Attach salary or property of defaulter.

DIVORCE — HINDU MARRIAGE ACT 1955 (For Hindus, Buddhists, Jains, Sikhs)

GROUNDS FOR DIVORCE (Section 13):
1. Adultery.
2. Cruelty (physical or mental).
3. Desertion for 2+ continuous years.
4. Conversion to another religion.
5. Mental disorder of incurable nature.
6. Leprosy (virulent and incurable).
7. Venereal disease in communicable form.
8. Renunciation of the world.
9. Not heard of as alive for 7+ years.
Additional grounds for wife: Husband's rape/sodomy/bestiality; repudiation of marriage (married before 15 years, exercised right before 18 years); non-resumption of cohabitation after maintenance order.

MUTUAL CONSENT DIVORCE (Section 13B):
Both spouses jointly petition. Must have lived separately for at least 1 year.
First motion → 6-month cooling-off period (can be waived by court) → Second motion → Divorce decree.
Fastest and least adversarial method.

WHERE TO FILE: Family Court (or Civil Court in districts without dedicated Family Court) in district where parties last resided together.

ALIMONY/PERMANENT ALIMONY (Section 25):
Either spouse can claim permanent alimony. Court considers: income, earning capacity, conduct, other circumstances. Can be paid as lump sum or monthly.

STRIDHAN — WOMAN'S RIGHT:
All gifts (cash, jewellery, property) received before, during, and after marriage from family, friends, in-laws belong to the wife absolutely. Husband has no right over stridhan. If taken by force: complaint for criminal breach of trust (BNS 316) and also under DV Act for economic abuse.

CHILD CUSTODY (Guardians and Wards Act 1890 + Hindu Minority and Guardianship Act 1956):
Both parents have equal right to custody. Court decides based on best interests of child.
For children under 5: Mother usually gets custody.
Interim custody: Court can order during pendency of divorce proceedings.
Visitation rights: Non-custodial parent gets scheduled access.
Where to file: Family Court.

DIVORCE — MUSLIM PERSONAL LAW

Talaq (by husband): Husband pronounces divorce. Triple talaq (instant talaq) is now illegal under The Muslim Women (Protection of Rights on Marriage) Act 2019 — punishable with up to 3 years imprisonment.
Khula (by wife): Wife initiates divorce by returning mehr to husband.
Mubarat: Mutual divorce by agreement.
Talaq-e-Tafwid: Wife exercises delegated divorce right given in nikah-nama.
Judicial divorce (Dissolution of Muslim Marriages Act 1939): Wife files suit in court on grounds of cruelty, desertion, husband's imprisonment, impotence, insanity, non-maintenance, etc.

Maintenance for Muslim woman post-divorce:
Muslim Women (Protection of Rights on Divorce) Act 1986 — reasonable provision and maintenance for iddah period. Supreme Court in Shah Bano case affirmed right to maintenance under Section 125 CrPC (now BNSS 144) as well.

DIVORCE — CHRISTIAN (Indian Divorce Act 1869), PARSI (Parsi Marriage and Divorce Act 1936):
File petition before the relevant court. Grounds and procedures differ. Consult DLSA for free legal guidance.

SPECIAL MARRIAGE ACT 1954 — INTER-RELIGIOUS MARRIAGES:
Applies to any couple regardless of religion who register marriage under this Act.
Divorce under Section 27 of the Act — similar grounds as HMA.

DOMESTIC VIOLENCE — CIVIL REMEDY (PWDVA 2005):
Separate from divorce. Woman can seek:
- Protection Order (no further violence/contact).
- Residence Order (right to stay in shared home).
- Monetary Relief (compensation, maintenance).
- Custody Order (temporary custody of children).
File complaint with Protection Officer (free) or Magistrate directly.
Protection Officers are in every district — find them at the district collectorate.

FREE LEGAL AID FOR FAMILY DISPUTES:
District Legal Services Authority (DLSA) provides free lawyers for divorce, maintenance, custody cases for persons who cannot afford legal fees.
Call 15100 (NALSA helpline) or visit your district's DLSA office.

==========================================================================
IF YOU ARE THE RESPONDENT — DEFENSE RIGHTS IN DIVORCE / MAINTENANCE CASES
==========================================================================

YOUR RIGHTS AS RESPONDENT:
- Right to file a written statement within 30 days of receiving summons.
- Right to cross-examine petitioner and their witnesses.
- Right to produce evidence, witnesses, financial documents.
- Right to appeal any Family Court order to the High Court.

WHAT PETITIONER MUST PROVE IN CONTESTED DIVORCE:
- The specific ground for divorce (cruelty, desertion, adultery, etc.) must be proved with evidence.
- Cruelty must be of such nature that it is unsafe or unreasonable to expect the petitioner to live with you — not just ordinary matrimonial discord.
- Desertion requires 2 continuous years of abandonment with intent to desert — temporary separation or departure under compelling circumstances is not desertion.

DEFENSES IN MAINTENANCE CLAIMS (BNSS Section 144):
Wife living in adultery: If wife is living in adultery, she is NOT entitled to maintenance (BNSS Section 144(4)).
Wife refusing to live without reasonable cause: If the wife left without justification and refuses to return, maintenance can be denied or reduced.
Husband has no means: If you genuinely have insufficient income to pay the claimed amount, produce income proof, liabilities, and other dependants.
Child is in your custody: If children live with you, maintenance claimed for children may be reduced or denied.

MUTUAL CONSENT DIVORCE — OPTION FOR BOTH SIDES:
Both parties can jointly file for divorce under Section 13B HMA after 1 year of separation. This avoids adversarial proceedings and lengthy litigation. Both parties agree on: property division, custody, alimony — and courts typically grant it in 6 months (cooling-off period can be waived by court).

PROTECTING YOURSELF IN MATRIMONIAL DISPUTES:
Preserve all financial records showing your income, debts, and assets.
Document any harassment, false allegations, or provocation by spouse.
If false dowry/cruelty case filed: Apply for anticipatory bail immediately and file counter-complaint if warranted.
