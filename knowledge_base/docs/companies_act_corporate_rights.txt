COMPANIES ACT 2013 — SHAREHOLDER RIGHTS, DIRECTOR DUTIES, AND CORPORATE COMPLAINTS

============================
TYPES OF COMPANIES
============================

Private Limited Company: 2–200 shareholders, cannot invite public to subscribe shares, name ends with "Private Limited."
Public Limited Company: Minimum 7 shareholders, can invite public, listed on stock exchange, name ends with "Limited."
One Person Company (OPC): Single owner company — minimum 1 director, 1 shareholder (same person).
Limited Liability Partnership (LLP): Hybrid of company and partnership. Partners have limited liability.

============================
SHAREHOLDER RIGHTS (Companies Act 2013)
============================

ANNUAL GENERAL MEETING (AGM):
Every company must hold AGM within 6 months of financial year end (by September 30 for companies with March 31 year-end).
You have the right to attend AGM, vote on resolutions, and receive notice 21 days in advance.
AGM can be attended through video conferencing (post-COVID norm made permanent).

RIGHT TO VOTE:
Every shareholder has the right to vote — one vote per share (equity). This is a fundamental right.
Postal ballot: For certain special resolutions, voting is through postal ballot / e-voting.
Proxy: You can appoint any person as your proxy to attend and vote at a general meeting.

DIVIDEND:
Once declared, dividend must be paid within 30 days. If unpaid for 7 years, dividend goes to Investor Education and Protection Fund (IEPF). You can still claim from IEPF.
Interim dividend: Board can declare anytime.
Companies cannot declare dividend if they have unpaid dividend for 2 or more years.

INSPECTION OF REGISTERS:
Every shareholder has the right to inspect (free of cost) at the registered office:
- Register of Members (shareholders list)
- Register of Directors
- Minutes of General Meetings
- Financial statements and Annual Report

RIGHT TO INFORMATION:
Company must send Annual Report, financial statements, auditor's report, and Board's report to every shareholder before AGM.
Right to receive copy of memorandum and articles of association on request.

MINORITY SHAREHOLDER PROTECTION:

Section 241 — Oppression and Mismanagement:
If the affairs of the company are being conducted in a manner prejudicial to public interest OR oppressive to any member(s), any shareholder holding at least 10% of shares (or 100 members) can file a petition before the National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT).
NCLT can: Regulate conduct of company's affairs; purchase shares of minority by majority; remove directors; wind up the company.

Class Action Suit (Section 245):
100 members or members holding 10% of shares can file class action against company/directors/auditors for:
- Improper conduct of company affairs
- Misleading statements in prospectus/financial statements
- Declaration of dividend from reserves without profit

NCLT (National Company Law Tribunal):
Quasi-judicial body handling corporate disputes, insolvency, oppression and mismanagement, winding up. Located in 16 cities across India.

============================
DIRECTOR DUTIES AND LIABILITY
============================

Directors owe fiduciary duties to the company and its shareholders:
- Act in good faith in the best interests of the company
- Not to derive undue personal advantage
- Not to be involved in situations where there is a conflict of interest
- Disclose interest in any contract or arrangement

INDEPENDENT DIRECTORS: Listed companies and certain large companies must have at least 1/3rd of board as independent directors (no pecuniary relationship with company).

DIRECTOR DISQUALIFICATION (Section 164):
A person cannot be director if:
- Convicted of offence involving moral turpitude — 5 year disqualification
- Order passed for winding up
- Failed to pay calls on shares for 6 months
- Director of a company that has failed to file financial statements or annual returns for 3 consecutive years
- Director of a company that has failed to repay deposit, debenture, or dividend for 1 year

PERSONAL LIABILITY OF DIRECTORS:
Generally directors are not personally liable for company debts (limited liability).
EXCEPTIONS: Directors can be personally liable for:
- Fraudulent trading (business conducted with intent to defraud creditors)
- Wrongful trading (continuing business when insolvent, knowing no reasonable prospect of avoiding insolvency)
- Personal guarantees given to banks/lenders
- Statutory dues not deposited (like TDS, EPF — directors are personally liable)

============================
INSOLVENCY AND BANKRUPTCY CODE 2016 (IBC)
============================

IBC provides for insolvency resolution of companies, LLPs, and individuals.

CORPORATE INSOLVENCY RESOLUTION PROCESS (CIRP):
A creditor (financial or operational) can initiate CIRP if company has defaulted on payment of Rs. 1 crore or more.
File application before NCLT. Process must be completed in 180 days (extendable by 90 days with NCLT approval).

RESOLUTION PLAN:
Resolution Professional (RP) runs the company during CIRP. Committee of Creditors (CoC) approves a resolution plan (like a buyer/investor taking over and paying creditors).

LIQUIDATION:
If no resolution plan is approved, company goes into liquidation. Assets sold and proceeds distributed in order: Insolvency resolution costs > Secured creditors > Unsalary employee dues (24 months) > Unsecured creditors > Government dues > Preference shareholders > Equity shareholders.

PERSONAL INSOLVENCY:
Individuals and firms with defaults above Rs. 1,000 can file for insolvency. Process handled by Debt Recovery Tribunal (DRT).
Fresh start process for individuals with income below Rs. 60,000/year and assets below Rs. 20,000 — can get debts of up to Rs. 35,000 discharged.
