PROTECTION OF WOMEN FROM DOMESTIC VIOLENCE ACT 2005 — KEY PROVISIONS
======================================================================

OVERVIEW
The Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act 2005 (PWDVA) is a comprehensive civil law that provides immediate relief to women facing domestic violence. It recognizes physical, sexual, verbal, emotional, and economic abuse as forms of domestic violence.

WHO IS PROTECTED?
Any woman who is or has been in a domestic relationship with the respondent (abuser). This includes:
- Wife or female live-in partner
- Sister, mother, daughter living in a shared household
- Widow
- Divorced women (for existing complaints from when marriage existed)
- Women in relationships like marriage (live-in partners)

WHO IS A RESPONDENT (ABUSER)?
Any adult male member of the household. In exceptional cases, female relatives can also be respondents (like mother-in-law in a joint family context — for harassment).

WHAT IS DOMESTIC VIOLENCE?
Section 3 defines domestic violence as any act, omission, or commission that:

PHYSICAL ABUSE:
- Causing bodily pain, harm, danger to life, limb, or health
- Any form of assault, criminal intimidation, criminal force

SEXUAL ABUSE:
- Any conduct that abuses, humiliates, degrades or violates dignity of a woman
- Forced sex within marriage

VERBAL AND EMOTIONAL ABUSE:
- Insults, ridicule, humiliation, name-calling
- Insulting for not having a male child
- Threats to cause physical pain to anyone of interest to the woman

ECONOMIC ABUSE:
- Deprivation of economic or financial resources (food, clothing, medicines, education)
- Disposing of household effects, stridhan, property
- Preventing from carrying on employment or business
- Forced removal from house

DOWRY-RELATED HARASSMENT:
Any demand for dowry or meeting any unlawful demand.

IMMEDIATE RELIEF AVAILABLE

1. PROTECTION ORDER (Section 18):
Court can prohibit respondent from:
- Committing further acts of violence
- Aiding or abetting domestic violence
- Entering workplace or school of aggrieved woman or children
- Communicating with woman by post, phone, email
- Alienating assets or disposing of stridhan

2. RESIDENCE ORDER (Section 19):
Even if woman has no legal title, court can:
- Restrain respondent from dispossessing woman from shared household
- Direct respondent to remove himself from shared household
- Prevent respondent from entering premises occupied by woman and children
- Secure alternate accommodation of similar standard for woman

3. MONETARY RELIEF (Section 20):
Court can direct respondent to pay:
- Loss of earnings
- Medical expenses
- Loss caused by destruction/damage/removal of property
- Maintenance for woman and children
- This is in ADDITION to maintenance under other laws

4. CUSTODY ORDERS (Section 21):
Temporary custody of any child or children to the aggrieved woman.

5. COMPENSATION ORDER (Section 22):
Compensation and damages for injuries, including mental torture and emotional distress.

HOW TO GET HELP — STEP BY STEP

STEP 1: Contact Protection Officer or Service Provider
- Protection Officers are appointed in every district by the state government
- You can also approach NGOs registered as Service Providers
- Nearest police station can connect you to Protection Officer

STEP 2: File Domestic Incident Report (DIR)
- Protection Officer helps fill DIR form
- Free of cost
- No court required at this stage
- Immediate relief can be arranged (shelter, medical aid)

STEP 3: File Application before Magistrate Court
- File under Section 12 of PWDVA
- Can be filed by aggrieved woman directly
- Or through Protection Officer or any registered Service Provider
- No court fee required

STEP 4: Ex-parte Orders
In urgent cases, Magistrate can pass immediate protection order without hearing the respondent first (Section 23).

CONTACT NUMBERS AND RESOURCES
- National Domestic Violence Helpline: 181
- Women Helpline (All India): 1091
- Emergency: 112
- District Legal Services Authority (DLSA): Free legal aid
- iCall: 9152987821 (psychosocial support)

IMPORTANT RIGHTS DURING PROCEEDINGS
- Right to reside in shared household (Section 17) — regardless of ownership or tenancy
- Right to free legal services (Legal Services Authorities Act)
- Protection Officer must assist with all steps — completely free
- Complaint can be filed in any language the woman is comfortable in

SHARED HOUSEHOLD — KEY CONCEPT
Section 2(s): Shared household means any household where aggrieved person lives or has lived with respondent in a domestic relationship. This includes:
- Owned by respondent
- Jointly owned
- Owned by HUF where respondent is member
- Rented premises

CRIMINAL PROVISIONS — SECTION 498A IPC (NOW BNS)
This is separate from PWDVA but often used together.
For cruelty by husband or his relatives:
- Cognizable and non-bailable offence
- Imprisonment up to 3 years AND fine
- Can be filed at police station directly

STRIDHAN — WOMAN'S PROPERTY
Stridhan includes all gifts received before, during, and after marriage from family, friends, husband. This belongs solely to the woman. Husband/in-laws cannot claim it. If taken, complaint can be filed for theft/criminal breach of trust.

WHAT IF WOMAN IS AFRAID TO COMPLAIN?
- She can approach any NGO (Service Provider) in confidence
- Protection Officer visits can be arranged discreetly
- Interim orders can be obtained before respondent even knows
- Woman can stay in Swadhar Greh (government shelter) during proceedings

TIMELINE
- Magistrate must hear case within 3 days of application
- Final order within 60 days of first hearing date
- Ex-parte orders same day if urgency shown

PENALTIES FOR VIOLATING PROTECTION ORDER (Section 31)
Imprisonment of 1 year OR fine up to Rs. 20,000 OR both. Repeat offence: imprisonment up to 2 years.

==========================================================================
IF YOU ARE THE RESPONDENT — DEFENSE RIGHTS IN DOMESTIC VIOLENCE CASES
==========================================================================

IMPORTANT: PWDVA is a civil law — criminal conviction is not automatic. A Protection Order does not mean you are convicted of a crime. However, BNS Section 85 (cruelty) running alongside IS criminal.

YOUR RIGHTS AS RESPONDENT:
- Right to be heard before final orders are passed (ex-parte interim orders are temporary).
- Right to cross-examine the complainant and witnesses.
- Right to produce your own witnesses and evidence.
- Right to a lawyer. Free lawyer from DLSA if you cannot afford one.
- Right to appeal any order within 30 days to the Sessions Court.

WHAT THE COMPLAINANT MUST ESTABLISH:
- That a domestic relationship existed (spouse, family member, live-in partner).
- That an act of domestic violence (as defined in Section 3 PWDVA) occurred.
- That you are the respondent responsible.
The burden of proof in civil proceedings is "balance of probabilities" — lower than criminal standard. However, evidence must still be produced.

COMMON DEFENSES:
False implication as leverage in matrimonial disputes: If the case is filed primarily to gain advantage in divorce/custody proceedings, this motive can be shown to the court.
No domestic relationship: If you and the complainant were never in a domestic relationship as defined by the Act, the Act does not apply.
Challenging specific allegations: Each allegation must be specifically answered with evidence — call records, bank records, medical records, witness accounts.
Prior settlement: If a settlement was reached or a deed of separation was executed, this is relevant to the court.

FOR BNS SECTION 85 (CRUELTY — CRIMINAL CHARGE):
Prosecution must prove cruelty — mental or physical — beyond reasonable doubt.
Mere matrimonial discord, arguments, or incompatibility is NOT cruelty under the law.
Supreme Court in Arnesh Kumar v. State of Bihar: Police cannot make automatic arrests in Section 85 (498A) cases. Magistrate approval required before arrest in most cases. If arrested without this, apply for bail immediately.
Bail: BNS Section 85 is Non-bailable — apply to Magistrate/Sessions Court immediately.
Compounding: With court permission and complainant's consent, Section 85 cases can be settled and withdrawn.

PROTECTING YOURSELF FROM FALSE CASES:
If you fear a false case will be filed, apply for anticipatory bail from Sessions Court proactively.
Keep records of all communication (avoid deleting messages even if they seem unimportant).
Get character witnesses — neighbours, colleagues, family friends.
Document any harassment or provocation by the complainant.
