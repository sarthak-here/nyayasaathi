RERA 2016, PROPERTY DISPUTES AND REAL ESTATE FRAUD
====================================================

RERA ACT 2016 — REAL ESTATE (REGULATION AND DEVELOPMENT) ACT

OVERVIEW
RERA 2016 regulates the real estate sector and protects home buyers from builder fraud, delays, and mis-selling. Each state has its own RERA authority. Civil courts are BARRED from hearing RERA matters — must approach RERA authority.

MANDATORY REGISTRATION

Section 3 — Every residential/commercial project above 500 sq. meters OR 8+ units must be registered with state RERA before selling, advertising, or accepting any booking amount.
Selling without RERA registration: Fine up to 10% of project cost; repeat: up to 3 years imprisonment.
Verify registration: Check state RERA portal before paying any money to builder.

BUILDER'S OBLIGATIONS TO BUYER

Section 14 — Builder must deliver exactly as per sanctioned plans, specifications, and carpet area mentioned in agreement.
Section 14(3) — Structural Defect Warranty: Builder is liable to fix any structural defect or workmanship defect reported within 5 years of handing over possession. No charge to buyer.

BUILDER DELAYS — YOUR RIGHTS (Section 18):
If builder fails to give possession on promised date:
OPTION A: Withdraw and get full refund + interest at SBI MCLR + 2% per annum (currently approx. 10%) from date of each payment until refund.
OPTION B: Continue in project and get monthly interest compensation at same rate until actual possession.
THE CHOICE IS YOURS — builder cannot force you to choose.

HOW TO FILE RERA COMPLAINT

Step 1: Check builder's RERA registration on your state's RERA portal.
Step 2: Calculate your dues — refund amount with interest OR monthly compensation.
Step 3: File complaint on state RERA portal (e.g., maharera.mahaonline.gov.in for Maharashtra, hrera.gov.in for Haryana, rera.rajasthan.gov.in for Rajasthan, etc.).
Step 4: Pay prescribed filing fee (usually Rs. 1,000 to Rs. 5,000 depending on state and claim amount).
Step 5: RERA must decide within 60 days.
Step 6: If RERA order not obeyed, file execution application before RERA/Revenue Recovery.

MISLEADING ADVERTISEMENTS

Section 60 — Builder making false or misleading advertisement about project:
Penalty up to 5% of estimated project cost.
File complaint with RERA authority.

ILLEGAL CONSTRUCTION / UNAPPROVED CHANGES

Builder cannot change approved layout plans without consent of 2/3rd allottees.
Changes to specifications must be with buyer's consent.
If builder makes unauthorized changes: Complaint to RERA + Local Municipal Authority.

PROPERTY DISPUTES (GENERAL)

Illegal Encroachment on your land/property:
Criminal trespass under BNS Section 329/331 — File FIR at police station.
Civil suit for permanent injunction restraining encroachment — File in Civil Court.
Complaint to Collector/SDM for government land.

Property Title Fraud / Forged Documents:
BNS Section 316 (Criminal breach of trust) + Section 318 (cheating) — File FIR.
Civil suit for declaration of title — File in Civil Court.
If registered document is forged: Complaint to Registrar's office + police.

Landlord-Tenant Disputes:
Most states have Rent Control Acts protecting tenants from arbitrary eviction and excessive rent hikes.
Eviction only through Rent Control Court/Civil Court — landlord CANNOT forcibly evict.
Forcible eviction by landlord = criminal trespass (BNS 329) + wrongful restraint.
Withholding security deposit without reason: Civil suit for recovery. Also Consumer Forum if services promised with deposit.
Tenant can approach Rent Controller (usually a magistrate/civil judge) for protection.

Land Acquisition by Government:
Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act 2013.
Government must:
- Give 3-4x market value as compensation.
- Conduct Social Impact Assessment.
- Obtain consent of 70-80% affected families for PPP/private projects.
Disputes about compensation: Special Land Acquisition Court.

Property Registration Issues:
All property transfers must be registered with Sub-Registrar (Registration Act 1908).
Unregistered agreements for sale are NOT enforceable as proof of title (but enforceable as contract).
Stamp duty must be paid — underpayment is deficiency and property documents are impounded.

DOCUMENTS TO PRESERVE IN PROPERTY MATTERS
- Sale deed / Agreement for sale.
- Builder-buyer agreement with all annexures.
- Payment receipts / demand letters / bank transfer records.
- Allotment letter, possession letter.
- Approved building plan, RERA registration certificate.
- Correspondence with builder (emails, WhatsApp — preserve screenshots).
- Municipal / panchayat approvals.

==========================================================================
IF YOU ARE THE BUILDER / OPPOSITE PARTY — DEFENSE RIGHTS IN RERA COMPLAINTS
==========================================================================

YOUR RIGHTS AS A BUILDER OR SELLER:
- Right to file a written reply within the time specified by RERA authority.
- Right to cross-examine the complainant.
- Right to produce your own expert evidence (structural reports, completion certificates, government delay records).
- Right to appeal RERA orders to the Appellate Tribunal within 60 days.

WHAT THE COMPLAINANT MUST PROVE:
- That you have a RERA-registered project and they are an allottee in it.
- That possession was not given by the agreed date, OR the construction is defective, OR there was a material deviation from approved plans.
- The quantum of loss — they must prove actual loss, not just delay.

COMMON DEFENSES:
Force majeure (Act of God / Government action): If delay was caused by: government lockdown (COVID), court stay orders, natural disasters, delay in government approvals beyond your control — these constitute force majeure and can be pleaded as defense. Produce documentary proof of these delays.
Buyer's default: If the buyer himself failed to pay instalments on time, their delay contributed to your construction timeline. Set off buyer's delay against your delay.
Approved plan compliance: If the complainant alleges deviation from plans, produce sanctioned plans and show the construction is within approved parameters.
Force majeure under contract: Check if the builder-buyer agreement specifically defines force majeure and covers the cause of delay.

APPEAL ROUTE:
RERA Authority order → RERA Appellate Tribunal within 60 days → High Court.
Deposit 30% of penalty amount or Rs. 10 lakh (whichever is less) to file appeal before Appellate Tribunal in most states.

IF FALSELY COMPLAINED AGAINST:
If a buyer is using RERA to extort or has filed multiple vexatious complaints: Show the Tribunal the pattern and apply for consolidation of all complaints for joint hearing.
