NARCOTIC DRUGS AND PSYCHOTROPIC SUBSTANCES ACT 1985 (NDPS ACT)

The NDPS Act is one of India's strictest laws. It prohibits manufacture, possession, sale, purchase, transport, use, and trafficking of narcotic drugs and psychotropic substances.

WHAT IS PROHIBITED:
Narcotic drugs: opium (including poppy straw), cannabis (charas, ganja, hashish), cocaine, heroin, morphine, codeine (except in prescribed medicines)
Psychotropic substances: Amphetamine, methamphetamine (meth), LSD, MDMA (ecstasy), benzodiazepines (when obtained without prescription), ketamine

PERMITTED USE: Medicinal and scientific purposes only, with proper licensing.

PUNISHMENT — BASED ON QUANTITY:

Small Quantity:
Cannabis (charas): below 100 grams; Cocaine/heroin: below 5 grams; MDMA: below 0.5 grams
Punishment for possession: Rigorous imprisonment up to 6 months OR fine OR both.

Commercial Quantity:
Cannabis (charas): 1 kg or above; Cocaine/heroin: 100 grams or above
Punishment: Rigorous imprisonment 10 to 20 years AND fine Rs. 1 to 2 lakh (mandatory).

Intermediate Quantity (between small and commercial):
Punishment: Rigorous imprisonment up to 10 years and fine.

FINANCING or HARBOURING drug offences: Same punishment as the trafficker (10–20 years RI).

REPEAT OFFENCES: Double the punishment; death penalty in certain cases of repeat commercial trafficking.

BAIL — VERY STRICT:
Section 37 NDPS: No bail for commercial quantity offences UNLESS:
1. Public prosecutor is given opportunity to oppose bail
2. Court is satisfied that accused is NOT guilty AND is NOT likely to commit any offence while on bail
This makes bail extremely difficult in NDPS cases. This is a special provision overriding normal bail rules.

ARREST AND SEARCH:
Police, Central Bureau of Narcotics, NCB (Narcotics Control Bureau), Customs, BSF, CRPF officers can arrest without warrant.
They can search persons, premises, vehicles without warrant in certain circumstances.

IMPORTANT: NDPS cases must follow strict procedural requirements (Section 42 and 50):
- Before personal search: Officer must inform you that you have the right to be searched before a Gazetted Officer or a Magistrate. If you request this, they MUST comply.
- Failure to follow this procedure can be a ground for acquittal.

CONTROLLED DELIVERY: Undercover operations are permitted under NDPS to catch drug traffickers.

POSSESSION vs. CONSUMPTION:
Mere consumption of drugs (without possession) attracts lighter punishment under Section 27: Imprisonment up to 1 year or fine or both.
Note: Urine test showing drug use can be evidence of consumption.

REHABILITATION (Section 64A):
First-time offender found possessing small quantity for personal consumption: Can opt for drug treatment and rehabilitation instead of punishment. Charge may be dropped on successful completion.

IF YOU ARE FALSELY ACCUSED:
NDPS cases often involve allegations of planted drugs ("haath saaf"). If this happens:
1. Immediately demand search in front of a Magistrate or Gazetted Officer (your right under Section 50)
2. Note the name and badge number of arresting officer
3. Insist on video recording of search
4. Demand to see the inventory/seizure memo
5. Contact a lawyer immediately — bail is very difficult in commercial quantity cases
6. File complaint with police accountability body if drugs are planted

NARCOTICS CONTROL BUREAU (NCB):
National agency for drug law enforcement. Handles inter-state and international drug cases.
NCB Helpline: 1800-180-0003 (to report drug trafficking)

POPPY CULTIVATION:
Licensed opium poppy cultivation is permitted only in Madhya Pradesh, Rajasthan, Uttar Pradesh. Cultivating without licence: treated as production of narcotic drug — severe punishment.

NDPS and YOUNG PERSONS:
Supplying drugs to a person under 18 years: Double the punishment.
Using a person under 18 years for drug trafficking: Double the punishment.
