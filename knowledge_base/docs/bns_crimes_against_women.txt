BNS 2023 — CRIMES AGAINST WOMEN AND CHILDREN (Chapter V, Sections 63–99)
==========================================================================
(Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita replaced IPC w.e.f. July 1, 2024)
(Cases before July 1, 2024 continue under old IPC sections)

OVERVIEW
The Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023 replaced the Indian Penal Code 1860 on July 1, 2024. Chapter V covers offences against women and children with significantly enhanced punishments compared to the old IPC.

RAPE AND SEXUAL ASSAULT

BNS Section 64 (Old IPC 376) — Rape Punishment:
Minimum 10 years rigorous imprisonment up to life + fine.
Cognizable, Non-bailable offence. Tried by Sessions Court.
File complaint at nearest police station or SJPU (Special Juvenile Police Unit for minors).

BNS Section 65 (Old IPC 376(3)) — Rape on woman under 18 years:
Minimum 20 years RI up to life imprisonment + fine, or death penalty.
Cognizable, Non-bailable.

BNS Section 66 (Old IPC 376A) — Rape causing death or persistent vegetative state:
Minimum 20 years RI up to life, or death + fine payable to victim.
Cognizable, Non-bailable.

BNS Section 69 (NEW — no IPC equivalent) — Deceptive sexual intercourse:
Sexual intercourse obtained by fraudulent means, deceit, false identity, or false promise of marriage/employment/promotion.
Up to 10 years imprisonment + fine.
Cognizable, Non-bailable.

BNS Section 70 (Old IPC 376D) — Gang rape:
Minimum 20 years RI up to life + fine. Death penalty if victim under 18.
Cognizable, Non-bailable.

SEXUAL HARASSMENT AND STALKING

BNS Section 74 (Old IPC 354) — Assault or criminal force outraging modesty of woman:
1 to 5 years imprisonment + fine.
Cognizable, Non-bailable.
File at: Police station.

BNS Section 75 (Old IPC 354A) — Sexual harassment:
Physical contact / unwelcome advances / demand for sexual favours: Up to 3 years + fine.
Making sexually coloured remarks: Up to 1 year + fine.
Cognizable, Non-bailable.
File at: Police station; also Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) under POSH Act for workplace.

BNS Section 76 (Old IPC 354B) — Assault to disrobe a woman:
3 to 7 years imprisonment + fine.
Cognizable, Non-bailable.

BNS Section 77 (Old IPC 354C) — Voyeurism (including online):
Capturing or publishing images of woman's private acts without consent.
First offence: 1 to 3 years + fine. Repeat: 3 to 7 years + fine.
First offence Bailable; Repeat Non-bailable.
File at: Police station / Cyber Crime Cell (cybercrime.gov.in)

BNS Section 78 (Old IPC 354D) — Stalking (physical or online):
Following a woman, contacting or attempting to contact despite clear disinterest, monitoring online activity.
First offence: Up to 3 years + fine (Bailable).
Repeat offence: Up to 5 years + fine (Non-bailable).
File at: Police station + Cyber Crime Cell for online stalking.

BNS Section 79 (Old IPC 509) — Words or gestures to insult modesty of woman:
Making sounds, gestures, showing object, or intruding upon privacy of a woman.
Up to 3 years + fine. Cognizable, Bailable.
File at: Police station.

DOWRY AND MARITAL CRUELTY

BNS Section 80 (Old IPC 304B) — Dowry death:
Death of woman within 7 years of marriage caused or connected to dowry harassment.
7 years imprisonment to life imprisonment.
Cognizable, Non-bailable. Tried by Sessions Court.
File at: Police station.

BNS Section 85 (Old IPC 498A) — Cruelty by husband or his relatives:
Subjecting woman to cruelty — physical or mental harassment, demands for dowry, threats, conduct driving woman to suicide.
Up to 3 years imprisonment + fine.
Cognizable, Non-bailable.
File at: Police station.

NOTE: BNS Section 85/86 (cruelty) runs separately from the Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act 2005 (PWDVA). PWDVA is a civil remedy; BNS 85 is criminal. Both can be filed simultaneously.

ACID ATTACK

BNS Section 124 (Old IPC 326A/326B) — Acid attack:
Throwing or administering acid causing permanent/partial damage:
Minimum 10 years RI up to life + fine (paid to victim for medical treatment).
Attempting acid attack: 5 to 7 years + fine.
Cognizable, Non-bailable.

- Women Helpline (All India): 1091
- Domestic Violence / Shelter: 181
- Police Emergency: 112
- Child Helpline (POCSO cases): 1098
- NHRC: 14433
- National Commission for Women: 7827170170

WHERE TO FILE COMPLAINT
1. Nearest police station — FIR must be registered, cannot be refused.
2. For POCSO/child cases: Special Juvenile Police Unit (SJPU) or Child Welfare Committee.
3. Online: cybercrime.gov.in for cyber-related sexual offences.
4. Zero FIR: Can file at ANY police station regardless of where incident occurred.

RIGHTS OF RAPE SURVIVORS
- Statement recorded by female police officer (mandatory under BNSS).
- Medical examination at government hospital — FREE (private hospitals also mandated under BNSS Section 397).
- In-camera court proceedings (identity not revealed publicly).
- Victim compensation from State Victim Compensation Scheme (apply to SLSA/DLSA).
- Free legal aid from District Legal Services Authority (DLSA).

==========================================================================
IF YOU ARE THE ACCUSED — DEFENSE RIGHTS IN CRIMES AGAINST WOMEN CASES
==========================================================================

YOUR CONSTITUTIONAL RIGHTS:
- Right to remain silent — no forced confession under any circumstances (Article 20(3)).
- Right to a lawyer from the moment of arrest (Article 22(1)). Free lawyer from DLSA.
- In rape cases: Right to medical examination by a doctor of your choice.
- No detention beyond 24 hours without magistrate's remand.

WHAT PROSECUTION MUST PROVE — RAPE (BNS Section 64):
- Sexual intercourse occurred.
- It was without consent OR consent was obtained by force, threat, fraud, or impersonation.
- You were the perpetrator — identity must be established beyond reasonable doubt.
Prosecution must prove absence of consent — this is a high burden of proof.

FOR BNS SECTION 85 (CRUELTY / DOWRY HARASSMENT):
Prosecution must prove cruelty — physical or mental — beyond reasonable doubt.
Matrimonial disagreements, arguments, and ordinary domestic friction are NOT cruelty under the law.
Supreme Court: FIR under 498A/BNS 85 does not automatically mean arrest — police must follow Arnesh Kumar guidelines and take magistrate approval before arrest.

FOR BNS SECTION 78 (STALKING):
Prosecution must prove: persistent following/contact despite clear disinterest. A single instance or a genuine attempt to reconcile in a relationship may not meet "persistent" threshold.

BAIL:
BNS Section 85 (cruelty): Non-bailable — apply immediately to Magistrate/Sessions Court. Bail often granted for first-time accused with no prior record in 498A cases.
BNS Section 64 (rape): Non-bailable — bail very difficult to get. Apply to Sessions Court with strong grounds (false implication, consensual relationship, alibi).
BNS Section 78 (stalking, first offence): Bailable — absolute right to bail.
BNS Section 75 (sexual harassment): Non-bailable — Sessions Court bail application.

COMMON DEFENSES:
Consent: In cases where the relationship was consensual — produce evidence of relationship (messages, social media, mutual plans). False promise of marriage cases require examining intent at the time of the promise.
Alibi: You were not present — phone location data, travel records, witnesses.
Motivated complaint: Establish the complainant's motive — prior dispute, failed relationship, property conflict.
Mistaken identity: If complainant cannot specifically identify you.
Contradictions in complainant's statement: Inconsistencies between FIR, statement before Magistrate, and deposition in court weaken the prosecution case.

PROTECTING YOURSELF FROM FALSE CASES:
If you anticipate a false case: Preserve all communication evidence. Apply for anticipatory bail proactively.
In matrimonial disputes: Before separation, record agreements, document financial transactions, keep witnesses to important interactions.
