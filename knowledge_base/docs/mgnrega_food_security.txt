MGNREGA (MAHATMA GANDHI NATIONAL RURAL EMPLOYMENT GUARANTEE ACT 2005) AND NATIONAL FOOD SECURITY ACT 2013

============================
MGNREGA — RIGHT TO WORK
============================

MGNREGA guarantees every rural household the right to at least 100 days of unskilled manual work per financial year (April to March) at the notified wage rate.

WHO IS ELIGIBLE:
Any adult member of a rural household who is willing to do unskilled manual work. Any person above 18 years in a rural area can apply. There is NO income limit — even comparatively better-off rural households are eligible.

HOW TO REGISTER AND GET WORK:
1. Register the household with the local Gram Panchayat. Get Job Card (MGNREGA Job Card) — free.
2. Job Card lists all adult members of the household who are registered.
3. When you need work, submit a written application (dated) to the Gram Panchayat.
4. Work MUST be provided within 15 days of application.
5. If work is not provided within 15 days: You are entitled to an UNEMPLOYMENT ALLOWANCE (1/4th of minimum wage for first 30 days, 1/2 of minimum wage for subsequent days).
6. Work must be within 5 km radius of the applicant's residence. If outside: 10% extra wage for travel.

WAGE RATE:
Minimum notified by Central Government per state (varies). Current rates (2024-25): Rs. 200–350+ per day depending on state.
Wages MUST be paid within 15 days of work completion. If delayed: Workers entitled to compensation at 0.05% of wages per day of delay.

TYPES OF WORK:
Water conservation, land development, rural connectivity (roads), flood protection, rural sanitation, individual beneficiary works (ponds on private land of SC/ST/BPL, disabled persons).

TRANSPARENCY AND SOCIAL AUDIT:
All MGNREGA works must be subject to Social Audit. Social Audit Report must be read publicly in Gram Sabha. Any irregularity found: Gram Panchayat must act on findings within 3 months. If no action: Matter referred to State Government.

CORRUPTION IN MGNREGA — HOW TO COMPLAIN:
1. Gram Sabha
2. District Programme Coordinator (DPC) — Block/Tehsil level
3. State Level Ombudsman (every state must have MGNREGA Ombudsman)
4. Central MGNREGA helpline: 1800-345-22-44
5. Grievance: nregasoft.nic.in (MGNREGA MIS portal)
6. RTI application for work records

RIGHT TO TRANSPARENCY:
Muster rolls (attendance records) and measurement books must be available for public inspection at the Gram Panchayat.
Anyone can inspect MGNREGA records and ask for copies.

INDIVIDUAL WORKS:
SC, ST, BPL families, and disabled persons are entitled to works on their own land: ponds, irrigation channels, land levelling. Apply at Gram Panchayat.

============================
NATIONAL FOOD SECURITY ACT 2013 (NFSA)
============================

The NFSA provides legal entitlement to subsidised food grain to 75% of rural population and 50% of urban population.

ENTITLEMENT:
- Priority Households (PH): 5 kg of foodgrain per person per month
- Antyodaya Anna Yojana (AAY) households (poorest of poor): 35 kg per household per month
Price: Rice at Rs. 3/kg, wheat at Rs. 2/kg, coarse grains at Rs. 1/kg. (Bharat Prices — may be revised or further subsidized by state governments)
Many states provide grains FREE (like Delhi, Tamil Nadu etc.) over NFSA guarantee.

RATION CARD:
To receive PDS (Public Distribution System) ration, you need a Ration Card.
- Green/Priority Card: Priority household — entitled to 5 kg/person/month
- Yellow/AAY Card: Antyodaya household — 35 kg/household
Application: Apply at Block/Tehsil office or online on state government portal.
Ration card is linked to Aadhaar for authentication at Fair Price Shop (FPS).

PORTABILITY:
One Nation One Ration Card (ONORC): You can use your ration card at ANY Fair Price Shop in India — not just in your home state. Useful for migrant workers.

MID-DAY MEAL (PM-POSHAN):
All government and government-aided school children get free cooked meal under this scheme. Child must receive meal on all school days. Complaint if meal not served: School authorities → District Education Officer → State Nodal Officer.

SUPPLEMENTARY NUTRITION (ICDS/POSHAN):
Children below 6 years and pregnant/lactating women get supplementary nutrition at Anganwadi Centres (AWC). This is an entitlement — AWC worker must provide.

IF YOU ARE NOT GETTING YOUR ENTITLED RATION:
1. Complaint to FPS dealer's supervisor (Block Supply Officer)
2. Complaint to District Supply Officer
3. State Food Commission (every state must have one under NFSA)
4. National Food Commission (if state doesn't act)
5. File RTI for ration distribution records
6. State Food helplines

NATIONAL FOOD SECURITY PORTAL: nfsa.gov.in — check your entitlement, find your FPS, lodge grievance.

EXCLUSIONS FROM PDS:
Government employees, income tax payees, large landholders, vehicle owners (exclusion criteria vary by state). Wrongful exclusion: Appeal to District Grievance Redressal Officer.

PDS CORRUPTION (ration diversion, phantom beneficiaries, poor quality grain):
File complaint with District Supply Officer or State Vigilance Committee.
Social Audit of PDS is being implemented in many states.
