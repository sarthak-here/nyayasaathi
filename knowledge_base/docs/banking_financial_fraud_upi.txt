BANKING FRAUD, UPI SCAMS, FINANCIAL FRAUD — RIGHTS AND REMEDIES
================================================================

OVERVIEW
Financial fraud includes UPI fraud, credit/debit card fraud, bank fraud, investment scams, insurance fraud, loan fraud, and chit fund fraud. Multiple agencies and laws apply. Act FAST — money recovery chances decrease with time.

IMMEDIATE STEPS FOR FINANCIAL FRAUD
1. Call 1930 (National Cybercrime Helpline) IMMEDIATELY — this is the most important step.
2. Call your bank's 24-hour helpline and freeze the account/card.
3. File complaint at cybercrime.gov.in — get acknowledgment number.
4. Visit nearest police station (Cyber Crime Cell) within 24 hours with all documents.
5. Your bank MUST give you a complaint reference number.

UPI FRAUD — SPECIFIC TYPES AND REMEDIES

"Wrong number" scam: Fraudster claims they sent money to wrong number, sends screenshot, asks you to return money. NEVER return money shown in fake screenshots — check your actual bank balance.

QR Code scam: Fraudster sends QR code to scan "to receive money" — scanning actually SENDS money.

Fake KYC calls: Fraudsters impersonate bank/NPCI officials and ask for OTP, card number, CVV, Aadhaar, or ask you to install remote access apps (AnyDesk, TeamViewer).
YOUR BANK WILL NEVER ASK FOR OTP OR FULL CARD NUMBER BY PHONE.

Fake investment apps promising 10-30% monthly returns: These are Ponzi schemes. Report to SEBI (sebi.gov.in) + cybercrime.gov.in + police.

Fake lottery/prize calls asking for "GST" or "fee" before releasing prize: No legitimate lottery charges fee to give prize. Report to cybercrime.gov.in.

APPLICABLE LAWS FOR FINANCIAL FRAUD
- IT Act Section 66C: Identity theft (using your OTP/credentials without consent).
- IT Act Section 66D: Cheating by personation (fake bank official, fake portal).
- BNS Section 318: Cheating (inducing delivery of property by fraud).
- BNS Section 308: Extortion (if threatening calls).
- RBI Guidelines on unauthorized transactions (most important for bank refunds).

RBI GUIDELINES — UNAUTHORIZED TRANSACTIONS IN BANK ACCOUNT

Zero Liability (you pay NOTHING) if:
- Fraud is due to bank's negligence (bank system hacked, insider fraud).
- You reported the fraud within 3 working days.

Limited Liability (you pay part) if:
- Fraud is due to negligence on your part but you reported within 4-7 days.
- Liability capped at Rs. 5,000 to Rs. 25,000 depending on account type.

Higher Liability if reported after 7 days or if due entirely to your gross negligence.

HOW TO CLAIM REFUND FROM BANK:
Step 1: File complaint with bank's customer care within 24-72 hours — get complaint number.
Step 2: Bank must respond to complaint within 90 days.
Step 3: If bank refuses or doesn't respond in 30 days, file complaint with:
RBI Banking Ombudsman: bankingombudsman.rbi.org.in (free, no fee).
Step 4: If RBI Ombudsman doesn't resolve, file complaint at Consumer Forum (Consumer Protection Act 2019 — deficiency in banking service).

CHEQUE BOUNCE — NEGOTIABLE INSTRUMENTS ACT SECTION 138
See also: bns_property_crimes_fraud.txt
A cheque bounce for insufficient funds is both a civil and criminal matter.
Creditor's steps:
1. Receive dishonour memo from bank (within validity period — 3 months of cheque date).
2. Send demand notice within 30 days of receiving dishonour memo.
3. Wait 15 days for payment.
4. If unpaid: File complaint in Magistrate court within 30 days of expiry of notice period.
Punishment: Up to 2 years OR fine up to twice the cheque amount, or both.
Interim compensation: Court can order 20% of cheque amount to be paid during trial.

INVESTMENT FRAUD — SEBI

If stockbroker, mutual fund, or investment advisor cheats you:
- Registered broker/advisor: Complaint to SEBI (sebi.gov.in/sebiweb/other/OtherAction.do?doRecognisedFpi=yes&intmId=32).
- SCORES portal: scores.sebi.gov.in — online complaint platform.
- SEBI Helpline: 1800 266 7575 (toll free).
- For BSE/NSE: Investor grievance cell of respective exchange.
- Unregistered entity taking money: Police complaint (BNS 318) + SEBI.

INSURANCE FRAUD / CLAIM DENIAL

If insurance company denies or delays legitimate claim:
1. File complaint with insurance company's grievance cell — get reference number.
2. Insurance Ombudsman (free): Approach if not resolved within 30 days by insurer. Covers: life, health, motor insurance. Website: cioins.co.in (Council for Insurance Ombudsman).
3. Consumer Forum: Consumer Protection Act 2019 — deficiency in service.
4. IRDAI Bima Bharosa portal: Bimabharosa.irdai.gov.in or call 155255.

Common issues:
- Claim rejection citing "pre-existing condition" not disclosed: Challenge with medical records showing it was actually a new condition.
- Motor insurance claim rejected after accident: Challenge citing Section 149 MV Act (pay-and-recover principle).
- Life insurance claim rejected citing suppression: If genuinely not aware of condition, challenge with medical opinion.

LOAN RECOVERY HARASSMENT BY BANKS/NBFCs/RECOVERY AGENTS

RBI guidelines prohibit banks and recovery agents from:
- Calling before 7 AM or after 7 PM.
- Using abusive, threatening, or intimidating language.
- Visiting borrower's residence or workplace repeatedly.
- Approaching relatives, friends, or employer.
- Making threatening calls or publicly shaming the borrower.

If this happens:
1. Record calls (if possible).
2. File complaint with bank's Nodal Officer/Grievance Cell.
3. File complaint with RBI Ombudsman: bankingombudsman.rbi.org.in.
4. File FIR if threats amount to criminal intimidation (BNS Section 351).
5. File complaint with IRDAI/RBI/SEBI depending on type of institution.

CHIT FUND FRAUD (Ponzi Schemes)
Regulated under Chit Funds Act 1982 and SEBI (for collective investment schemes).
Unregistered chit funds are illegal. Report to: State Registrar of Chit Funds + Economic Offences Wing (EOW) of state police + cybercrime.gov.in.
File FIR under BNS Section 318 (cheating) + state chit fund regulations.

==========================================================================
IF YOU ARE ACCUSED OF FINANCIAL FRAUD — DEFENSE RIGHTS
==========================================================================

YOUR CONSTITUTIONAL RIGHTS:
- Right to remain silent — do NOT explain transactions to police without a lawyer.
- Right to a lawyer immediately upon arrest.
- Bank account freeze orders by police can be challenged — demand proper legal authority for any freeze.

WHAT PROSECUTION MUST PROVE:
For BNS Section 318 (Cheating): Prosecution must prove you made a false representation KNOWING it was false, and the victim was induced by it. A bad investment that lost money, a failed business deal, or a genuine dispute about repayment terms is NOT cheating.
For IT Act Section 66C (Identity theft): Prosecution must prove you fraudulently used someone else's credentials — not that your own credentials were misused by someone else.

CIVIL VS. CRIMINAL DISTINCTION:
Unpaid loans, failed investments, and business disputes gone wrong are CIVIL matters — not fraud. Supreme Court has held repeatedly that criminal courts cannot be used as debt recovery tools. If you have a genuine dispute about a loan, contract, or investment, apply to High Court to quash any criminal FIR filed to harass you.

BANK ACCOUNT FREEZE — YOUR RIGHTS:
Police can request banks to freeze accounts linked to fraud investigation — but only with proper written orders.
You can challenge an account freeze by approaching the bank for the written authority and then challenging it before the Magistrate/Sessions Court.
Legitimate business transactions frozen due to erroneous cyber complaint: File representation to Cyber Crime Cell immediately with evidence of legitimate transactions.

COMMON DEFENSES FOR ACCUSED IN FINANCIAL FRAUD:
Genuine transaction: Produce contracts, receipts, communication showing the transaction was legitimate.
You were defrauded too: If you were also a victim in a chain fraud (e.g., a middleman in a scam), show you acted in good faith and suffered loss too.
Identity compromise: Your bank account/UPI ID was used without your knowledge — show login alerts, unusual location data, police complaint you filed about the compromise.
