MEDICAL NEGLIGENCE, PATIENT RIGHTS, AND HOSPITAL DISPUTES
===========================================================

WHAT IS MEDICAL NEGLIGENCE?
Medical negligence occurs when a doctor, hospital, or healthcare provider fails to provide the standard of care expected of a reasonably competent medical professional, resulting in harm to the patient.
Must prove: (1) Duty of care existed; (2) Duty was breached; (3) Breach caused harm; (4) Actual damage/injury resulted.

PATIENT RIGHTS (Recognized by Supreme Court and NMC/IMA guidelines):
1. Right to be informed about diagnosis, treatment options, risks, and alternatives.
2. Right to give INFORMED CONSENT before any procedure (can refuse treatment).
3. Right to privacy and confidentiality of medical records.
4. Right to a second opinion.
5. Right to access your own medical records and get copies.
6. Right to emergency treatment regardless of ability to pay (cannot be refused).
7. Right to know the qualifications of the treating doctor.

EMERGENCY TREATMENT — NO REFUSAL ALLOWED
No hospital (government or private) can refuse emergency treatment citing:
- Non-payment of fees.
- Lack of beds.
- Lack of blood.
Under BNSS Section 397: Hospitals MUST provide free first aid and emergency treatment to victims of rape, acid attack, and POCSO offences.
Supreme Court directions: Any hospital denying emergency treatment is liable for negligence.

REMEDIES FOR MEDICAL NEGLIGENCE

OPTION 1: CONSUMER FORUM (Most Accessible)
Doctors and hospitals are "service providers" under Consumer Protection Act 2019.
Patient is a "consumer." Medical negligence = deficiency in service.
File complaint at: District Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission (claims up to Rs. 1 crore).
Compensation for: Medical expenses, loss of income, pain and suffering, mental agony.
Limitation: 2 years from date of negligence or knowledge thereof.

OPTION 2: CIVIL SUIT FOR DAMAGES
File civil suit in appropriate civil court claiming compensation for negligence.
Requires expert medical opinion supporting your claim.
Limitation: 3 years from date of cause of action.

OPTION 3: CRIMINAL COMPLAINT
Only for gross negligence amounting to recklessness (not mere error of judgment).
BNS Section 106 (Old IPC 304A) — Death by negligence (for doctors): Up to 2 years + fine (reduced from 5 years to protect doctors from misuse — per 2023 change).
File FIR at police station for criminal negligence.
Supreme Court in Jacob Mathew case: Criminal prosecution requires prima facie evidence of gross negligence beyond a mere mistake.

OPTION 4: MEDICAL COUNCIL COMPLAINT
For professional misconduct, negligent behaviour, or ethics violations by a doctor.
National Medical Commission (NMC): nmc.org.in — for MBBS and specialists.
State Medical Council: For state-registered doctors.
Consequence: Warning, suspension, or cancellation of medical registration.
This does NOT give financial compensation — use Consumer Forum for that.

SPECIFIC SITUATIONS

WRONG DIAGNOSIS / DELAYED DIAGNOSIS:
If wrong/delayed diagnosis caused harm: Consumer Forum for compensation.
Collect: Prescriptions, lab reports, discharge summary, all medical records.

SURGICAL ERRORS / WRONG SITE SURGERY:
Gross negligence — both Consumer Forum and criminal complaint viable.
Get second opinion from another specialist to establish the error.

HOSPITAL OVERCHARGING:
State governments fix price caps for many procedures under Clinical Establishments Act.
Complaint to: State Clinical Establishments Regulatory Authority or District Health Officer.
Consumer Forum: Overcharging = deficiency in service.

DENIAL OF MEDICAL RECORDS:
You have an absolute right to your own medical records.
Hospital must give copies (may charge nominal fee).
If refused: Consumer Forum complaint + complaint to NMC/State Medical Council.

AYUSHMAN BHARAT (PM-JAY) DENIAL:
Empanelled hospitals cannot deny treatment to Ayushman Bharat beneficiaries for covered procedures.
If denied: Call 14555 (PM-JAY helpline) or visit pmjay.gov.in.
Complaint to State Health Agency or National Health Authority.
District Magistrate can intervene.

BLOOD TRANSFUSION ERRORS / CONTAMINATED BLOOD:
Hospital strictly liable for transfusion of wrong/HIV-contaminated blood.
Both Consumer Forum and criminal complaint available.
Report to: State Drug Controller (for blood bank licence action) + Consumer Forum.

EVIDENCE TO COLLECT
All prescriptions, OPD slips, admission notes.
Discharge summary.
Operation notes/procedure reports.
Nursing records.
All bills and receipts.
Diagnostic reports (X-ray, MRI, blood tests).
Post-mortem report (if death occurred).
Second opinion from another specialist (expert evidence is critical in negligence cases).

HELPFUL RESOURCES
- National Consumer Helpline: 1800-11-4000
- National Medical Commission: nmc.org.in
- Ayushman Bharat helpline: 14555
- State Health Ombudsman (where exists): Check state health department portal

IF YOU ARE THE DOCTOR / HOSPITAL — DEFENSE RIGHTS IN NEGLIGENCE CASES
==========================================================================

YOUR RIGHTS AS A MEDICAL PROFESSIONAL:
- Right to produce your own expert medical opinion supporting the treatment given.
- Right to challenge the complainant's expert with your own specialist.
- Right to appeal Consumer Forum orders to the State Commission within 45 days.

WHAT THE COMPLAINANT MUST PROVE:
- That a duty of care existed (doctor-patient relationship).
- That the duty was breached — a departure from the accepted standard of medical care.
- That the breach directly caused the harm suffered.
Supreme Court in Jacob Mathew v. State of Punjab: A mere error of judgment, a wrong diagnosis, or an unsuccessful treatment is NOT negligence. Negligence requires a departure from the standard of a "reasonably competent doctor" — not the standard of the best doctor.

KEY DEFENSES:
Error of judgment vs. negligence: Choosing one of several accepted treatment options that did not succeed is NOT negligence. Show that the treatment chosen was within the range of accepted medical practice.
Informed consent was obtained: Produce the signed consent form. If the risk that materialized was a known complication disclosed to the patient, the claim fails.
Pre-existing condition: The harm was caused by the patient's underlying condition, not by the treatment given.
Patient non-compliance: Patient did not follow prescribed treatment, missed follow-ups, or ignored warnings — this contributed to or caused the harm.
Expert evidence: Obtain a supportive opinion from a specialist in the same field confirming the treatment was appropriate.

FOR CRIMINAL NEGLIGENCE (BNS Section 106):
The standard for criminal liability is much higher — the prosecution must prove GROSS negligence, not just a medical error.
Supreme Court: Criminal prosecution for medical negligence requires a prima facie case of recklessness, not mere lack of skill.
Apply to High Court to quash criminal FIR if the case is really about civil negligence dressed up as a crime.

CONSUMER FORUM DEFENSE:
A private doctor running a clinic is a "service provider" — Consumer Act applies.
Defense: Produce all records showing treatment was appropriate, informed consent was obtained, and the harm was due to factors beyond your control.
