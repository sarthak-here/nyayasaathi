MATERNITY BENEFIT (AMENDMENT) ACT 2017 AND EQUAL REMUNERATION ACT 1976

============================
MATERNITY BENEFIT ACT 1961 (as amended 2017)
============================

APPLICABILITY: All establishments with 10 or more employees. Women employed in mines, factories, plantations, shops, government offices.

MATERNITY LEAVE ENTITLEMENT:
- 26 weeks paid maternity leave for women with fewer than 2 surviving children (increased from 12 weeks in 2017)
- 12 weeks paid maternity leave for women who already have 2 or more surviving children
- Leave can be taken up to 8 weeks before expected delivery (remaining after delivery)

ADOPTING MOTHERS (2017 Amendment):
- 12 weeks of paid maternity leave for women adopting a child below 3 months of age (from date of adoption)
- 12 weeks for commissioning mother (surrogate mother who provides egg)

MISCARRIAGE/MTP: 6 weeks of paid leave from the day of miscarriage or medical termination of pregnancy.

TUBECTOMY: 2 weeks of paid leave immediately after the operation.

ILLNESS FOLLOWING DELIVERY: Additional paid leave up to 1 month for illness arising out of pregnancy, delivery, premature birth.

WORK FROM HOME (2017 Amendment):
For establishments with 50 or more employees, employer may allow working from home after maternity leave ends, based on mutual agreement.

CRÈCHE FACILITY (2017 Amendment):
Every establishment with 50 or more employees must provide crèche facility within prescribed distance. Women employees are entitled to 4 visits per day to the crèche (including rest interval).

PAYMENT DURING MATERNITY LEAVE:
- Full wages at the rate of average daily wages for the maternity benefit period
- Employer cannot deduct wages during maternity leave
- Advance payment for 8 weeks before delivery, remaining after delivery

DISMISSAL DURING MATERNITY LEAVE:
- A woman cannot be dismissed during or because of maternity leave
- Any dismissal during maternity leave is illegal — reinstatement + full wages can be claimed
- The employer cannot change any terms and conditions of service of a woman during maternity leave to her disadvantage

MEDICAL BONUS: If employer does not provide pre-natal and post-natal care, must pay a medical bonus of Rs. 3,500 (may be revised by government).

HOW TO CLAIM:
1. Give written notice to employer about expected date of delivery (before 8 weeks)
2. Submit Form A (Notice of Claim) with medical certificate
3. Employer must pay within 48 hours of receiving notice
4. If employer refuses: file complaint with Inspector (appointed by state government) under Section 17
5. Inspector can examine records, enter premises, and direct payment
6. Appeal against Inspector's order: Chief Inspector

PENALTY FOR EMPLOYER VIOLATION:
Imprisonment up to 1 year, or fine up to Rs. 5,000, or both. If employer dismisses or discharges a woman on account of her pregnancy: imprisonment up to 3 months and fine.

============================
EQUAL REMUNERATION ACT 1976
============================

PRINCIPLE: Equal pay for equal work — no discrimination in remuneration between men and women for same or similar work.

KEY PROVISIONS:

Section 4 — Duty to Pay Equal Remuneration:
Employer MUST pay equal remuneration (wages and allowances) to men and women workers performing the same or similar nature of work. Any difference based solely on sex is illegal.

Section 5 — No Discrimination in Recruitment:
Employer cannot discriminate while recruiting workers for same or similar work on grounds of sex. However, employment of women can be restricted where their employment is prohibited or limited by law (like in certain mining operations at night).

Section 6 — Advisory Committees:
Government constitutes advisory committees (with women members) for promoting employment opportunities for women.

Section 8 — Maintenance of Registers:
Every employer must maintain a register of workers (male and female) doing same/similar work with their wages — Form D Register.

COMPLAINT PROCEDURE:
1. File complaint with Inspector (Labour Inspector) appointed by appropriate government
2. Inspector can enter premises, examine records and workers
3. If employer has contravened: Inspector passes order directing payment of difference in remuneration

PENALTY FOR VIOLATION:
First offence: Fine Rs. 10,000 to Rs. 20,000 or imprisonment up to 3 months or both.
Second/subsequent offence: Imprisonment up to 1 year and fine.

CONSTITUTION BACKING: The right to equal pay for equal work flows from Articles 14 (equality), 15 (non-discrimination), and 39(d) of the Constitution (Directive Principle on equal pay). Courts have upheld this right consistently.

GENDER PAY GAP REMEDIES:
If you are being paid less than male colleagues for the same work:
1. Document the wage differential with payslips
2. File complaint with Labour Inspector / Labour Commissioner
3. File complaint under the Code on Wages, 2019 (which also prohibits gender discrimination in wages)
4. File writ petition in High Court citing Articles 14 and 21 of the Constitution

CODE ON WAGES 2019:
Replaces and consolidates the Equal Remuneration Act, Payment of Wages Act, Minimum Wages Act, and Payment of Bonus Act. Prohibits discrimination in wages on grounds of gender. Applicable to all employees regardless of sector.
